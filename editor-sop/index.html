Editor Standard Operating Procedure

How we edit at Fusion Creative

Everything you need to edit our videos to standard. Read this fully before your first edit, and keep it open while you work. The single most important rule is at the top of the next section.

The golden rules
  • Every brief comes with a reference video. Match its pacing, energy and style. When in doubt, copy the reference.
  • Edit and export in 4K 60fps, 9:16, maximum quality. One export, used for both Reels and TikTok.
  • All text and captions in SF Pro Text Bold with the exact shadow below, kept inside the safe zone.
  • Use sound to make it funnier and more entertaining, never just for the sake of it.
01

Before you start, every video

  • Watch the reference video first, all the way through. It defines the pacing, cut rhythm, energy and overall feel. Your edit should feel like it belongs next to it. Do not start cutting until you have watched it.
  • Know the content type. Funny / skit content is comedy-led with snappy cuts and sound. Promotional / serious content is cleaner and message-led, still dynamic but more polished.
  • Read the brief notes for anything specific to that video.

04

Text & captions

  • Font: SF Pro Text Bold (install SF-Pro-Text-Bold.otf from the Drive folder). Use it for every piece of on-screen text and for captions.
  • Caption everything that is said. When someone talks, it gets captioned, timed tightly to the audio and paced like the reference. Keep lines short and readable.
  • Place text inside the safe zone, never under the right-side buttons or the bottom caption bar.

05

Sound & audio

  • Funny content: use funny audios and sound effects to amplify the comedy. Pull viral sounds from the SAT viral sounds board on Myinstants where they fit.
  • Do not overdo it. Only add a sound when it genuinely makes the moment funnier or more entertaining. If in doubt, leave it out.
  • Promotional content: clean, fitting music and minimal effects, matched to the tone.
  • Levels: dialogue clear and up front, music and effects sitting under it. No clipping or distortion.
06

Annotations & on-screen animation

Used well, these lift retention and land the jokes. Used too much, they get noisy. Study the reference videos for how heavy to go, and copy that level.

  • Punch-ins / zooms on reactions, punchlines and key moments to add energy.
  • Animated captions that pop in with the speech, word by word or line by line, matched to the pace.
  • Emphasis on key words, scale up, change colour, or sync a sound effect to them.
  • Callouts like arrows, circles and highlights to point at exactly what matters.
  • Cutaways and b-roll to illustrate what is being said and keep it visual.
  • Speed ramps for comedic timing, snap into the punchline, hold on the reaction.

Rule of thumb: every effect should earn its place by making the video clearer, funnier or more engaging. If it does not, cut it.

07

Pacing & style

  • Match the reference pacing and cut rhythm. This is the most important creative rule.
  • Hook in the first 1 to 2 seconds. Open on the most interesting frame or line.
  • Funny: comedic timing, hold on reactions, punch in on the beat, no dead air.
  • Promotional: cleaner and clearer, still energetic, message comes through.
  • Cut tight. If a moment is not adding energy, information or comedy, trim it.

09

Delivery

  • Export to spec: 4K 60, 9:16, maximum quality, one .mp4 file.
  • Name the file ClientName_VideoTitle_v1.mp4 (use v2, v3 for revisions).
  • Upload to the shared delivery folder and notify on the agreed channel.
  • Revisions: read the notes carefully, change only what is asked, re-export and re-upload as the next version.
10

Final checklist, before you submit

  • 4K, 60fps, 9:16, maximum quality
  • One export, works for both Reels and TikTok
  • Everything important inside the safe zone
  • All text in SF Pro Text Bold with the correct shadow
  • Every line of speech captioned and tightly timed
  • Sounds make it funnier or better, never distracting
  • Pacing, energy and style match the reference
  • Hook lands in the first 1 to 2 seconds
  • File named correctly and uploaded to the right folder
