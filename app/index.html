/ The Problem

The delivery apps take a third of everything you sell.

Deliveroo and Uber Eats charge around 30 percent commission, up to 35, and once you add the VAT on their fee the real deduction is closer to 36 percent. On a £15 order you keep about £9.75. Do £8,000 a month and you are handing over roughly £2,400, every month, on orders you generated yourself. That is nearly £29,000 a year, gone.

Worse, you never own the customer. The app owns the relationship, the data and the reorder. You cannot bring them back with an offer, because they are the app's customer, not yours.

/ Questions
Do I have to leave Deliveroo and the others?

No. Plenty of venues run both: keep the delivery apps for reach, and push your regulars to order direct on your own app where you keep everything. Every order that moves across is money back in your pocket.

What is actually included for £99 a month?

The whole platform: your branded app, ordering for collection and delivery, live tracking, push notifications, loyalty, and the kitchen and driver screens, plus hosting, support and menu updates. No commission on top, ever.

Is it a real app on the Apple App Store and Google Play?

Yes. We publish your app to both the Apple App Store and Google Play under your own name, so customers can search for you, download it, and keep it on their phone like any other app, with your icon on their home screen. We handle the whole submission and approval process for you.

How does the loyalty card work?

Every customer gets your loyalty card inside the app, and they can add it to Apple Wallet so it lives on their phone like a bank card. They earn points on every order, unlock rewards, and get push offers that bring them back. It gives people a real reason to order through your app instead of a delivery platform.

Do customers really use their own restaurant's app?

Yes, when it is genuinely good. The chains push their own apps hard precisely because direct customers order more often and are far cheaper to keep. The key is a fast, clean app that is easy to order from, which is the part we handle.

Is there a contract?

Yes. The plan runs on a 12-month minimum term at £99 a month, then continues monthly. That covers the build, the app stores, hosting, support and updates, and it still costs less over the year than most venues hand the delivery apps in a single month.

How long does it take to build?

Once we have your menu and branding, we build the whole platform and launch it for you. We will give you a clear timeline on our first call.
