The problem

The delivery-app tax

The apps are brilliant at bringing in new customers — but they charge for every single order, forever, even from regulars who already know you. Here's what each channel takes on a typical delivered order (commission is quoted before VAT, which the platforms add on top).

Move even 40 orders a week off Uber Eats and onto direct, and at roughly £9 saved each that's about £360 a week — on the order of £18,000 a year staying in the business.

Verify #1

Are your app prices lower than your own? If Uber Eats / Just Eat prices sit at or below your dine-in and website prices, you're paying commission on top of a cheaper price — losing twice, and training customers that the app is the best deal. Apps should be your most expensive channel, not your cheapest.

Verify #2

Are you on the right commission tier? You deliver with your own cars — so you should be on the self-delivery tiers (~13% Uber), not the ~30% full-courier tier. If you're on the wrong one, that's an instant saving before we change anything else.

The strategy

Three moves, one goal: make direct the obvious choice

1
Pull

Make direct the best deal

A website-only offer regulars can't get on the apps. Apps stay as the shop window for new faces; your site becomes where loyal customers go to save.

2
Protect

Re-price the apps

Build the commission into your app menu so a delivered app order never loses you money — and so ordering direct is genuinely cheaper for the customer, every time.

3
Own

Own the last mile

Your branded Smart-car fleet means faster, hotter food and no gig-courier lottery. It's a real reason to order direct — and a moving billboard around Whitley Bay.

The website-exclusive offer

Give people a reason to skip the app

The offer has to be strong enough to change a habit, but still cheaper for you than the commission you'd have paid. Three options — we recommend the first.

Recommended

Order Direct & Save

Free delivery + 10% off — every time you order at takdir.co.uk.

  • Free delivery costs you little — you already run the cars
  • 10% is far less than the 14–30% the apps take
  • Simple, permanent, easy to say in an ad
Option B

Free starter or drink

A free popadom & dips, side, or soft drink on every direct order over £25. Feels generous, low real cost, and shows off the kitchen.

Option C

Loyalty — 6th order free

A digital stamp for every direct order; the sixth is on the house. Built to drive repeat direct ordering, not just a one-off.

Seasonal hook

Layer your live “free bottle of beer with every main on England match days” promo on top — direct orders only. It's already built, it's topical, and it points people to the website instead of the app.

The flywheel

Turn app customers into direct regulars

Because you deliver every order yourself, every bag that leaves the kitchen is a chance to win the next order back off the app.

  • QR flyer in every delivery bag — “Ordered on the app? Order direct next time and get free delivery + 10% off.” Scans straight to your site.
  • Receipt & menu inserts — the direct website and phone number on everything that goes out.
  • Collect details at checkout — build an SMS/email list so we can nudge repeat direct orders (Friday curry night, match days, offers).
  • Google Business Profile — make “Order online” point at your site, not just the apps.
