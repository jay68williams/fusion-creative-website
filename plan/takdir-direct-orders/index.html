The problem

The delivery-app tax

Verify 01

Are your app prices lower than your own? If Uber Eats and Just Eat prices sit at or below your dine-in and website prices, you are paying commission on top of a cheaper price. That loses twice, and it trains customers that the app is the best deal. The apps should be your most expensive channel, not your cheapest.

Verify 02

Are you on the right commission tier? You deliver with your own cars, so you should be on the self-delivery tiers (around 13% on Uber), not the 30% full-courier tier. If you are on the wrong one, that is an instant saving before we change anything else.

The maths

A £9 commission is gone. A £9 customer is yours.

This is the most important idea in the plan, so it is worth spelling out. There is a big difference between a cost that vanishes and a cost that pays you back. The numbers below are illustrative, using a round £9 to keep it clear.

Money that leaves

App commission

  • Uber Eats takes about £9 on the order
  • It repeats on every order, forever, even the 50th
  • You get no name, no number, no email
  • You cannot bring them back yourself
12 orders in a year = £108 gone
Money that comes back

Ad spend

  • About £9 to acquire the customer, once
  • You capture their number and email
  • You remarket for free: SMS, email, offers
  • Every repeat order costs about £0 to win
12 orders in a year = £9 total
Your cost to win them falls every time they come back

Spend £9 once to acquire a customer. The more they order, the smaller that one-off cost becomes per order. The app never does this, it charges the full cut every single time.

The words your ad manager will use
CPLCost per lead

What you pay in ads for one person to click through and start an order or enquiry.

CPA / CACCost to acquire a customer

What you pay in ads to turn a stranger into a paying customer. The goal is to keep it below the margin you make back on their orders.

AOVAverage order value

What a typical order is worth. The higher it is, the more each new customer is worth winning.

LTVLifetime value

The total profit a customer brings across every order they ever make, not just the first one.

ROASReturn on ad spend

Pounds back for every pound spent on ads. A 3x ROAS means £3 returned for every £1 in.

RetargetingWarm audiences

Showing ads only to people who already visited your site or ordered. Cheaper, and far more likely to convert.

The flywheel

Turn app customers into direct regulars

Because you deliver every order yourself, every bag that leaves the kitchen is a chance to win the next order back off the app.

  1. QR flyer in every delivery bag. "Ordered on the app? Order direct next time and get free delivery plus 10% off." Scans straight to your site.
  2. Receipt and menu inserts. The direct website and phone number on everything that goes out.
  3. Collect details at checkout. Build an SMS and email list so we can nudge repeat direct orders: Friday curry night, match days, offers.
  4. Google Business Profile. Make "Order online" point at your site, not just the apps.
