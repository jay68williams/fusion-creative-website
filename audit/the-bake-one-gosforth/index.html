Ready-Made Content
5 Video Ideas You Could Film This Week

These are formats working right now for food on TikTok and Instagram, and every one of them plays to what The Bake One already has: the food being made, the platters, and the Lebanese story.

Idea 01
"How our manakish is really made"
How It Is MadeProcessSaves Machine

Start with the dough being stretched by hand. The zaatar, cheese, or meat going on. Into the oven. The bake, the bubble, the colour coming up. Then the cut and the cheese pull. Close-up, satisfying, no talking needed. Just the process and the right audio.

Why it works: "How it is made" content is one of the most reliable formats on TikTok and Reels. People cannot scroll past a process they have never seen up close, and food this hands-on gets saved and sent to friends. This single format could carry the account on its own.
Idea 02
"The platter that feeds the whole table"
Full PlatterRevealShareable

Build a full mixed-grill or mezze platter on camera, then the big overhead reveal as it lands on the table. Every dish, the breads, the dips, the grills, the colour. Slow pan across the spread. End on the first piece being torn off.

Why it works: Big abundant platters are made to be shared. They drive the "we need to go here" comment and the tag-a-mate share, which is exactly how a post reaches new people. It also sells the experience of coming in as a group, which is higher-value footfall.
Idea 03
"Watch this shawarma come off the spit"
ASMRSatisfyingHook

Tight close-up on the shawarma turning on the spit, the char, the carve, the meat falling into the wrap, the garlic and pickles going in, the wrap and the cut. Slow motion on the carve. Let the sound and the visuals do the work.

Why it works: The carve is a perfect three-second hook, the kind of opening that stops the scroll instantly. This style of close-up, satisfying food footage is some of the highest-retention content on the platform, which is exactly what pushes a video into more feeds.
Idea 04
"The Lebanese dessert you have not tried"
DessertCheese PullVisual

A knafeh cut and the cheese pull, or fresh baklava layered and drizzled, or the mhalbeh poured and topped. Pure close-up dessert content. Slow motion on the pull and the pour. Minimal voiceover, maximum visuals.

Why it works: Dessert content drives saves like almost nothing else, and a Lebanese dessert most people have never seen adds curiosity on top. It is the kind of clip that gets bookmarked and shared into group chats, putting The Bake One in front of dessert lovers across the North East.
Idea 05
"A Friday night at The Bake One"
EnergyBehind The ScenesBrand

Fast-cut footage of a busy service. The grill firing, the team working, orders flying out, the breads coming out of the oven, the Gosforth High Street buzz. Quick cuts in time with the audio. The energy of the place in 20 seconds.

Why it works: Energy and behind-the-scenes content makes a place feel alive and worth visiting. It humanises the brand, shows the quality and the volume, and gives people a reason to choose you on a night out. People do not just buy food, they buy the experience.
