Where The Opportunity Is
Five Things Holding You Back

The food is great, the setting is unique, and bloggers who find you are impressed. But there are structural issues and strategic gaps that are keeping KISS invisible to the thousands of potential customers walking past The Gate every day.

1

827 Followers for a City Centre Restaurant Is a Visibility Crisis

The Gate is one of Newcastle's busiest entertainment venues. Thousands of people walk through it daily. KISS is sitting inside one of the highest-footfall locations in the city, and yet only 827 people follow you on Instagram. For context, even small neighbourhood cafes outside the city centre often have more followers than this.

This is not a reflection of the quality of the food or the experience. It is a content and visibility problem. People who find KISS love it. The issue is that almost nobody is finding it online. With the right content strategy and growth tactics, there is no reason KISS should not be at 5,000+ followers within six months.

2

UGC Massively Outperforms Brand Content

Food blogger posts about KISS average 200+ likes. Your own brand posts average 25 likes. That is an 8-10x performance gap. When @comescranwithus__ posted about the sizzling platter, it got 248 likes and 165 comments. Your own posts about the same food struggle to break 30 likes.

This tells us two things. First, the food and the experience are clearly good enough to generate strong engagement. Second, the way the content is being created and presented on the brand account is not connecting with people the same way. A structured influencer programme that brings Newcastle food bloggers in regularly would generate consistent high-performing content while you build the brand account's quality and reach.

3

The Cave-Inspired Interior Is Barely Being Showcased

Your most unique visual asset is the dramatic, cave-inspired interior. It is the single thing that makes KISS instantly different from every other Indian restaurant in Newcastle. The @travel_scientist specifically called it out. And yet, scrolling through your Instagram feed, you would barely know it exists.

This should be the centrepiece of your visual identity online. A proper video walkthrough of the space, atmospheric lighting shots, food being served against the cave walls, cocktails glowing in that setting. Every piece of content should remind people that eating at KISS is a visual experience, not just a meal. Right now, your most shareable asset is going to waste.

4

Video Content Is Almost Non-Existent

Your naan bubbling Reel was your best engagement piece: 57 likes and 7 comments. That is more than double the average engagement on your other posts. The format works. But it is one of the only video posts on the entire account.

The tandoor, the sizzling platters arriving at the table, cocktails being crafted, the dramatic interior lit up at night. All of this is screaming for video content. Instagram and TikTok both prioritise video in their algorithms. Static photos of food in 2026 simply do not get the reach that short-form video does. You have already proven with the naan Reel that video outperforms for KISS. The next step is making it the default format, not the exception.

5

No Paid Social Visible

With TheFork running 30% off deals and match day offers at 15 pounds, you already have compelling offers that would drive clicks and footfall. But there is no paid amplification behind any of it. These offers are only reaching people who already follow you, which is just 827 people.

Even a small, targeted budget promoting match day deals to Newcastle locals within a 5-mile radius, or boosting the bottomless brunch offer to 25-35 year olds in the city, would put KISS in front of thousands of potential customers who have never heard of you. The offers exist. The audience exists. You just need to connect the two with paid spend.

Ready-Made Content
5 Video Ideas You Could Film This Week

These are formats that are working right now for food brands on Instagram and TikTok. All of them play to what makes KISS unique: the interior, the tandoor, the cocktails, and the match day positioning.

Idea 01
"You have never seen an Indian restaurant like this"
Reveal Atmosphere Scroll Stopper

Slow reveal of the cave-inspired interior. Walk through the entrance. The dramatic walls. The lighting. The textures. End on a sizzling platter arriving at a candlelit table. Make people stop and say "where is this?"

Why it works: Interior reveal content stops the scroll because people do not expect it. An Indian restaurant with cave walls inspired by Ajanta and Elora is genuinely surprising. This is the kind of content that gets saved, shared, and commented on with "where is this?!" which is exactly the reaction that drives new followers and footfall.
Idea 02
"15 pounds gets you this on match day at KISS"
Value Reveal Match Day Local

Show the full match day deal: a proper curry, rice or naan, and a pint. Film it being plated, the pint being pulled, someone tucking in. Text overlay with "minutes from St James' Park."

Why it works: Value reveal content performs consistently well because it triggers an immediate emotional reaction: "that is a great deal." Tying it to Newcastle United match days taps into a massive, passionate local audience. This is the kind of content that gets tagged in group chats before every home game.
Idea 03
"Watch our naan bubble in the tandoor"
Food ASMR Process Satisfying

Close-up tandoor footage. The naan going in. The heat. The bubbling. The pull. The butter being brushed on. Pure food ASMR. Your existing naan Reel already proved this works. Double down on it.

Why it works: Your naan Reel was already your best engagement piece at 57 likes and 7 comments, more than double your average. Tandoor content is inherently satisfying: the fire, the technique, the speed. This format has been proven to go viral for Indian restaurants globally. You have the tandoor. You just need to point a camera at it consistently.
Idea 04
"The cocktail they named after Newcastle"
Storytelling Craft Local Pride

The Toon Love Affair being made from scratch. The pour, the shake, the strain, the garnish. Tell the story of why they named it that. End with someone taking the first sip in front of the cave walls.

Why it works: Cocktail-making content is inherently cinematic and satisfying to watch. Adding the Newcastle angle with the Toon Love Affair name gives it local relevance and shareability. People tag their friends in content that feels like it belongs to their city. This turns a cocktail into a story worth sharing.
Idea 05
"Food bloggers keep calling us a hidden gem"
Social Proof Trending Compilation

Stitch or reference the blogger reviews. "Found a gem in Newcastle." "Really impressed." "Amazing food." Cut between the quotes and the actual food. End with "maybe it is time you found us too."

Why it works: Social proof is the most persuasive form of marketing. When multiple independent voices all say the same thing, it creates trust and curiosity. The "hidden gem" angle works perfectly for KISS because it makes people feel like they are discovering something before everyone else. That exclusivity drives action.
Digital Enhancements
Beyond Social Media

These are strategic changes that would make a real difference alongside the social media work.

Leverage The Gate Footfall

Thousands of people walk through The Gate daily. That is a massive captive audience that most restaurants in The Gate are not capturing online. QR codes on signage and menus linking to your Instagram, location-tagged posts and Stories that appear when people search The Gate, and geo-targeted content would all help capture some of that passing traffic.

Quick win: Every post should be location-tagged to The Gate, Newcastle. When people search for food near The Gate on Instagram, KISS should appear. Right now, that is not happening consistently.

Build a Match Day Content Series

Newcastle United play regularly at St James' Park, which is a short walk from The Gate. Every home match is a content opportunity. Pre-match energy, the 15 pound deal being served, crowds heading past, post-match celebrations.

  • Pre-match teaser: "Match day at KISS" Story or Reel posted morning of the game
  • The deal in action: film the curry, the naan, the pint being served to match day customers
  • Post-match atmosphere: the buzz in the restaurant after a win
  • Recurring series: make it a recognisable format that fans look forward to every home game

Over a full season, that is 19+ home matches worth of content. Each one reaching Newcastle United fans who are already in the area looking for somewhere to eat.

Get the Interior on Camera Properly

Commission a proper video walkthrough of the cave-inspired space. This is the single most shareable asset KISS has and it is barely being used. A cinematic 30-60 second walkthrough showing the textures, the lighting, the atmosphere would serve as anchor content that can be pinned, reused, and repurposed across every platform.

Every restaurant in Newcastle has food photos. Only KISS has cave walls. Lead with what makes you different.
