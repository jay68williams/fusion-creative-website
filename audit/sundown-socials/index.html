The main bit
6 Ideas You Could Try

These are formats doing really well on Reels and TikTok right now, all built around stuff you already do. You could film every one of them on your phone at your next event, no kit, no faff. The big shift across all of them: lead with a real face and a hook in the first second, because that's what gets a reel pushed out to new people instead of just your existing followers.

Idea 01
"POV: you just moved to Newcastle and don't know a soul"
RelatablePOVYour #1 growth format

Open on that lonely scrolling-at-home feeling, then cut hard to the warmth of a Sundown social: the laughing, the new faces, the hugs at the end. Big text hook on the first frame, trending audio, under 20 seconds.

Why it works: it speaks straight to the exact woman you want, and the lonely-to-belonging arc is some of the most shared content going. This is the one I'd start with.
Idea 02
The event aftermovie: "tell me you wouldn't have loved this"
RecapFOMOSells the next ticket

Fast, punchy cuts from your latest event set to the right audio, ending on "next one's [date], link in bio". Post it while the feeling's still fresh. 15 to 20 seconds.

Why it works: watching everyone else have the best night is the single best way to sell the next ticket. Every event quietly markets the one after it.
Idea 03
"Why I started a women's community in the North East"
FounderStoryConnection

Just you, talking honestly to camera: the why behind Sundown Socials, the gap you saw, the kind of woman you wanted to help. No script. Warm event clips underneath.

Why it works: people back people. Your story turns a casual viewer into someone who genuinely wants to be part of what you're building.
Idea 04
"I came on my own and left with five new friends"
Member voiceReassurance

A member talking, real and unpolished, about turning up nervous and alone and how it actually went. Cut with her laughing with the group. Tag the local area.

Why it works: the biggest reason women don't book is "what if it's awkward". A real member answering that fear removes the last reason not to come.
Idea 05
"Get ready with me for tonight's Sundown social"
GRWMAnticipation

The classic getting-ready format, but the chat is all about where you're off to and who you'll meet. Builds the excitement before the event and pulls people into the vibe.

Why it works: GRWM is one of the most-watched formats on the app, and it lets you sell the night out without it feeling like an advert.
Idea 06
"3 things to know before your first Sundown event"
HelpfulSavesLowers the nerves

Quick, friendly to-camera: what to wear, that loads of people come solo, how easy it is to join in. Answers the worries that stop someone hitting book.

Why it works: helpful reels get saved and shared, which the algorithm loves, and it gently nudges the nervous-but-curious over the line.

A couple of quick wins on top: aim for more reels than feed posts (right now it's about 1 in 4, flip that), and try to reply to every comment with a question to get a proper conversation going. The more comments and replies a post gets early, the further Instagram pushes it. And whenever you can, get a real face in the first frame rather than a graphic. Faces stop the scroll, calendars don't.
