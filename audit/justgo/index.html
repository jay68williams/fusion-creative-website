Where The Opportunity Is
Five Things Holding You Back

The product is clearly strong. Customers who find you and book tend to have a great experience. The issue is that your online presence is not reflecting the quality of what you actually offer. Here is where the biggest gaps are.

1

TikTok Is Completely Untouched

6 followers. Zero videos. Ever. For a travel and adventure brand, this is the single biggest missed opportunity we have seen in an audit.

#vanlife has billions of views on TikTok. #motorhome, #roadtrip, #uktravel, #nc500 are all massive. Your customers are literally driving scenic routes through the Scottish Highlands, the Lake District, Cornwall, and the Wild Atlantic Way. Every single one of those 12,000 annual hires is a piece of content waiting to happen. You are sitting on 12,000 stories per year and capturing none of them.

The travel and adventure space on TikTok is one of the fastest growing categories on the platform. Your competitors are not there either. The first motorhome hire brand to properly show up on TikTok in the UK wins.

2

Instagram Engagement Is Flatlined

0.04% engagement rate. To put that in context, the industry benchmark for an account your size is 2-5%. You are getting 2-4 likes on posts to an audience of nearly 6,000 followers. That means your content is reaching almost nobody, even among people who already follow you.

The cause is clear. Every post is a branded graphic template with a sales pitch caption ending in a phone number. No video. No user generated content. No storytelling. No customer trips. No destination content. It is the same format every time, and the algorithm has stopped showing it to people because nobody engages with it.

With 5,800+ followers you should be getting 120-290 likes per post. That is 50-100x what you are getting right now. The audience is there. The content just needs to change.

3

Your Entire Website Is Invisible to Google

This is the big one. Every single page on justgo.uk.com has a robots noindex, nofollow tag. That is a technical instruction telling Google "do not show any of our pages in search results." It means your entire website, all 71+ blog posts, all your vehicle pages, all your location pages, all your promotions, none of it appears in Google.

Someone searching "motorhome hire UK" or "motorhome hire Scotland" or "campervan hire Edinburgh" will never find you through Google. You are getting zero organic search traffic. For a company that has invested in 71 articles worth of content, this is catastrophic. All that work is invisible.

This is most likely a migration error from when the site moved to the Indie Campers platform. It is a single technical fix that would start bringing organic traffic within weeks. But right now, it is costing you thousands of potential visitors every month.

4

Facebook Reputation Is Damaged

50% recommendation rate on Facebook from 38 reviews. Half of the people who have reviewed you on Facebook would not recommend you. That number is visible to every single potential customer who visits your Facebook page.

Combined with only 1-8 likes per post and identical cross-posted content from Instagram, your Facebook page is actively working against you. When someone is considering spending hundreds of pounds on a motorhome hire and they check your Facebook, a 50% recommendation rate plants doubt. This needs a proper review response strategy and a plan to encourage your happy customers (and you clearly have thousands of them based on Trustpilot and Google) to leave Facebook reviews too.

5

Zero Paid Advertising

We checked the Facebook Ad Library. No ads running. No Facebook ads, no Instagram ads, no TikTok ads. Nothing.

You are a premium motorhome hire business with prices in the hundreds per night, operating in a seasonal industry that needs to fill fleet capacity during peak periods like summer, bank holidays, and festival season. Paid social should be a core part of your marketing mix. Targeted ads for summer bookings, festival packages, bank holiday getaways, and last minute deals would drive direct revenue. Right now you are leaving money on the table.

Ready-Made Content
5 Video Ideas You Could Film Today

These are formats that are working right now in the travel and adventure space. All of them use content your team and customers are already creating, you just need to capture it.

Idea 01
"POV: Your first night in a motorhome on the Scottish Highlands"
POV Travel Scotland

Film from inside the motorhome at sunset. Show dinner being cooked with a view out the window. The bed setup. The stars outside. Let the scenery do the talking. No voiceover needed, just ambient sound and maybe a trending audio track.

Why it works: POV travel content consistently goes viral on TikTok. The Scottish Highlands are among the most searched UK destinations. This format lets people imagine themselves there.
Idea 02
"We gave a family the keys to a 6-berth motorhome for a week"
Family Travel Transformation Storytelling

Film the handover moment at the depot. Capture the kids' reaction when they see the motorhome. Get clips from their trip over the week. End with them returning it with a tan and a grin. Before and after energy.

Why it works: Transformation and reaction content drives engagement. Families are a huge untapped audience for motorhome content. The emotional arc of excitement to adventure to happy memories is compelling.
Idea 03
"5 things I wish I knew before hiring a motorhome"
Tips Educational Hook

Quick-cut talking head with practical tips. Where to park overnight. How the toilet actually works. What to pack. What the driving is really like. Address the fears and questions that stop people from booking.

Why it works: Educational content builds trust and removes objections. This format works across TikTok, Reels, and Shorts. It positions you as the expert and answers the exact questions holding people back from booking.
Idea 04
"I drove the NC500 in a motorhome and this happened"
Road Trip Scotland NC500

Cinematic montage of the North Coast 500 route. Drone shots of the coastline. POV driving footage through mountain passes. Wild camping spots at golden hour. Show why this route is on everyone's bucket list.

Why it works: NC500 content gets millions of views. Combine it with the motorhome lifestyle angle and you have a format that sells the dream and the product at the same time, without ever feeling like an ad.
Idea 05
"Motorhome vs hotel for a Lake District weekend - honest comparison"
Comparison Travel Budget

Compare the cost, freedom, experience, and convenience of hiring a motorhome versus booking a hotel in the Lakes. Use split-screen, real prices, real locations. Be genuinely honest about both sides. Show the trade-offs and let people decide.

Why it works: Comparison content performs because it triggers opinions and debate in the comments. It also directly addresses the consideration stage of someone thinking about booking for the first time.
Digital Enhancements
Beyond Social Media

These are technical fixes and strategic changes that would make a real difference alongside the social media work.

SEO Fix (Critical)

This is the single most impactful thing you could do right now. Remove the noindex/nofollow meta tag from every page on justgo.uk.com. This one change would allow Google to start indexing your 71+ blog posts, vehicle pages, location pages, and promotions.

Within weeks you would start appearing in search results for terms like "motorhome hire UK," "campervan hire Scotland," "motorhome rental Edinburgh," and hundreds of long-tail keywords your blog content already targets. All of that content you have invested in creating would finally start working for you.

This is likely a migration error from the move to the Indie Campers platform. It is a single technical fix, but every day it stays in place you are losing potential organic traffic. This should be your number one priority.

Schema Markup

Adding structured data to your website would help Google understand your content better and display richer search results.

  • FAQ schema on your homepage FAQ section so answers appear directly in Google
  • LocalBusiness schema for each depot (London, Edinburgh, Belfast, Dublin) to boost local search
  • Product schema for each vehicle type so pricing and availability show in search results

Review Strategy

You have 1,071 Trustpilot reviews and 261 Google reviews. That is great. But your Facebook rating is dragging down your overall reputation with a 50% recommendation rate.

Implementing a post-hire email sequence that encourages happy customers to leave reviews on Trustpilot, Google, AND Facebook would dilute that poor Facebook score over time. With 12,000 hires per year, even a small conversion rate on review requests would transform your Facebook rating within a few months.
