Ready-Made Content
5 Video Ideas You Could Film This Week

These are formats working right now for food on TikTok and Instagram, and every one of them plays to what The Seaton Tandoori already has: the tandoor, the grills, the curries, and the seafront story.

Idea 01
"Naan straight out of the tandoor"
How It Is MadeProcessSaves Machine

Dough slapped onto the side of the clay oven, puffing and blistering, then pulled out, brushed with butter or garlic, and torn open in close-up. Satisfying, no talking needed. Just the process and the right audio.

Why it works: "How it is made" content is one of the most reliable formats on TikTok and Reels. People cannot scroll past a process they have never seen up close, and the tandoor is pure theatre. This one format could carry the account on its own.
Idea 02
"The mixed grill that lands sizzling"
Full PlatterRevealShareable

Build a full charcoal mixed grill, then the big overhead reveal as the sizzling platter hits the table with the smoke still rising. Slow pan across the spread, the breads, the dips. End on the first piece being lifted.

Why it works: Big abundant platters are made to be shared. They drive the "we need to go here" comment and the tag-a-mate share, which is exactly how a post reaches new people. It also sells the group night out, which is higher-value footfall.
Idea 03
"Watch this curry come together"
ASMRSatisfyingHook

Tight close-up on a curry being finished in the pan. The sizzle of the tempering, the pour, the bubble, the fold of cream, the final garnish. Slow motion on the pour. Let the sound and the visuals do the work.

Why it works: The finish of a proper curry is a perfect three-second hook, the kind of opening that stops the scroll instantly. This style of close-up, high-retention food footage is exactly what pushes a video into more feeds.
Idea 04
"The dish people keep coming back for"
SignatureBuildVisual

Build a signature on camera, the Mita Thali or the chicken tikka masala wrap, layer by layer, finishing on the reveal. Close-up, colourful, with the sauce going on and the wrap or thali coming together.

Why it works: Signature-dish content turns your most-loved item into a reason to visit. It is the clip people send to a mate with "this is the one," putting The Seaton Tandoori in front of new local mouths every time it is shared.
Idea 05
"12 years on the Seaton Carew seafront"
StoryBrandEnergy

Fast-cut footage of a busy service, the tandoor firing, the team working, orders going out, mixed in with the seafront setting and a nod to the new look. The energy and the story of the place in 20 seconds.

Why it works: Energy and story content makes a place feel alive and worth visiting. The seafront and the 12-year, award-winning heritage are a brand story almost no competitor can match, and it gives people a reason to choose you.

Quick Wins
Things Worth Doing Either Way

A few changes that would make a difference alongside the content, whether you work with us or not.

Start a TikTok This Week

There is no active TikTok for The Seaton Tandoori, and it is the biggest open goal in this whole report. With the food you serve, a brand-new account can reach a lot of local people fast.

Quick win: Set up the account, film the naan coming out of the tandoor, and post it. That one video could be the start of something big.

Get the Posting Off Your Plate

When posting sits with one person around a busy service, it is always one more job to do, so it slips. That is just how it goes, and the honest fix is that it should not be your job at all. Consistent posting, by people whose only job is content, is where the growth comes from, and it frees you up to run the restaurant.

Build the Google and TripAdvisor Reviews

You are already at 4.3 on Google, which is a strong base. A simple system would push it higher and grow the count, and reviews are still one of the biggest deciders for new customers:

  • QR code on the table and at the counter linking straight to your Google review page
  • A gentle prompt from staff to happy customers, and a follow-up on delivery and app orders
  • Target: a steadily climbing review count across Google and TripAdvisor over the next few months
