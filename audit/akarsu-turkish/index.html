Where The Opportunity Is
Five Areas With Real Potential

Akarsu has an incredible product, a stunning location, and nearly a decade of reputation. These are the five areas where focused effort would turn a well-loved local restaurant into a destination people drive to from across the region.

1

Instagram Is Massively Underperforming for a Restaurant This Good

1,350 followers after nearly ten years and 270 posts. For a restaurant ranked #9 in Durham with 810+ reviews and a Travellers' Choice award, that number should be ten times higher. The gap between how good Akarsu is and how many people know about it online is the single biggest growth opportunity available to you right now.

Turkish food is one of the most visually satisfying cuisines on social media. Charcoal-grilled kebabs, sizzling Adana, halloumi fries, mezze platters, the cheese pull on a pide. This is the content that stops people scrolling. The food is already there. It just needs to be filmed properly and consistently, with the kind of hooks and formats that the algorithm rewards.

2

TikTok Does Not Exist Yet

Akarsu has no TikTok account, but food bloggers and visitors are already posting about you on the platform. Creators like @fatbottomfoodie and @beccastravel have filmed content at Akarsu that reached thousands of people. That is organic proof that your food performs on TikTok. The problem is you are not capturing any of that audience because you do not have an account for them to follow.

Turkish food content is one of the best performing niches on TikTok globally. Kebab carving, coal grill sizzle, mezze spreads, the theatre of a mixed grill being assembled. People watch these videos on repeat. The first Turkish restaurant in Durham to claim TikTok owns that space. It should be Akarsu.

3

The Riverside Setting Is Not Being Used in Content

You are sitting in a Grade II listed building with views of Durham Cathedral, the Castle, and the River Wear. That is one of the most photogenic restaurant settings in the entire North East. But scrolling through the Instagram, the content does not consistently showcase that backdrop. Most posts are close-up food shots that could have been taken anywhere.

The location is a differentiator. A reel that opens with a wide shot of the Cathedral, pulls inside to the restaurant, then lands on a sizzling mixed grill tells a story that no competitor can replicate. "Turkish food with this view" is the kind of hook that makes people save a post and plan a visit. Every piece of content should remind people where they are eating.

4

No Content Consistency or Series Format

270 posts over nearly ten years works out at roughly one post every two weeks. The algorithm on both Instagram and TikTok rewards consistency above almost everything else. Three to four posts per week is the minimum for growth. One post every two weeks means the algorithm has already forgotten you exist by the time the next one lands.

Beyond frequency, there is no recognisable series or recurring format. The best-performing restaurant accounts all have signature content: a weekly special reveal, a "dish of the week" close-up, a behind-the-grill POV, a customer reaction series. These formats build habit and give followers a reason to come back. Right now the content feels sporadic rather than strategic.

5

The Tourist and Student Market Is Untapped Online

Durham has two massive audiences that actively search for restaurants online: tourists visiting the Cathedral and Castle, and 20,000+ Durham University students. Both groups default to social media and Google when deciding where to eat. You are already winning the tourist crowd on TripAdvisor, but you are invisible to anyone who searches on Instagram or TikTok first.

Students especially discover restaurants through social media, not review sites. A strong Instagram and TikTok presence would open up an entire market segment that currently does not know Akarsu exists. A student lunch deal promoted through a viral reel could fill your tables every weekday afternoon.

Ready-Made Content
4 Video Ideas You Could Film This Week

These are formats working right now on TikTok and Instagram Reels, tailored specifically to a Turkish restaurant with a charcoal grill and a riverside setting. Film any one of these properly and it could be the video that puts Akarsu in front of hundreds of thousands of new people.

Idea 01
"Adana kebab on the charcoal grill, start to finish"
Process Satisfying ASMR

Close-up on the raw mince being shaped onto the skewer by hand. Place it on the charcoal grill. Sizzle. Flames lick up. Turn it. The char marks form. Slide it off onto the plate. Add the garnish, the flatbread, the grilled peppers. Hero shot of the finished plate with the river in soft focus through the window behind it. Trending audio or pure grill ASMR. Under 20 seconds.

Why it works: Charcoal grill content is one of the most reliably viral formats in food TikTok. The fire, the sizzle, the handmade process. People cannot scroll past it. The river view in the background is the detail that makes this uniquely Akarsu and not just any kebab shop. This is both a brand film and a conversion tool in one.
Idea 02
"POV: dinner overlooking Durham Cathedral at the only Turkish restaurant in the city"
POV Location FOMO

First person camera. Walk down Silver Street. Step inside Akarsu. Pan to the window showing the Cathedral lit up across the river. Sit down. Mezze arrives. Then the mixed grill. Camera slowly pans across the table. Cut to the view. Text overlay: "Turkish food with this view. Durham, England." Trending cinematic audio.

Why it works: POV restaurant videos are the highest-converting format for hospitality. The Cathedral view is the hook that makes this shareable beyond Durham. People tag friends, save it for "next time we visit Durham," and plan a trip around it. No other restaurant in Durham can film this exact video. That exclusivity is what makes it go viral.
Idea 03
"The Akarsu mixed grill: everything you get for under 30 quid"
Value Portion Reveal Close-Up

Overhead shot. Empty table. Dishes start arriving one by one. Bread and dips. Mezze. Then the mixed grill lands. Camera slowly zooms out to show the full spread. Text overlay counting up the items: "Adana. Lamb shish. Chicken shish. Lamb chops. Rice. Salad. Bread. Grilled veg. All for under 30 quid." End on the hero shot. Trending audio.

Why it works: Value-reveal content drives massive engagement because viewers tag friends and say "we need to go here." The portion size and price point are the hook. For Durham students especially, seeing this kind of spread at this price point is the video that gets them through the door for the first time.
Idea 04
"10 years of Akarsu: how Cengiz built Durham's only Turkish restaurant"
Origin Story Founder Personality

You, Cengiz, talking to camera in the restaurant. Why Turkish food? Why Durham? What was the first day like? What does "Akarsu" mean? Cut between your words and shots of the kitchen, the grill, the view, regulars laughing, plates going out. Keep it raw and honest. 45-60 seconds. Text overlay: "Our story." Emotional trending audio underneath.

Why it works: People follow people, not restaurants. A founder story humanises the brand and creates an emotional connection. Once your audience knows your name, your family, and why you opened this restaurant, they are not just booking a table. They are supporting Cengiz and the story behind Akarsu. That is the difference between a customer and a fan who comes back every month.
Digital Enhancements
Beyond Social Media

These are strategic changes that would make a real difference alongside the content work.

Launch TikTok and Claim the "Turkish Food Durham" Space

Food bloggers are already posting about you on TikTok but you have no account to capture that audience. Setting up @akarsugrill on TikTok and posting three to four videos per week would put you in front of tens of thousands of people who have never searched TripAdvisor but eat out in Durham every week.

  • Create TikTok account matching the Instagram handle (@akarsugrill)
  • Post 3-4 short videos per week focused on the grill, the food, and the view
  • Engage with food creators who have already posted about Akarsu
  • Use location tags and trending audio to reach Durham and North East audiences

Quick win: The food bloggers who have already filmed at Akarsu have proven the content works. You just need your own account to capture the audience they are building for you.

Fix Instagram Posting Frequency

One post every two weeks is not enough for the algorithm to show your content to anyone. Moving to three to four posts per week, with a mix of Reels, carousel posts, and Stories, would transform your reach within 60 days. The content is already there in the kitchen and the dining room every single night. It just needs to be captured consistently.

  • Post 3-4 times per week minimum (Reels, carousels, Stories)
  • Create recurring series: "Dish of the Week," "Behind the Grill," "Table with a View"
  • Use Instagram Stories daily to show the kitchen, the prep, the view, the atmosphere
  • Cross-post TikTok content as Reels to maximise reach from every video

Target the Student Market

20,000+ Durham University students eat out regularly but discover restaurants through social media, not TripAdvisor. A student lunch deal promoted through a viral reel or a Durham student food account collab could open up a market segment that currently does not know Akarsu exists.

The opportunity: Students share content with each other constantly. One viral video showing a generous lunch deal at Akarsu could fill your tables Monday to Friday during term time. The lifetime value of a student who discovers you in first year and keeps coming back for three years is enormous.
