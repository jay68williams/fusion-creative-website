Where The Opportunity Is
Five Things We Would Focus On First

You have the shop, the product and the two revenue streams. The job now is reach, getting Prime Meats in front of the people in Blyth and across the North East who do not know you exist yet. These are the five areas we would start with.

1

Start Filming the Craft

The single biggest opportunity is turning what already happens behind your counter into short-form video. The knife work, a cut being trimmed and tied, a tray of steaks laid out, the fresh seafood on ice. This is the exact content that racks up views for butchers all over the world, and right now none of it is being captured. One good video of a cut being broken down has more reach potential than a hundred photos.

You do not need a studio or a script. The raw material is on your block every morning. We film it, edit it to travel, and post it.

2

Get on TikTok

There is no Prime Meats TikTok yet, and for a butcher that is the biggest single gap of all. TikTok is where local food businesses blow up, because the algorithm pushes content to people nearby regardless of how many followers you have. Butchery content in particular does huge numbers on there. Some of the biggest food accounts in the world are butchers, and they built it all on TikTok.

You do not need to learn the platform or be a natural on camera. We set the account up, film, and run it for you.

3

Teach People Why Your Meat Is Better

The content that builds trust for a butcher is education. Why a properly aged steak beats a supermarket tray, how to spot good marbling, which cut to use for a Sunday roast, the best way to cook a piece so it is never dry. People save and share this content, and it positions you as the expert, which is exactly why they choose the local butcher over the big shop. You know all of this already. We just get it on camera.

This is the difference between being a shop that sells meat and being the butcher people trust and follow.

4

Put the Catering and Trade Side On Camera

Your supply to restaurants and businesses is a whole second story most butchers never tell. A big trade order being prepped, a restaurant collection going out, the scale of what you produce for venues across the North East. Content like that does two jobs at once: it makes retail customers trust you more ("if the restaurants use them, they must be good") and it quietly puts you in front of other chefs and venues who could become trade clients.

Nobody in your area is showing this. It is a genuinely open lane.

5

Build the Brand From the First Video

Because you are brand new, every early video is doing double duty: getting reach and defining who Prime Meats is. A consistent look, a recognisable face behind the counter, the same energy every week. That is how a new shop becomes "the Blyth butcher everyone talks about" rather than just another name on the high street. Starting now, with a clean account and a clear identity, means we build that reputation deliberately instead of by accident.

Consistency is the thing most businesses cannot keep up. That is the part we take off your plate entirely.

Proof From The Niche
What The Biggest Butchers In The World Are Doing

You asked for a look at what is working for other butchers, so here it is. Butchery is one of the most quietly viral niches on social media, and the accounts winning at it are not doing anything you could not do. They are filming the craft, teaching people, and staying consistent. Here is the proof.

The Bearded Butchers, a small US butchery, built an audience of millions

Two brothers from a family butcher shop in Ohio started filming themselves breaking down cuts and explaining the craft. They now have around 776,000 followers on Instagram, 1.8 million on TikTok, and over 3.6 million subscribers on YouTube. One of their videos alone reached more than 127 million views across platforms. No fancy production, just a butcher, a block, and a camera showing the craft and teaching people. This is the clearest proof that butchery content travels.

The UK's best butchers now sell through content

Premium British butchers like HG Walter, Thomas Joseph Butchery and Turner & George have built serious reputations on the back of close-up cut footage, dry-aging content and cooking guidance. The formula is the same everywhere: show the quality up close, explain why it matters, make people hungry. It works because meat done well is genuinely beautiful on camera.

The content formula that keeps winning for butchers

Across all of them, four formats do the heavy lifting, and every one of them is something you already do behind the counter every day:

  • The craft: knife work, a cut being broken down, tying a joint, filleting fish. Satisfying to watch, endlessly shareable.
  • The education: which cut for which dish, how to cook it right, why quality meat is worth it. The stuff people save and come back to.
  • The finished result: a cooked steak, a loaded BBQ, a Sunday roast. Makes people hungry, drives them to the shop.
  • The personality: the butcher behind the counter, the banter, the local character. This is what turns viewers into loyal regulars.

The takeaway: none of these butchers had an advantage you do not have. They had a block, a camera and consistency. The reason they have millions of followers and most butchers have none is simply that they started filming and kept going. That is the entire game, and it is exactly the part we handle for you.

Ready-Made Content
6 Video Ideas We Could Film In One Visit

These are formats working right now for butchers on TikTok and Instagram Reels, built around exactly what you already do. Every one of these could be filmed in a single morning behind your counter.

Idea 01
"This is why our meat is better"
EducationTrustUSP

You, holding one of your steaks next to a standard supermarket one, talking through the difference: the marbling, the colour, the aging, where it comes from. Honest, straight to camera, no fluff. Strong hook on frame one ("Here is why our steak costs more, and why it is worth every penny").

Why it works: It answers the exact question every customer has and turns your price into a selling point instead of a barrier. Education content builds trust faster than anything, and trust is why people choose a local butcher.
Idea 02
"The best way to cook the perfect steak"
How-toSave-baitCooking

Pick a cut, then show the exact method start to finish: how to season it, the pan or the fire, the timing, the rest. Under 30 seconds, clean and confident. Works for steak, then a whole series: lamb, pork belly, the perfect roast, a Sunday joint, how to cook fish.

Why it works: "How to cook it perfectly" is one of the most saved and re-watched formats for any food business. People save it to use later, which means they come back to you for the meat when they do.
Idea 03
"Watch this get broken down"
The craftSatisfyingSkill

A clean, close-up film of you breaking down a larger cut into portions, or tying a joint, or filleting a fish. No talking needed, just the sound of the knife and a tight edit. The kind of skill footage people watch three times over.

Why it works: This is the exact format that built the biggest butcher accounts in the world. Skilled knife work is mesmerising, endlessly shareable, and it shows off craftsmanship no supermarket can match.
Idea 04
"What goes into a restaurant order"
TradeBehind the scenesCredibility

A behind-the-scenes look at prepping a big catering or restaurant order: the scale, the trays, the quality control, the collection or delivery going out. Lean into the fact that professional kitchens across the North East trust you to supply them.

Why it works: "The restaurants buy from them" is powerful social proof for retail customers, and it quietly markets your trade side to other chefs and venues at the same time. Two audiences, one video.
Idea 05
"Meet your Blyth butcher"
PersonalityLocalBrand

A short, warm intro to you and the shop: who you are, why you opened Prime Meats, where your meat comes from, what you care about. The local, family, quality story told in your own words. This is the video that turns a new shop into a name people root for.

Why it works: People buy from people, especially locally. Putting a face and a story to the shop builds the kind of loyalty a supermarket can never compete with, and it makes every future video feel personal.
Idea 06
"Your weekend BBQ, sorted"
SeasonalShoppableRange

A fast, mouth-watering showcase of a themed box or spread: a BBQ pack, a Sunday roast bundle, a seafood platter, whatever suits the season. Build the tray, show the price, end with "come grab one this weekend" or "order for local delivery". Pure appetite plus a clear call to action.

Why it works: It shows the range, creates appetite, and drives a direct action. Seasonal and event-led content (BBQ weather, Christmas, a big match) gives people a reason to buy right now, not someday.
Digital Enhancements
Beyond the Feed

A few extra wins that would compound the content work and turn local visibility into actual footfall, orders and trade enquiries.

Google Business Profile

When someone in the area searches "butcher near me" or "butcher in Blyth", Google decides who shows up. For a brand new shop, a complete and active Google Business Profile is one of the highest-return things you can set up, and it is free. It is often the very first way a new local customer finds you.

  • Claim and fully complete the profile with your hours, address and photos
  • Add fresh photos of your cuts and counter regularly (Google rewards it)
  • Ask happy customers for a Google review at the counter to build up your rating
  • Turn your best reviews into shareable social content

A Website Built to Convert and Take Orders

Your site is a solid start. The next step is making sure someone who finds you through a video lands on a page that shows off the cuts, makes ordering or delivery dead simple, and has a clear line for catering and trade enquiries. As you grow, online ordering or click-and-collect for meat boxes is a natural add.

  • Strong food-first imagery and a clear "order local delivery" path
  • A dedicated catering and trade enquiry section for restaurants and businesses
  • Every social bio linked straight to it, so content turns into orders
