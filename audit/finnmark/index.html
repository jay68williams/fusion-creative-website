The one thing I would focus on

Here is the gap, and it is the same one the job ad describes so well. The reach does not yet match the product. Around 7,000 on Instagram, a TikTok that is barely started, most reels landing in the low thousands. The content is built to be admired, beautifully written and shot, when the formats that travel are built to stop a thumb: a hook in the first second, a face, a trend, a sound. That is wide open ground, and for a category this cinematic it is a big one.

A few ideas I would love to try

Hooks over captions.The writing is beautiful, but it lives under the fold. Lead with the steam, the reveal, the löyly pour, the first second has to earn the next thirty. Let the craft do the talking once you have the scroll.
Put a face to it.Right now it is all product, no person. A warm, watchable host walking us through a build, a customer's garden, a trade show in Finland. People follow people, and the job ad is right to want an on-camera voice.
The löyly money-shot, and the sound.Water hits the stones, the steam wave rises, sound up, slight slow-mo. Sauna ASMR, the ladle, the crackle of the HUUM wood burner, the birch whisk, is a genre in itself. You are sitting on the most sensory content in the category.
Two modes: cinematic and fast.Keep the high-production build films, but run trend-led, green-screen, run-and-gun pieces alongside them. "Things people get wrong about saunas", cold-plunge reactions, "POV: your garden sauna is ready." Volume and reach, not just beauty.
Own TikTok and Shorts.133 followers on TikTok is not a problem, it is the opportunity. The same builds and reveals, cut for TikTok and Shorts with trending audio, are exactly what the Saltwater Sauna deserves to be seen by.
Customer transformation films.Empty garden to finished sauna, and the customer's face on the first session. Your 35 to 65 home-and-garden audience saves these, sends them, and books off the back of them.
