Where The Opportunity Is
Three Areas With Huge Potential

The brand is visually stunning, the expansion is impressive, and the concept clearly works. These are the areas where a focused digital effort could make a significant difference, especially for the new Jesmond branch.

1

The Jesmond Branch Has Almost No Digital Footprint Yet

This is both the biggest challenge and the biggest opportunity. The Jesmond Instagram account (@flowercafejesmond) is brand new with very few followers. When someone in Jesmond searches for brunch spots, cocktail bars, or cafes on Osborne Road, Flower Cafe Jesmond is not showing up in the way it needs to. The brand recognition from the main Flower Cafe accounts helps, but the Jesmond-specific discovery is almost non-existent.

A new location launch is the perfect moment for a content blitz. Showcase the space, the menu, the cocktails, the interiors. Make it feel like an event. Create a sense of "this just opened and you need to see it." Right now, the digital presence does not match the ambition of the physical location, and that means potential customers on Osborne Road are walking past without knowing what is inside.

2

TripAdvisor 3.4 Is Well Below Where the Brand Should Be

A 3.4 on TripAdvisor for a brand with this level of visual appeal and press coverage is a gap that needs addressing. TripAdvisor is still one of the first places people check when deciding where to eat, especially visitors to Newcastle and people looking for special occasion venues like afternoon tea.

The good news is that TripAdvisor ratings are very responsive to a focused review generation effort. A systematic approach of asking happy customers to leave reviews, combined with professional responses to all existing reviews (positive and negative), could shift that number significantly within a few months. The Jesmond branch has a fresh start and can build its TripAdvisor presence from day one with a strong rating.

3

No Dedicated Website for Bookings, Menus, or Information

When someone Googles "Flower Cafe Jesmond" or "brunch Jesmond," there is no website to land on. No menu to browse, no booking link to click, no information about the space, the cocktails, or the afternoon tea offering. All of that traffic, all of those curious potential customers, have nowhere to go except social media or third-party listings that you do not control.

A clean, beautiful website that matches the visual standard of the physical space would convert browsers into bookings. It does not need to be complex. It needs a menu, a booking widget, a gallery of the interiors and food, location details, and links to your socials. For a brand this visual, the website is a natural extension of the experience.

Ready-Made Content
5 Video Ideas You Could Film Today

These are formats that are working right now for visually-led hospitality brands on TikTok and Instagram. All of them play to Flower Cafe's greatest strength: being one of the most beautiful spaces in Newcastle.

Idea 01
"POV: You just walked into the most Instagrammable cafe in Jesmond"
Interior Reveal POV Viral Potential

First-person walk-through. Push through the door. The camera pans up to reveal the floral ceiling, the pink interiors, the lush greenery. Slow, cinematic shots of every beautiful corner. No voiceover. Just a dreamy audio track and the space doing all the work. End with the address and a "book now" prompt.

Why it works: Interior reveal videos are one of the most reliably viral formats for visually stunning venues. People save them for future visits, share them with friends as "we need to go here," and tag their location. For a space as beautiful as Flower Cafe Jesmond, this video practically makes itself. One good version could reach hundreds of thousands of people.
Idea 02
"The full afternoon tea experience at Flower Cafe Jesmond"
Experience Luxury Occasion

Arrive at the cafe. Sit down in the most beautiful corner. The afternoon tea arrives: tiers of sandwiches, scones, pastries, and a pot of tea. Show every detail. The presentation, the setting, the first bite. Make it feel like a special occasion. End with "from [price]. Book the link in bio."

Why it works: Afternoon tea content performs incredibly well because it targets a specific audience who are actively looking for special occasion venues. Birthdays, hen parties, catching up with friends. It is a high-value booking that fills tables during quieter afternoon hours. One video can drive bookings for months as people save it and come back to it when they need a venue.
Idea 03
"We found a secret cocktail bar hidden inside a flower shop"
Discovery Cocktails Surprise

Start outside on Osborne Road. Walk in past the floral displays. The camera reveals that behind the cafe is a cocktail bar with a completely different atmosphere. Show the cocktails being made, the evening lighting, the vibe shift from daytime cafe to evening bar. Play up the "hidden gem" angle.

Why it works: "Hidden" and "secret" discovery content is one of the most viral formats on TikTok. People love feeling like they have found something special that not everyone knows about. It positions the evening cocktail offering as something distinct from the daytime cafe and captures an entirely different audience. The contrast between daytime floral cafe and evening cocktail bar is a story that tells itself.
Idea 04
"Rating the brunch at Newcastle's prettiest cafe"
Review Style Brunch Local

Sit down. Order the signature brunch items. Show each dish arriving. Rate the presentation (10/10 obviously), the taste, the value. Show the interiors throughout. Keep it honest and enthusiastic. End with the overall score and a recommendation. "If you are in Jesmond, you have to come here."

Why it works: Review-style content is trusted more than promotional content. It feels authentic and helps people make decisions. The visual appeal of Flower Cafe means the presentation scores will be off the charts, which creates shareable moments. People searching "best brunch Jesmond" will find this and book immediately.
Idea 05
"Get ready with me: brunch edition at Flower Cafe"
GRWM Lifestyle Aspirational

A "get ready with me" format where someone gets ready for a brunch date, then cuts to arriving at Flower Cafe Jesmond. The outfit, the walk down Osborne Road, the entrance, the table, the food, the photos with friends. It positions visiting Flower Cafe as a lifestyle moment, not just a meal.

Why it works: GRWM content is one of the most popular formats on TikTok and Instagram. It cross-pollinates with fashion, beauty, and lifestyle audiences who might not follow food accounts but are exactly the demographic that visits Flower Cafe. It turns a brunch visit into aspirational lifestyle content and reaches audiences far beyond the food niche.
Digital Enhancements
Beyond Social Media

These are strategic improvements that would make a real difference alongside the social media work, especially for the new Jesmond location.

Build a Dedicated Website

A brand as visually strong as Flower Cafe deserves a website that matches. Right now, there is no central place for potential customers to browse the menu, see the interiors, book a table, or learn about the afternoon tea offering. A stunning, mobile-first website would convert a huge amount of the social media traffic that currently has nowhere to go.

  • Photo gallery that showcases the interiors, food, and cocktails
  • Online booking integration so people can reserve directly
  • Separate pages for each location, with Jesmond featured prominently as the newest branch
  • Afternoon tea and events booking as a dedicated section to drive high-value reservations

Quick win: Even a simple one-page landing site with a gallery, the menu, and a booking link would capture significant business from people searching "Flower Cafe Jesmond" on Google right now and finding very little.

TripAdvisor Recovery Strategy

A 3.4 TripAdvisor rating for a brand this popular is an area where a focused effort could make a big difference. The Jesmond branch has a clean slate. Starting a review generation programme from day one, with QR codes at tables, gentle staff prompts, and follow-up messages, means you can build a strong Jesmond-specific TripAdvisor listing that reflects the actual quality of the experience.

  • Respond professionally to every review across all locations, showing you value customer feedback
  • Set up a dedicated Jesmond TripAdvisor listing and actively drive reviews to it
  • Train staff to ask happy tables to leave a review, especially after afternoon tea and celebrations
  • Target: 50+ reviews with a 4.5+ rating within the first 6 months of the Jesmond location

Google Business Profile for Jesmond

Make sure the Jesmond location has a fully optimised Google Business Profile with professional photos of the interiors, the food, and the cocktails. Post weekly updates about specials, events, and seasonal menus. Respond to every review. For a new location, Google Business is often the very first impression someone gets. It needs to be as beautiful as the cafe itself.
