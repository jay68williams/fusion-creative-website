Ready-Made Content
5 Video Ideas You Could Film Today

These are formats that are working right now for food businesses on TikTok and Instagram. All of them play to Star Pizza's strengths as a high-volume, menu-packed takeaway with fresh dough and standout pizzas.

Idea 01
"How we make our stuffed crust from scratch"
Behind the Scenes ASMR Process

Film the entire stuffed crust process: dough being stretched out, the filling going in, edges being folded and sealed, into the oven, and then the money shot of the crust being pulled apart with cheese stretching. Use a trending audio or go full ASMR with just the natural kitchen sounds.

Why it works: Stuffed crust content is one of the most satisfying food formats on social media. The reveal moment when you cut or pull the crust apart drives massive engagement. This is the kind of video people save, share with friends, and comment "I need this" on. It practically goes viral by itself.
Idea 02
"Ten pounds gets you all this at Star Pizza"
Value Hook Menu Reveal Local

Show what a tenner gets you. Lay out a loaded meal deal: pizza, sides, drink. Film each item being placed down one by one. Close-up of the food. End with the total price on screen. "Ten pounds. Star Pizza. Benwell. You are welcome."

Why it works: Price reveal content is one of the most viral food formats on every platform. People love seeing what they get for their money. It drives comments ("where is this?"), saves (people bookmark it for next time), and shares (people tag their mates). This single video format could be repeated weekly with different combinations and it would perform every time.
Idea 03
"Our most popular pizza vs our spiciest"
Taste Test Debate Engagement

Side by side comparison. Show both pizzas being made. Someone tries both. Give an honest reaction. Ask the audience "Which one are you going for?" End with a poll sticker or a comment prompt. Keep it fun and genuine.

Why it works: Comparison and debate content drives comments like nothing else. People love picking sides. The algorithm rewards videos with high comment counts, so a simple "which one?" question can push your video to thousands of new viewers. It also showcases two menu items in one video, which is smart marketing.
Idea 04
"Friday night in the kitchen"
Energy Social Proof Behind the Scenes

Capture the Friday night rush. Orders coming in, pizzas being made at speed, boxes stacking up, deliveries going out the door. The kitchen in full flow. Quick cuts, fast-paced, set to an upbeat track. Show the energy and the volume. No voiceover needed.

Why it works: Busy kitchen content is instant social proof. When viewers see a kitchen working at full pace with orders flying out, it tells them this place is popular, this place is trusted, this place is where everyone else is ordering from. It creates urgency and FOMO. People see it on a Friday night and immediately want to order.
Idea 05
"Rating every pizza on our menu"
Series Interactive Fun

With 50+ pizzas on the menu, this is a series that could run for weeks. Staff or a regular customer rates each pizza out of 10. Quick reactions, honest opinions. "This one is an 8, but this one... this one is a 10." Keep it light and entertaining. Ask viewers to comment their favourite.

Why it works: Rating content is inherently engaging because people want to agree or disagree. With 50+ pizzas, this becomes a recurring series that keeps people coming back. Each video showcases a different part of the menu, which drives curiosity and orders. It also creates inside jokes and debates among your audience, which builds community.
Digital Enhancements
Beyond Social Media

These are strategic changes that would make a real difference alongside the social media work.

Claim and Build Your TripAdvisor Listing

Your TripAdvisor listing is unclaimed, sitting at #833 of 1,171 with only 6 reviews and a 3.0 rating. This does not reflect the reality of Star Pizza at all. Claiming the listing is free, takes minutes, and lets you add photos, update information, and respond to reviews.

  • Claim the listing at tripadvisor.co.uk/Owners and verify ownership
  • Add professional food photos and your full menu
  • Respond to every review, both positive and negative, to show you care
  • Start prompting happy customers to leave reviews with QR codes on packaging

Quick win: With the volume of customers you serve, getting to 50+ TripAdvisor reviews within 3 months is very achievable. That alone could jump you hundreds of places in the rankings and put you in front of people actively searching for food in Newcastle.

Google Review Strategy

171 Google reviews is a solid base, but the 3.8 rating shows some polarisation. The best way to improve that is to systematically encourage your happy customers to leave reviews. Most satisfied customers never think to review unless prompted. A simple QR code on receipts or delivery bags linking directly to your Google review page would steadily improve both the volume and the average rating.

Responding to every Google review, especially the negative ones, also makes a huge difference. It shows potential customers that you care about the experience and are actively engaged. A thoughtful response to a critical review can actually win new customers.

Leverage That 5/5 Hygiene Rating

A perfect food hygiene score is a powerful trust signal that most takeaways do not have. It should be prominently featured on your website, your social media profiles, your delivery packaging, and your content. When people are choosing between takeaways, hygiene ratings matter. Make sure everyone knows about yours.

  • Add it to your Instagram and Facebook bios
  • Feature it on the website homepage
  • Include it in your social content as a recurring graphic or badge
  • Use it in any paid advertising as a trust element
