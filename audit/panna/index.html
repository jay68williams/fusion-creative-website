Where The Opportunity Is
Five Areas With Real Potential

Panna already has the product, the brand and the engagement. The gap is reach, and the kind of content that actually spreads. These are the five areas where focused effort would turn a beautiful but small account into a name every couple and event planner in the North East knows.

1

Reactions Sell, And They Are Missing From the Content

The content is polished and on brand, but it is mostly the product and the finished setup. What it does not capture is the thing that actually sells an experience: people's faces when they taste it, and the moment guests spot the cart roll in. Reaction is the most shareable thing in food content. A bride lighting up, a guest's eyes going wide on the first spoon, a whole room grinning. That is what makes someone watching at home think "I want that at my wedding".

We would make reaction the core format: film guests tasting, film the reveal of the cart at a venue, film the room when it arrives. It costs nothing extra to capture at events you are already booked for, and it is the single biggest unlock for reach. This is the format we would lead with.

2

Put Paddy and the Team Front and Centre

Your own numbers have already proven this. "Meet the gaffer" is your top post and "Meet Lucy" is right up there. People follow people. Right now Paddy appears occasionally, but he should be the face of the brand: talking about the gelato, why it is different, how it is made, what goes into a custom menu. An owner who clearly loves the craft is far more compelling than another product shot.

A regular Paddy series, him explaining flavours, walking through how a wedding booking works, reacting to events, talking about what makes Panna special, would build real connection. This is exactly the content your data says performs best, and it is some of the cheapest to make.

3

Build Viral, Reaction-Led Challenge Formats

This is where the reach comes from. Simple, repeatable challenge formats at events travel far beyond your followers and put real people and real joy on camera:

Going round wedding guests asking them to guess the flavour. Asking guests to pick which one is best. The "double it or pass it on" trend with a tray of gelato. Handing gelato down a line of guests and capturing every reaction. These are low effort and high return, built to be shared, which is exactly what a small account needs to break out. One of these landing could put you in front of tens of thousands of the right people overnight.

4

Show the Events and How It Actually Works

A lot of people do not know what hiring a gelato cart involves, and that uncertainty stops enquiries. Content that educates while it entertains, Paddy talking to the bride and groom, the cart being set up at the venue, how a couple choose their custom menu, the branded wafers with a company logo for a corporate event, demystifies the whole thing.

This does two jobs at once. It sells the premium, bespoke nature of what you do, and it answers the questions a couple has before they have to message you. The more the content explains, the warmer and more frequent the enquiries become.

5

Reach Is the Only Real Ceiling, And There Is No TikTok

Everything points to the same thing: the content converts well, there just is not enough of it reaching enough people. A top reel around 1,900 views is a small ceiling for a product this photogenic. And there is no TikTok account at all, which is currently the single best platform for visual, reaction-led, event content.

Launching TikTok, posting reaction and challenge content consistently, and cross-posting to Reels would multiply your reach. For an events business where one booking is worth hundreds, even a modest lift in reach pays for itself many times over.

Ready-Made Content
4 Video Ideas You Could Film This Week

These are formats working right now on TikTok and Instagram Reels, tailored to Panna's gelato, the events, and Paddy. They are reaction-led and built to spread. Any one of these could be filmed on a phone at your next event.

Idea 01
"We asked wedding guests to guess the flavour"
Reaction Challenge Guests

At a wedding, go round guests with a spoon of each gelato and ask them to guess the flavour blind. Capture the faces, the confusion, the delight, the "oh my god that is amazing". Quick cuts between guests, caption their guesses. Trending audio. Under 30 seconds.

Why it works: Reaction plus challenge is the most shareable combination in food content. Real guests, real reactions, at a real wedding. It shows the product tasting incredible and the experience being fun, and it is exactly the format that breaks a small account out to new audiences.
Idea 02
"Handing out gelato at a wedding and filming every reaction"
Reaction Hero Format Joy

Set up the cart, then film the line of guests as they get their gelato. Every face lighting up, the first spoon, the "this is unreal", kids and grandparents alike, the bride and groom getting theirs. End on the room buzzing. Warm trending audio. Under 25 seconds.

Why it works: Pure joy sells an experience better than any product shot. This is the reel a couple watches and instantly pictures at their own wedding. Reaction content like this consistently outperforms polished setups, and it is free to capture at every event.
Idea 03
"How a Panna wedding booking actually works, with Paddy"
Owner Education Trust

Paddy to camera, in his element. What makes the gelato different, how a couple choose their custom menu, the branded wafers, how it all comes together on the day. Cut between Paddy talking and shots of the cart, the gelato, a real event. Honest and passionate. 45 to 60 seconds.

Why it works: Your owner content is already your best performing. People buy from people. This builds trust, demystifies the booking, and answers the questions a couple has before they enquire. The more Paddy explains, the warmer the enquiries get.
Idea 04
"Double it or pass it on, wedding edition"
Trend Challenge Reaction

The trending "double it or pass it on" format with a tray of gelato going round the wedding. Each guest decides whether to keep one or double up for the table, with their reaction each time. Fun, fast, full of personality. Trending audio. Under 30 seconds.

Why it works: Plugging your product into a trend people already know gives it a head start with the algorithm. It is playful, reaction-led, and shows the gelato being passed around a happy room. Exactly the kind of low-effort, high-reach content that grows a small account fast.
Digital Enhancements
Beyond Social Media

A few extra digital wins that would complement the content work.

Launch TikTok and Double Your Reach

There is no TikTok account yet, and it is currently the best platform for reaction-led, visual event content. Every reel you make for Instagram can be cross-posted, so it is free reach you are leaving on the table.

  • Set up TikTok with the @panna_gelato handle
  • Cross-post every reel to TikTok and YouTube Shorts
  • Use Newcastle and North East location tags, plus wedding and event hashtags
  • Lean into the reaction and challenge formats, which over-perform on TikTok

Run Paid Ads to the People Planning Events

This is a perfect business for paid ads. Bookings happen months in advance, one booking is worth hundreds, and Meta lets you target exactly the right people, recently engaged couples, event planners, anyone interested in weddings in your area. A small, well-targeted budget can fill the calendar well ahead of time.

  • Target engaged couples and event planners across the North East, and wider for destination venues
  • Use your best reaction and owner content as the ads, the same content that performs organically
  • Retarget anyone who watches a reel or visits the site but does not enquire
  • Track cost per enquiry and cost per booking so the spend is always accountable

Why it fits Panna: For an events business, ads are not a gamble. One booking covers the spend many times over, and because you book so far ahead, ads let you fill quieter months on purpose.

Build a Booking Flow on the Website

The site looks great, but right now enquiries mean a back-and-forth by email or DM. A proper booking flow, where a couple can pick their date, package, guest numbers and custom options in a few steps, would capture more enquiries and cut the admin massively. It is also exactly where your ads should send people.

  • A step-by-step enquiry form: date, venue, package, guest count, custom menu and wafers
  • Instant confirmation and a clear next step, so hot leads do not go cold
  • A dedicated landing page for ad traffic, built to convert
  • Availability so couples can see and secure a date with less back-and-forth

The opportunity: Ads and content drive people to the site, and a clean booking flow turns that traffic into booked events with far less manual chasing. This is how you scale beyond word of mouth.
