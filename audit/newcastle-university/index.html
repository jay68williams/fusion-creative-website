What We Noticed

A strong brand with
room to scale

Instagram is strong at 123K, but TikTok at 30K is underperforming for where Gen Z actually spends their time. For a university competing for UCAS applications, that gap is a missed opportunity with the exact demographic you need to reach.
The Organic Social Media Assistant role is asking one person to do too much. Content creation, community management, analytics, student team coordination, and internal training across 4+ platforms. That is a lot to put on a single junior hire at the 29-31K band.
Short-form video output is likely inconsistent given the current team size constraints. Professional Reels and TikToks require dedicated filming time, editing skills, and trend awareness that are difficult to maintain alongside community management and reporting duties.
No video-first content on the homepage or social media directory page. The website leads with static imagery and text. In 2026, prospective students expect to see the campus, the city, and the student experience in motion before they ever visit in person.
Blog content is fragmented across 4 separate subdomains. This dilutes the content strategy and makes it harder to build a cohesive brand voice. A unified short-form video presence would tie these narratives together across platforms.
The "Hero, Hub, Help" content framework is smart but it needs more Hero-level video content. The kind that stops the scroll, sparks emotion, and gets shared. That is where a dedicated video team makes the biggest difference.

Content Concepts

5 videos we would make first

1

"This Is Newcastle"

Cinematic campus and city montage. Golden hour on the Armstrong Building, the Arches, students crossing the Quad, the Tyne Bridge at dusk. Trending audio. The video that makes a sixth-former choose Newcastle over every other university they are considering.

2

"Lecture Break"

Quick campus vlog format. Library to coffee to lecture hall and back. Real student energy captured in 30 seconds. The kind of content that performs on TikTok because it feels authentic, not produced by a marketing department.

3

"Research That Matters"

60-second visual explainer of groundbreaking Newcastle research. Make complex science accessible and shareable. The kind of Hero content that builds reputation beyond the student audience and into mainstream visibility.

4

"Freshers' Guide"

The real student experience, not the prospectus version. Best coffee spots, where to sit in the library, the kebab shop at 2am, the walk from halls to campus. Content that speaks directly to incoming students and gets saved and shared in group chats.

5

"City vs Campus"

Split-screen format. University life meets Newcastle nightlife, culture, and food. Show prospective students that choosing Newcastle means getting a world-class education in a world-class city. Two selling points in one piece of content.
