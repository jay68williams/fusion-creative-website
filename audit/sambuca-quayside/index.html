Ready-Made Content
5 Video Ideas You Could Film Today

These are formats that are working right now for restaurants on TikTok and Instagram. All of them play to Sambuca Quayside's unique strengths: the riverside location, the value, the people, and the atmosphere.

Idea 01
"POV: Italian dinner with THIS view"
Location Atmosphere Food

Camera starts on a plate of food. Slow pan up and out through the window to reveal the Tyne Bridge lit up at night. The river reflecting the lights. Back to the table. Cocktails, pasta, the whole scene. No voiceover needed. Let the view do the talking. End with a text overlay: "Sambuca Quayside. Every table has this view."

Why it works: The view sells the restaurant. The Tyne Bridge at night is one of the most recognisable landmarks in the North East. A video that reveals it as your dinner backdrop creates an immediate emotional response. People save it, share it, tag friends. "We need to go here." That is the comment you want, and this video gets it.
Idea 02
"We have been serving Italian classics since 2001"
Heritage Brand Story Trust

A heritage story video. Show the restaurant through the years if you have old photos. Show the kitchen, the team, the regulars. The hands that make the food. The dining room being set up before service. A voiceover or text overlay telling the story: "25 years on the Quayside. Same recipes. Same passion. Same view." End with a packed Saturday night.

Why it works: Chain restaurants struggle with authenticity on social media. People assume they are corporate and impersonal. A heritage story that shows 25 years of real history, real people, and real food breaks that assumption completely. It turns Sambuca Quayside from "a chain restaurant" into "the Italian that has been on the Quayside for a quarter of a century." That is a completely different proposition.
Idea 03
"Our waiter Adam has 50+ five-star reviews"
Staff Spotlight Social Proof Personality

Show screenshots of TripAdvisor reviews mentioning Adam by name. Then cut to Adam at work. Greeting customers, carrying plates, recommending dishes, having a laugh with a table. Let his personality come through. End with: "Come for the Italian food. Stay because Adam will not let you leave without trying the tiramisu."

Why it works: People follow people, not restaurants. Adam is mentioned by name in dozens of reviews. He is already a reason people come back. Turning him into a content character gives the restaurant a human face. It is personal, relatable, and shareable. When someone sees Adam in a video and then meets him in person, the experience feels connected. That builds loyalty and repeat visits.
Idea 04
"What you get for under 15 pounds at Sambuca"
Value Hook Price Reveal Local

Walk into the restaurant. Sit down at a table by the window. Order a two-course meal or the lunch deal. Show each dish arriving. Close-ups of the food. The view behind it. End with the bill or a price graphic. "All of this. Under 15 pounds. On the Quayside." Disbelief is the reaction you want.

Why it works: Price reveal content is one of the most viral restaurant formats on both TikTok and Instagram. People genuinely cannot believe you can eat proper Italian food with Tyne Bridge views for that price. It drives comments ("where is this?"), saves (people bookmark it), and shares (people tag friends). The combination of quality, value, and location makes this almost unfairly shareable.
Idea 05
"Live music Friday night at Sambuca Quayside"
Atmosphere Events Nightlife

Capture a busy Friday evening. The live musician performing. People clinking cocktail glasses. Plates of Italian food going out. The river and bridge lights through the windows. Quick cuts, warm tones, the energy of a great night out. End with: "Every Friday. Live music. Italian food. The best view in Newcastle."

Why it works: Atmosphere content drives bookings more than food content alone. People do not just choose a restaurant for the food. They choose it for the experience. A video that shows a lively Friday night with live music, great food, and riverside views makes the viewer feel like they are missing out. That fear of missing out turns into a booking. This is the kind of video that gets shared in group chats with "Friday night here?"
Digital Enhancements
Beyond Social Media

These are strategic changes that would make a real difference alongside the social media work.

Give the Quayside Location Its Own Landing Page or Microsite

The current page on sambucarestaurants.co.uk is a generic chain listing that does not capture what makes the Quayside location special. The Quayside branch deserves its own dedicated page or microsite that showcases the experience: the views, the events, the vibe, the team.

  • Atmospheric photography showing the riverside setting and Tyne Bridge views at different times of day
  • An events calendar highlighting live music nights, special menus, and seasonal events
  • Staff profiles featuring team members like Adam who are mentioned by name in reviews
  • A curated selection of customer reviews that specifically praise the Quayside location

Key point: The Quayside location has a unique selling point that no other Sambuca branch has. The website should make someone feel the atmosphere before they have even walked through the door. Right now it does not. Make it feel like a destination, not just another branch listing.

Leverage the Riverside Location in Every Single Piece of Content

The Tyne Bridge and Quayside at night are one of the most photographed scenes in Newcastle. This is an asset that most restaurants would dream of having. Every video, every photo, every story should include the view. It is the single most powerful visual hook you have and it costs nothing to use.

  • Set a rule: no content goes out without the river or bridge visible somewhere in the shot
  • Use the location tag on every Instagram post and reel to capture people browsing Quayside content
  • Create content at different times: sunset, blue hour, full dark with the bridge lights. Each creates a different mood
  • Encourage customers to tag the location when they post their own photos. User-generated content from the Quayside is free marketing

Turn Adam and the Staff into Content Characters

Personal service is what people rave about in the reviews. Adam is mentioned by name in dozens of TripAdvisor reviews. That is extraordinary for a member of staff. He is already a reason people come back. Show the people, not just the food.

  • Create recurring content series featuring staff: "Adam's pick of the week," "Ask the chef," "Behind the bar"
  • Film genuine interactions between staff and customers (with permission). Authenticity is what performs on social media
  • Let staff personalities come through. People follow people. A restaurant with recognisable, likeable staff builds a community, not just a customer base

Why this matters: The biggest challenge for chain restaurants on social media is feeling corporate and impersonal. Staff-led content is the antidote. When people see Adam, the chef, the bartender, they connect with humans, not a brand. That connection drives loyalty, repeat visits, and word-of-mouth.
