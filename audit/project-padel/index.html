Where The Opportunity Is
Three Areas With Huge Potential

You have the venue, the sport, and the early-mover advantage. These are the areas where investing in your digital presence could accelerate growth significantly.

1

No TikTok Presence for the Most Visual Sport Going

Padel is inherently visual. The glass courts, the fast rallies, the dramatic smashes, the social energy of doubles play. It is made for short-form video. TikTok is where sports discovery happens now, especially for newer sports that people are curious about. "What is padel?" is searched thousands of times a month in the UK, and the people searching are exactly the audience you want to reach.

Right now, there is almost no professional padel content from the North East on TikTok. That is a wide open space waiting to be filled. The first padel venue in Newcastle to own TikTok will capture an enormous amount of attention, and right now that opportunity is yours for the taking.

2

Community Building Through Leagues and Tournaments

The biggest padel venues in the UK have built their businesses on community, not just court bookings. Leagues, tournaments, social mixers, beginner sessions, and coaching programmes create recurring revenue and turn one-time visitors into regulars who come back every week.

More importantly, community events are content goldmines. A monthly tournament gives you footage of competitive play, celebrations, socialising in the cafe afterwards, and stories from players. That content then attracts new players who want to be part of it. It creates a flywheel: events drive content, content drives new members, new members fill more events.

3

"What Is Padel?" Educational Content Is a Massive Opportunity

Most people in Newcastle have heard of padel but have never played it and do not fully understand it. That is not a problem. That is an enormous opportunity. Educational content explaining the sport, showing how easy it is to pick up, demystifying the rules, and making it feel accessible is exactly what drives first-time bookings.

"I tried padel for the first time" is one of the highest-performing content formats across TikTok and Instagram right now. People love watching others try something new, especially when it looks fun and social. Position Project Padel as the place where everyone is welcome, from complete beginners to competitive players, and that content will do the marketing for you.

Ready-Made Content
5 Video Ideas You Could Film Today

These are formats that are working right now for sports venues and fitness brands on TikTok and Instagram. All of them play to Project Padel's strengths as Newcastle's first and only dedicated padel venue.

Idea 01
"I tried padel for the first time. Here is what happened."
Beginner First Experience Relatable

Follow a complete beginner from arriving at the venue to playing their first point. Show the nervous energy, the first swing, the first rally, the first celebration. End with them saying they are already booking another session. Keep it real and unscripted.

Why it works: "I tried X for the first time" is one of the most consistently viral formats on TikTok. It removes the intimidation barrier. People watching think "if they can do it, so can I." It directly drives first-time bookings from people who have been curious but have not taken the leap.
Idea 02
"POV: You just walked into Newcastle's first padel venue"
Facility Tour POV Discovery

First-person walk-through of the venue. Push through the doors, see the courts, hear the sound of padel balls, see players in action, walk past the cafe, see the buzz. Use a cinematic audio track. Let the space sell itself.

Why it works: POV content performs incredibly well because it puts the viewer inside the experience. People who have never been to a padel venue do not know what to expect. This video removes that uncertainty and makes them want to visit. It also showcases your clean, professional facility.
Idea 03
"Padel explained in 30 seconds"
Educational Quick Guide Shareable

Fast-paced breakdown of what padel is, how it works, why it is different from tennis, and why everyone is obsessed with it. Use on-court footage, quick text overlays, and energetic music. End with "Book a court. Link in bio."

Why it works: "What is padel?" is one of the most searched sports questions in the UK right now. A clean, shareable explainer positions Project Padel as the local authority on the sport. People will save it, share it with friends, and tag people they want to play with. It is evergreen content that keeps driving bookings for months.
Idea 04
"The best point we have ever seen at Project Padel"
Highlights Action Viral Potential

Capture an incredible rally or point. Slow it down. Add dramatic music. Show the reactions. Replay it from another angle. This is pure entertainment content that showcases the excitement of the sport. No explanation needed. Just raw, thrilling padel.

Why it works: Sports highlight clips are some of the most shared content on social media. A spectacular padel point will reach people who have never even heard of the sport and make them curious. It crosses over from the padel community into the general sports audience. One clip could reach hundreds of thousands of people across the North East.
Idea 05
"We challenged a tennis player to try padel. They were not ready."
Challenge Crossover Entertainment

Invite a local tennis player to try padel. Show the moments where tennis instincts help, and the moments where padel catches them off guard. The glass walls, the underarm serve, the different strategy. Make it fun, competitive, and entertaining.

Why it works: Challenge content drives enormous engagement. The padel vs tennis angle taps into an existing audience of tennis players who are exactly the demographic most likely to convert into padel players. It is entertaining, shareable, and directly targets the people you want to attract.
Digital Enhancements
Beyond Social Media

These are strategic improvements that would make a real difference alongside the social media work.

Google Business Profile Optimisation

As a relatively new venue, building your Google presence is critical. Regular photo uploads of the courts, the cafe, and events. Responding to every review. Posting weekly updates about leagues, open sessions, and special offers. When someone searches "padel near me" or "things to do in Newcastle," your Google listing needs to be as impressive as the venue itself.

Quick win: Encourage every visitor to leave a Google review. A QR code at reception or in the cafe that links directly to your Google review page. Getting to 100+ reviews with a strong rating will dramatically improve your visibility in local search results.

Targeted Paid Advertising

Padel appeals to a specific demographic: active people aged 25-50 who enjoy racket sports, fitness, and social activities. That is an incredibly targetable audience on Facebook and Instagram. A campaign targeting tennis club members, gym-goers, and sports enthusiasts within 15 miles of Benton would drive trial bookings at a very low cost per acquisition.

  • Retarget website visitors who browsed but did not book
  • Lookalike audiences based on your existing customer base
  • Seasonal campaigns: "New year, new sport" in January, "summer leagues" in June, corporate events year-round
  • Offer a discounted first session to remove the barrier to entry for newcomers

Email and Community Building

Every person who books a court is a potential repeat customer. Building an email list and a WhatsApp community group would let you communicate directly with your player base about upcoming leagues, tournaments, social events, and last-minute court availability. This turns one-time visitors into a loyal community.
