Where The Opportunity Is
Areas With Real Growth Potential

The food, the reviews, and the customer loyalty are all genuinely strong. You have built something special. These are the areas where a bit of focused effort could take things to the next level.

1

Instagram Following Does Not Match Your Reputation

With a 4.8 Google rating, a 9.6 on TheFork, and consistently glowing reviews, Zenobia should have a much larger Instagram following than 2,558. For context, restaurants in Newcastle with similar review quality typically have 5,000 to 15,000 followers. The gap between the quality of your food and the size of your audience represents a huge growth opportunity.

The good news is that the hardest part, having genuinely great food that people love, is already done. The content is already happening in your kitchen every day. It just needs to be captured and shared consistently. Short-form video of charcoal grills, shawarma being carved, and falafel being prepared would perform incredibly well on Instagram Reels.

2

No Website Is Holding You Back

Using Linktree instead of a proper website means you are missing out on search traffic, credibility, and control over the customer journey. When someone Googles "Syrian food Newcastle" or "Arabian restaurant Heaton," a dedicated website with your menu, story, location, and booking options would rank and convert. A Linktree page cannot do that.

A clean, mobile-first website does not need to be complicated. Your menu, your story, your location and hours, and a way to book or order. That is it. But it gives you a proper home on the internet that you own and control, and it dramatically improves how you appear in search results.

3

TripAdvisor Ranking Has Room to Climb

You are currently ranked #323 of 1,135 restaurants in Newcastle on TripAdvisor, with 41 reviews at 4.5 stars. That rating is strong, but the review volume is keeping your ranking lower than it should be. TripAdvisor's algorithm heavily favours recent review volume. A focused effort to encourage happy customers to leave reviews could push you into the top 100 within a few months.

A simple QR code on the bill linking directly to your TripAdvisor page, combined with a friendly prompt from staff, would make a meaningful difference. Your customers already love the food. They just need an easy way to share that.

4

TikTok Is Your Biggest Untapped Channel

You have a TikTok account, which is great, but the platform is massively underutilised. Charcoal-grilled meats, open flames, sizzling shawarma, and fresh flatbread being made by hand. This is exactly the kind of content that goes viral on TikTok. The ASMR quality of your cooking process alone is a content goldmine.

Restaurants posting consistent short-form video on TikTok are seeing direct, measurable increases in footfall. One well-shot video of a shawarma being carved or a mixed grill platter being assembled could reach hundreds of thousands of people in the North East without spending a penny on advertising.

5

Four-Day Opening Creates Urgency (Use It)

Being open Thursday to Sunday could feel like a limitation, but it is actually a brilliant content angle if framed correctly. "Only 4 days a week. Do not miss it." That kind of scarcity messaging drives urgency and bookings. It makes every opening day feel like an event.

The key is to make your social media work harder on the days you are closed. Monday to Wednesday should be about building anticipation: throwback food shots, behind-the-scenes prep, countdowns to Thursday. By the time you open, people should already be planning their visit.

Ready-Made Content
5 Video Ideas You Could Film Today

These are formats that are working right now for restaurants on TikTok and Instagram. All of them play to Zenobia's strengths as an authentic Syrian street food restaurant with incredible food and a great story.

Idea 01
"How we grill over real charcoal at Zenobia"
Behind the Scenes ASMR Process

Start with the charcoal being lit. Show the flames building. Then the meats going on the grill. Close-up of the sizzle, the char marks forming, the juices dripping. Pull back to reveal the full grill loaded with skewers. End with a finished plate hitting the table. Keep it under 30 seconds. Let the sound do the work.

Why it works: Charcoal grill content is one of the highest-performing food formats on TikTok and Instagram. The combination of fire, sizzling sounds, and smoke triggers an almost primal response. People share it, save it, and most importantly, they want to eat it.
Idea 02
"The shawarma carve that stops the scroll"
Food Porn Satisfying Signature Dish

Close-up of the shawarma rotating. The knife slicing through the outer layer. Thin strips falling onto the plate. Then the wrap being assembled: flatbread, meat, sauces, garnish. Wrap it up. Cut it in half. Show the cross-section. That is the money shot.

Why it works: Shawarma carving videos regularly get millions of views on TikTok. The slicing motion is hypnotic and satisfying. It is one of the most universally appealing food formats online, and Zenobia does it over real charcoal which makes it even more compelling.
Idea 03
"From a market stall to our own restaurant"
Origin Story Emotional Brand

Tell the Zenobia story in 60 seconds. Start with footage or photos from the early market stall days. Show the journey: the early mornings, the queues growing, the loyal customers. Then reveal the restaurant on Chillingham Road. End with a busy service, plates going out, happy customers. Use a voiceover or text overlay with a warm backing track.

Why it works: Origin stories are some of the most powerful content a small business can create. People root for real stories. A family bringing authentic Syrian cuisine to Newcastle, starting at a market and building to their own restaurant. That is the kind of story people share because it makes them feel something.
Idea 04
"We are only open 4 days a week. Here is why it is worth the wait."
Scarcity FOMO Local

Start with a text hook: "We are only open Thursday to Sunday." Then rapid-fire cuts of your best dishes, the busiest moments, happy customers, the charcoal grill at full capacity. Make it feel like an event. End with "Thursday is coming. Are you ready?" and your location.

Why it works: Scarcity drives action. When people know something is only available for a limited time, they prioritise it. Framing your 4-day schedule as an exclusive experience rather than a limitation turns it into a marketing advantage. People will plan their week around it.
Idea 05
"The mixed grill platter that feeds the whole table"
Hero Dish Sharing Value

Build the platter from scratch. Each element being placed: grilled meats, falafel, hummus, flatbread, salads, sauces. The camera pulls back to reveal the full spread. Then show it landing at a table with a group of friends. Their reactions. Hands reaching in. The shared experience of eating together. End with "Chillingham Road, Newcastle. Thursday to Sunday."

Why it works: Platter and sharing content is incredibly shareable because it is inherently social. People tag their friends with "we need to go here." The generous portions Zenobia is known for make this even more impactful. When viewers see how much food arrives, the value proposition sells itself.
Digital Enhancements
Beyond Social Media

These are strategic changes that would make a real difference alongside the social media work.

Build a Proper Website

A Linktree page is a placeholder, not a website. You need a dedicated site that ranks in search results for terms like "Syrian food Newcastle," "Arabian restaurant Heaton," and "best shawarma Newcastle." A clean, mobile-first website with your menu, story, location, hours, and a way to book would immediately improve discoverability.

  • A dedicated domain (something like zenobiaarabian.co.uk) gives you a professional presence
  • SEO-optimised pages would help you rank for local food searches
  • An embedded booking or ordering system removes friction from the customer journey
  • Your origin story and food philosophy deserve a proper home, not a list of links

Quick win: Even a simple one-page website with your menu, location, hours, and a booking button would be a massive improvement over a Linktree. It takes your online presence from "we exist" to "we are a serious restaurant."

TripAdvisor Review Push

41 reviews at 4.5 stars is a great foundation, but you are ranked #323 in Newcastle. The restaurants above you often have lower ratings but many more reviews. A systematic review generation effort could change your ranking dramatically within a few months.

  • QR codes on bills linking directly to TripAdvisor and Google review pages
  • A friendly prompt from staff when customers express how much they enjoyed the meal
  • Target: 150+ TripAdvisor reviews within 6 months to push into the top 100

Google Business Profile Optimisation

Your 4.8 Google rating is outstanding. Make sure the listing is working as hard as possible: professional food photos, regular updates about specials, responses to every review, and accurate hours including market stall times. Google Business is often the first impression people get of your restaurant. Make it count.
