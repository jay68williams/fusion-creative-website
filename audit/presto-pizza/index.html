What Is Already Working Well
Credit Where It Is Due

✓ You are already posting consistently

Eight reels in about three weeks, roughly two to three a week. That sounds basic, but the vast majority of takeaways in Newcastle post nothing at all, or one photo a month. You are already in the habit of creating, which is the single hardest thing to start. We do not have to convince you to post. We just have to upgrade what you post.

✓ Your reels are already getting reach

Your posts are pulling 500 to 1,500 views each, with your best at 1,422. That means Instagram is already pushing your content to people who do not follow you. The distribution is there. The job now is making the content good enough to hold those viewers and convert them into followers and customers.

✓ You have a real following to build on

Around 1,910 followers is a genuine base. Plenty of businesses we work with start from near zero. You are starting with an audience that already chose to follow a pizza shop in Byker, which tells us there is local appetite for the content. We just need to give them a reason to engage and share.

✓ Your deal content clearly lands

The "£11 Munchies Box", "Budget Deal" and "World Cup meal deal" posts are among your better performers. Your audience responds to value. That is a real insight. We would keep leaning into deals and offers, just filmed so they make people hungry rather than reading like a static price card.

✓ You are already trying topical content

Tying posts to the World Cup shows good instinct. Hooking content to what people are already talking about is exactly the right idea, and it is how a lot of small food accounts break out. The instinct is there. It just needs sharper production and a stronger hook in the first second.

✓ The food itself is built for social

Pizza, kebabs and grills are some of the most reliably viral food on the internet. Cheese pulls, the kebab coming off the spit, a fresh box being built. This is exactly the content that does numbers. You are sitting on the right product for short-form video, it just is not being filmed to show it off yet.

Where The Opportunity Is
Five Areas With Real Potential

Presto already has the hardest parts in place: a following, regular posting, reach, and food that is built for video. These are the five areas where focused effort would turn those reels from a couple of hundred likes a month into real local reach and orders.

1

Your Profile Is Losing Followers Before They Even Watch

Your bio just says "ORDER NOW" with a link. There is no location, no "Byker, Newcastle", no mention of what you sell, no hours, and there are no story highlights at all. So every time one of your reels sends someone to your profile, there is nothing that tells them who you are, where you are, or why they should follow.

This is the cheapest, fastest win available. Rewriting the bio with your location, what you do, and a real call to action, plus adding story highlights for Menu, Deals and Reviews, is a one-hour job that lifts the follow rate on every single reel from here on. You are already getting the views. This makes them count.

2

The Content Is All "Deal Cards", Not Food

Almost every reel is a product shot with a price graphic on top. It tells people about an offer, but it does not make them hungry and it does not stop the scroll. Tellingly, your single best-performing post was just a simple clip of the food itself. That is the signal staring you in the face.

Appetite-led content, the cheese pull, the kebab coming off the spit, the sizzle, the first slice, consistently beats static deal graphics on Reels and TikTok. We would keep your deals and offers, your audience clearly responds to them, but film them so they look unmissable rather than reading like a price list. Same message, completely different result.

3

There Are No People In Your Content

Right now it is all food and graphics. No faces, no kitchen, no team. Faceless product reels cap out fast because there is nothing for the viewer to connect to. People follow people, not menus, and that is doubly true for a local takeaway.

A bit of personality from whoever is making the pizza, the energy of a busy Friday night, the team behind the counter, is the cheapest and fastest way to build a loyal local following. Once people recognise the faces at Presto, they are not just ordering a pizza, they are backing their local. That is what turns a one-off order into a regular.

4

Weak Captions, Hooks And Hashtags

Captions are often just "Presto Pizza" with a single hashtag, #newcastleupontyne. The first line of a caption and the first second of a video are what decide whether someone stops scrolling, and right now both are being left on the table.

Adding a proper hook to every post, and a mix of local and food hashtags like #NewcastleFood, #Byker, #NEeats and #NewcastleEats, widens how far each reel travels and puts it in front of more of the right local people. Small change, compounding effect across every post.

5

No System, So It Is Inconsistent And Hard To Sustain

You are posting, which is great, but it is clearly being squeezed in around actually running the shop, and it shows in the production. Doing it yourself between orders is not sustainable and it caps how good each post can be.

A simple repeatable system fixes that: one short batch shoot, a set of formats proven to work, and someone else handling the editing, captions, hooks and posting. That means three to four strong reels a week without it eating your time on a Friday night. Consistency plus quality is what compounds into real, lasting growth.

Ready-Made Content
4 Video Ideas You Could Film This Week

These are formats working right now on TikTok and Instagram Reels, tailored to Presto's pizza, kebabs and deals. Even before we officially start, any one of these could be filmed on a phone tonight and posted this week.

Idea 01
"POV: it's Friday night in Byker"
Food Closeup ASMR Hero Format

Close-up, slow motion. A fresh pizza straight out of the oven, the first slice lifting away, the cheese stretching long. Steam rising, a hand reaching in. No graphics, just the food. Trending audio. Under 15 seconds and built to loop.

Why it works: The cheese pull is one of the most reliably scroll-stopping shots in all of food content. Your single best post was already just food, this turns that insight into a repeatable hero format. Zero budget, films in two minutes, and it is exactly what the current deal-card reels are missing.
Idea 02
"The most satisfying 10 seconds in Byker"
Process ASMR Signature

Tight close-ups. The doner shaving off the spit, the meat falling, the sizzle on the grill, sauces going on, wrapped and cut in half to show the inside. Pure sound or a trending audio. Fast, satisfying, under 20 seconds.

Why it works: The kebab-off-the-spit shave is ASMR gold and travels far beyond your followers. It shows off exactly what you do best and makes people hungry instantly. This is the kind of clip that gets saved and shared, which is what pushes reach the most.
Idea 03
"Everything you get in our £11 Munchies Box"
Value Deal Build

Film the box being built in real time. Each item going in, the pizza, the kebab, the chips, the sides, the sauces, until it is full. End on the loaded box and the £11 price. Quick cuts, trending sound. Your munchies box reel already did well, this is the filmed-properly version.

Why it works: You already know value content lands with your audience. The difference is filming the build live instead of a static price card. Value-reveal builds are a proven format and yours has clear local demand. Same deal, far more appetite.
Idea 04
"A Friday night at Presto Pizza"
Behind the Scenes People Energy

A fast montage of a busy service. The oven firing, dough being stretched and spun, the team plating up and laughing, orders flying out the door, a customer grabbing their box. Quick cuts, upbeat trending audio, a few faces on camera. 20 to 30 seconds.

Why it works: People follow people. Putting faces and energy to the brand is the cheapest way to turn viewers into a loyal local following. It is the lane your content is completely missing right now, and it is what turns one-off orders into regulars.
