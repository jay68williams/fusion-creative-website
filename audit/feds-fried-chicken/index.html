Where The Opportunity Is
Three Areas With Real Growth Potential

Fed's has the product, the brand, and the loyal following. These opportunities are about making sure the digital presence matches the hype and catches up with the real-world reputation.

1

No TripAdvisor Listing for the New Grey Street Location

This is a quick win that should not be overlooked. The new Grey Street location opened in March 2026, and there is currently no TripAdvisor listing for it. TripAdvisor is still one of the most important discovery platforms for restaurants, especially for tourists and visitors to Newcastle who rely heavily on it to find places to eat.

Creating a TripAdvisor listing and encouraging early customers to leave reviews would establish Fed's on the platform from day one. Given the quality of the product and the enthusiasm of the customer base, the reviews would be overwhelmingly positive. Within a few months, Fed's could have a strong TripAdvisor presence that brings in new customers who discover you through the platform.

2

Instagram at 7.4K Does Not Match the Hype

Fed's has cult status in Newcastle. People genuinely love this brand. But 7,430 Instagram followers does not reflect that level of enthusiasm. For context, restaurants with less passionate followings often have significantly larger Instagram audiences simply because they post consistently and use video content effectively.

The opportunity is clear: consistent, high-quality short-form video content that captures the energy, the brand personality, and the food. Fried chicken is one of the most viral food categories on social media. The crunch, the pull, the sauce drip. Combined with Fed's distinctive brand identity, a focused Instagram strategy could grow the following dramatically in a short period.

3

No Visible Phone Number or Easy Contact

When someone searches for Fed's Fried Chicken online, there is no easily visible phone number. For a food business, this is a small but meaningful friction point. People who want to call to check opening hours, ask about the menu, or place an order need an easy way to reach you. Making a phone number visible on Google Business, the website, and social media profiles removes a barrier between a potential customer and a visit.

Similarly, ensuring the Google Business profile for the new Grey Street location is fully optimised with accurate hours, photos, menu information, and contact details would improve local search visibility and make it easier for people to find you.

Ready-Made Content
5 Video Ideas You Could Film Today

These are formats that are performing right now on TikTok and Instagram. Fried chicken is one of the most viral food categories online, and every one of these ideas plays to Fed's unique strengths.

Idea 01
"How we brine our chicken in sweet tea at Fed's"
Behind the Scenes Process USP

Show the sweet tea brine being made. The chicken going in. Time-lapse of it brining. Then the coating, the fry, and the final product. The entire journey from raw ingredient to finished plate. Let the process tell the story of why Fed's chicken tastes different from everything else.

Why it works: Process content is one of the highest-performing food formats. Sweet tea brining is unusual and intriguing. People will watch it because they are curious, and they will share it because it is genuinely interesting. This video positions Fed's as a craft fried chicken brand, not just another chicken shop.
Idea 02
"From a Grainger Market stall to Grey Street. The Fed's story."
Origin Story Brand Growth

Tell the story visually. Old photos or footage of the Grainger Market days. The growth. The community. Then reveal the new Grey Street location. The fit-out, the first service, the queue. End with a shot of the new space packed with people. "From a market stall to Grey Street. Still the same chicken."

Why it works: Origin stories create emotional connection. People root for independent businesses that grow from humble beginnings. This video humanises the brand, builds loyalty, and gives new customers a reason to care about Fed's beyond just the food. It is also the kind of content that gets shared widely because people love a good success story.
Idea 03
"The crunch heard around Newcastle"
ASMR Food Porn Sensory

Pure sensory content. Close-up of biting into the chicken. The crunch. The steam. The sauce drip. Multiple angles, multiple pieces of chicken. No music, just the sounds of fried chicken being eaten. Let the audio do the work. End with "Fed's Fried Chicken. Grey Street. Newcastle."

Why it works: Fried chicken ASMR is one of the most watched food content categories in the world. The crunch is universally satisfying. This type of content gets millions of views because it triggers a visceral, physical response. People watch it, crave chicken, and then they need to know where to get it. Simple, effective, and endlessly repeatable.
Idea 04
"We make every single sauce from scratch at Fed's"
Process Quality Differentiation

Quick cuts of every sauce being made. The ingredients going in, the mixing, the tasting, the bottling. Show the range. Show the colours. Show the care that goes into each one. End with all the sauces lined up next to a piece of chicken. "Every sauce. From scratch. Every day."

Why it works: "Made from scratch" content positions Fed's as premium and craft-focused. It differentiates you from every chain that uses pre-made sauces. People respect the effort, and they will pay more and travel further to support a business that does things properly. This video is proof of quality that customers can see with their own eyes.
Idea 05
"Friday night queue at Fed's new Grey Street spot"
Social Proof Energy New Location

Capture the energy of a busy Friday night. The queue, the kitchen in full swing, orders going out, people eating on Grey Street. Fast cuts, high energy, upbeat music that matches the 90s skate brand aesthetic. End with a wide shot of the new location buzzing with customers.

Why it works: Nothing sells a restaurant like a queue. It is the ultimate social proof. If people see others queuing, they want to know what all the fuss is about. This video builds FOMO and positions the Grey Street location as a must-visit destination. It also signals to anyone who has not visited the new spot yet that it is already a success.
Digital Enhancements
Beyond Social Media

These are strategic changes that would complement the social media work and strengthen your overall digital presence.

Create a TripAdvisor Listing

The new Grey Street location needs a TripAdvisor listing. This is free, takes minutes to set up, and opens up a discovery channel that brings in tourists and visitors. With the quality of the product and the enthusiasm of the customer base, Fed's would quickly build a strong review profile.

  • Claim or create the listing for 28 Grey Street
  • Add professional photos, menu, and accurate opening hours
  • Encourage early customers to leave reviews to build momentum
  • Respond to every review to build engagement

Quick win: Set up the TripAdvisor listing today. The sooner reviews start coming in, the sooner Fed's becomes discoverable to the thousands of visitors who use TripAdvisor to find food in Newcastle every week.

Add a Phone Number to All Platforms

A visible phone number on Google Business, the website, and social media profiles removes friction for potential customers. People who want to check hours, ask about the menu, or inquire about catering and events need an easy way to reach you. This is a small change that can make a real difference in converting interest into visits.

Optimise Google Business for the New Location

Make sure the Google Business profile for 28 Grey Street is fully complete: accurate hours, professional photos, menu items, phone number, and a link to the website. Google Business is often the first thing people see when they search for you. It should be as polished as the brand itself.
