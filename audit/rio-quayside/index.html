Where The Opportunity Is
Five Things Holding You Back

The restaurant, the reputation, and the consistency are all there. But the content is not converting attention into engagement, and that is limiting your reach. Here is where the biggest gaps are.

1

Almost Zero Video Content

Your feed is dominated by static images. In 2026, Instagram's algorithm overwhelmingly favours Reels and video content. A static photo of a steak gets shown to a fraction of the people who would see a 15-second video of that same steak being carved tableside with the sizzle and the steam.

Your dining concept is essentially a live performance. Gaucho chefs carving at the table, the theatre of the green and red card, cocktails being mixed, the Quayside views at sunset. All of this is video content waiting to happen. You are sitting on some of the most visually compelling restaurant footage possible and posting still photos instead.

Restaurants that switched from static to Reels-first content strategies typically see 3-5x increases in reach within the first month. For a concept as visual as yours, it could be significantly more.

2

Engagement Rate Is Well Below Industry Average

With 3,251 followers, your average post gets around 11 likes and 1 comment. That is an engagement rate of roughly 0.35%. The industry benchmark for restaurants on Instagram is 1-3%. You should be pulling 30-90 likes per post, not 5-15.

Low engagement tells the algorithm your content is not resonating, which means it shows your posts to fewer people, which drives engagement even lower. It is a downward spiral. The fix is content that triggers saves, shares, and comments, not just passive scrolling.

Your single best-performing post proves this. The UGC repost from @north_east_kids_ got 66 likes and 10 comments, outperforming your average post by 6x. That one post tells you exactly what your audience responds to: real people, real experiences, real stories. Not product shots.

3

No UGC or Influencer Strategy

Your best post by a massive margin was an influencer repost. 66 likes and 10 comments versus your average of 11 likes and 1 comment. That is not a coincidence. User-generated content and influencer partnerships consistently outperform brand-created content in hospitality because people trust other people more than they trust businesses.

Newcastle has a thriving food blogger and family influencer scene. A structured programme of inviting local creators for a complimentary Rio experience in exchange for content would generate a steady stream of authentic, high-performing posts that reach audiences you currently have no access to.

You are already getting tagged in customer posts. The gap is that you are not systematically amplifying, repurposing, or encouraging that content. Every happy table is a potential piece of content. Right now, those moments walk out the door.

4

Instagram Audience Is 3x Smaller Than Facebook

10,182 Facebook likes versus 3,251 Instagram followers. That gap is unusual for a restaurant in 2026. It suggests your Instagram growth has stalled or never had the right strategy behind it, while Facebook grew organically through check-ins and reviews.

There are thousands of people who follow you on Facebook but have never found you on Instagram. A cross-platform strategy that directs Facebook fans to your Instagram, combined with content that actually gives them a reason to follow, would close that gap quickly.

Instagram is where younger diners discover restaurants, plan nights out, and share their experiences. If someone searches "Newcastle restaurant" on Instagram and finds your competitors with more followers and more engaging content, that is a booking you have lost before they even saw your menu.

5

No Paid Social Strategy

We checked the Facebook and Instagram Ad Library. No ads running. For a restaurant with a unique concept, a strong review profile, and a "kids eat free" offer, paid social is the accelerant you are missing.

Even a modest budget targeting Newcastle locals with a 15-second Reel of meat being carved tableside and a "Kids under 10 eat free" overlay would drive bookings directly. Retargeting people who visited your website but did not book, promoting seasonal events, and reaching new audiences through lookalike targeting based on your Facebook fans would all deliver measurable ROI.

Your competitors in the Newcastle dining scene are running ads. Every impression they get is one you do not. With the right creative, your dining concept sells itself. It just needs to be seen by more people.

Ready-Made Content
5 Video Ideas You Could Film This Week

These are formats that are working right now for restaurants on Instagram and TikTok. All of them play to Rio's unique strengths as a Brazilian churrascaria with a theatrical dining concept.

Idea 01
"POV: You just sat down at Rio Quayside"
POV Experience Immersive

First-person POV from the moment you sit down. The cheese bread arrives. You flip the card to green. The first Gaucho chef approaches with a skewer of picanha. The carving. The plate filling up. Cut after cut. End with the Quayside view through the window. Trending audio. 20 seconds.

Why it works: POV restaurant content is one of the highest-performing formats on Reels and TikTok. It puts the viewer in the seat and creates an emotional response: "I need to go there." The green card flip is a unique visual hook that no other restaurant in Newcastle has. It is immediately distinctive and shareable.
Idea 02
"How much meat can one person eat at an all-you-can-eat Brazilian steakhouse?"
Challenge Entertainment Viral

Film someone attempting to try every single cut on the menu in one sitting. Keep a running count on screen. Show each new cut arriving, the reaction, the plate stacking up. End with the final tally and the person's face at the end. Light-hearted, fun, shareable.

Why it works: Challenge and food quantity content drives massive engagement. It triggers comments ("I could beat that", "No way they ate all that"), shares between friend groups planning nights out, and saves from people who want to try it themselves. It also subtly communicates the value proposition: unlimited meat for one price.
Idea 03
"My kids ate free and this is what they got"
Family Value UGC Style

A parent films their kids' reaction to the Rio experience. The cheese bread. The unlimited meat. The salad bar. Show the children's plates piling up. End with "and it was completely free." Text overlay with the offer details. Film it to feel like a genuine family video, not a polished ad.

Why it works: Your best-performing post was a family influencer talking about kids eating free. This is the same format, purpose-built for Reels. Parents share family dining content with other parents. It directly promotes your strongest offer and reaches the exact audience most likely to book: families looking for somewhere to eat this weekend.
Idea 04
"The art of the Gaucho: watch our chefs carve"
Cinematic Craft Brand

Slow motion, close-up footage of the Gaucho chefs at work. The knife sliding through picanha. The juices running. The precision of the carve. The sizzle. A cinematic, almost ASMR-style edit with minimal text. Let the visuals do the talking. End with "Rio Brazilian Steakhouse, Quayside."

Why it works: Food ASMR and craft content has exploded on social media. The slow-motion carving footage is visually hypnotic and stops people mid-scroll. It positions Rio as premium and theatrical. This type of content gets saved and shared at high rates because it is satisfying to watch repeatedly.
Idea 05
"Rio Quayside at golden hour hits different"
Lifestyle Location Aspirational

Sunset over the Tyne through the restaurant window. A cocktail being placed on the table. The warm glow of the venue interior. A Gaucho chef approaching in the background. A couple clinking glasses. Slow zoom out to reveal the full Quayside view. Trending audio. 15 seconds of pure atmosphere.

Why it works: Location and atmosphere content positions Rio as more than just a meal. It makes it a destination, an experience, a "we should go there" moment. The Quayside location is a genuine competitive advantage that most Newcastle restaurants cannot match. This content sells the evening, not just the food.
Digital Enhancements
Beyond Social Media

These are strategic changes that would make a real difference alongside the social media work.

Build a UGC Pipeline

Your single best-performing post was a repost from a family influencer. That is not a coincidence. A structured programme of inviting 2-3 Newcastle food bloggers and family creators per month for a complimentary Rio experience would generate a steady stream of authentic content.

  • Newcastle has dozens of active food and family influencers with 5K-50K engaged followers
  • Each visit generates 2-4 pieces of content that reaches an audience you currently have no access to
  • The cost is a complimentary meal. The return is content and reach worth many times more

Quick win: Invite 3 local food bloggers this month. Give them the full Rio experience. Let them create content naturally. Repost the best pieces. Watch the engagement difference versus your own brand posts.

Kids Eat Free Campaign

This is one of your strongest offers, but it is buried in generic posts that get 12 likes. A dedicated campaign with video testimonials from real families, targeted paid ads to parents within 15 miles of Newcastle, and a consistent content series around family dining at Rio would drive midweek and weekend lunch covers.

Your competitor set for family dining in Newcastle is large, but very few offer unlimited premium food for free for children. That is a remarkable offer. It deserves its own content strategy, not a single static image every few weeks.

Leverage the Multi-Venue Brand

Rio has locations in Jesmond, Quayside, and Middlesbrough. Cross-promoting between venue accounts, sharing content across locations, and building a unified brand presence would amplify reach for all three. A customer who follows Rio Jesmond but has never tried the Quayside location is a booking waiting to happen.

The APEX event you hosted, bringing 45 guests across Rio and Tomahawk, showed what is possible when you position the venues as more than just restaurants. More event content, more cross-venue promotion, more brand storytelling.
