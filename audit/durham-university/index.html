The Audit

What We Noticed

105K Instagram followers vs Newcastle's 113K. Your direct competitor, 15 miles up the A1, is ahead on the platform that matters most for brand perception. Newcastle ranks lower academically but outperforms Durham on social across the board.
TikTok is effectively a student side project. 9K followers puts Durham nowhere near the conversation. Glasgow has 80K. Newcastle has 30K. For the platform that drives prospective student decisions, Durham is invisible.
Absent from the top 10 Russell Group universities by TikTok content volume. The universities winning on TikTok are producing native, platform-first content at volume. Durham is not in that group, and prospective students notice.
Reels lean on static and carousel imagery rather than native short-form video. The Instagram grid looks polished, but the content that drives reach and engagement in 2026 is video. Static posts are not competing in the algorithm.
The campus is a visual goldmine that is massively underutilised. Durham Cathedral, the Norman Castle, the Wear loop, Palace Green, formal halls in candlelit medieval dining rooms. This is the most cinematic university campus in the country, and it barely appears in professional video content.
No professional short-form video strategy for recruitment or brand awareness. There is no visible, consistent short-form content engine driving awareness, applications, or alumni engagement. That is the gap, and it is a significant one for a top-10 institution.

The Strategy

The Opportunity

01

Campus Content That Sells

Durham Cathedral at golden hour. The castle silhouetted against a winter sky. Students in gowns crossing Palace Green. Formal halls lit by candlelight in rooms that have not changed in 500 years. The river at dawn. This campus was built for Reels and TikTok. Professional short-form video tours, seasonal content, and cinematic golden hour shots would give Durham a visual identity on social media that no other UK university could match. The raw material is extraordinary. It just needs a camera and a strategy.

02

Student Recruitment Content

Prospective students live on TikTok and Reels. That is where they form opinions, compare universities, and make decisions that shape their UCAS applications. Professional content showing campus life, city vibes, and the Durham experience reaches them where they already are. Not a prospectus PDF. Not a drone shot on a YouTube channel with 200 views. Native, platform-first content that shows what it actually feels like to be a Durham student. That is what drives applications.

03

Beat Newcastle

Your closest rival is 15 miles away and outperforming you on every social platform. Newcastle has 113K Instagram followers to your 105K. 30K TikTok followers to your 9K. They are not a better university. They are not in a more photogenic city. They simply have a more visible social media presence. A dedicated content partner changes that within three months. This is a winnable fight, and Durham has every advantage except execution.

Creative

5 Video Ideas

01

"Dawn at Durham"

Reels / TikTok

Golden hour cinematic campus walk. The cathedral emerging through morning mist. Light hitting the river. The castle in silhouette. Palace Green with no one on it. A 60-second piece that makes every prospective student stop scrolling and think: I need to go there.

02

"Formal Hall"

Reels / TikTok

The dining experience that no other UK university can match. Gowns, candlelight, medieval halls, Latin grace. POV content that shows what it actually looks like to sit down for formal hall at Durham. This is the content that goes viral because nowhere else in British higher education offers anything like it.

03

"A Day at Durham"

Reels / TikTok

Student perspective, start to finish. Morning coffee on the Bailey. Lectures in a building older than most countries. Library session with the cathedral visible through the window. College bar in the evening. The daily rhythm of Durham student life, shot natively for the platform.

04

"The City"

Reels / TikTok

Durham beyond the university. The independent coffee shops. The river walks. The market hall. The viaduct. The view from the train as you pull into the station. A love letter to the city itself, positioning Durham as a place to live, not just study.

05

"Why Durham?"

TikTok / Reels

Quick-fire student testimonials. 15 seconds each. Authentic and unscripted. Real students answering one question: why did you choose Durham? No rehearsal. No script. Just genuine answers from people who picked this university and would do it again. The content that builds trust with applicants more than any open day ever could.
