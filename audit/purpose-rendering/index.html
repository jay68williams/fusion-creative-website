You already have the reputation, the experience and the work. These are the five areas we would focus on.

1

Film Every Before and After

This is the whole game for a rendering business. A tired, cracked, pebble-dashed house turning into a crisp, smooth, freshly rendered home is one of the most shareable things on social. A quick clip of the property before, a few seconds of the work, then the big reveal, that format alone can carry your entire account. The transformation sells itself, no script needed.

You complete these jobs constantly. Capturing each one is two minutes of filming on a phone, and it builds a library of proof that runs for years.

2

Lean Into the Satisfying Process

The internet loves watching skilled trade work. The float gliding across wet render, pebble dash being stripped back, a wall going from rough to glass-smooth, this is the "oddly satisfying" content that racks up views and rewatches. Sound up, close in, no talking needed. It shows your skill and it holds attention, which is exactly what the algorithm rewards.

Most local renderers never film this. Whoever owns the satisfying-process content in this area owns the attention, and right now that space is wide open.

3

Start an Instagram and TikTok From Scratch

There is no Instagram or TikTok for Purpose Rendering yet, and that is an opportunity, not a problem. These are the two platforms where transformation and trade content travels furthest, and almost no renderer in the North East is using them properly. You would be early, in your own backyard, with the best raw material going.

We build and run both from day one, so it is done right, posted consistently, and tuned to the local homeowners who actually book jobs, not just a follower count for the sake of it.

4

Put Your Reviews and Story on Camera

170 Checkatrade reviews is a goldmine you are barely using. Real, on-site reactions from happy customers standing in front of their newly rendered home are worth more than any advert. Pair that with the "family run, 25 years" story and the staged-payment reassurance, and you answer the exact worries every homeowner has before they pick up the phone.

Trust is the deciding factor in trades. You already have it in spades, this just makes it visible to people before they are even looking.

Ready-Made Content
4 Video Ideas You Could Film on Your Next Job

These are formats working right now on TikTok and Instagram Reels, built around what you already do every day: the transformation, the process, the proof. Each one could be filmed on a phone on site, no extra time off the tools.

Idea 01
"This house hadn't been touched in 30 years"
Before / AfterTransformationReveal

The classic, and the best. A few seconds of the tired, cracked, pebble-dashed house, a quick montage of the render going on, then the full reveal of the finished home. Strong hook on frame one. Trending audio underneath. Under 25 seconds.

Why it works: Transformation is the most shareable content there is, and a render before-and-after is about as dramatic as it gets. It sells the result with zero words.
Idea 02
"Tell me this isn't the most satisfying job going"
ASMRSatisfyingProcess

Close and slow: the float gliding across wet render, the smooth finish appearing behind it, the sound of the tools. No talking, sound up, hook text on screen ("wait for the finish"). End on the perfect smooth wall. Under 20 seconds.

Why it works: Satisfying trade content gets enormous reach and rewatches. It quietly shows off your skill while holding attention, which is exactly what the For You page rewards.
Idea 03
"Ripping 30 years of pebble dash off this house"
SatisfyingBeforeProcess

Pebble dash removal is its own genre of satisfying. The dated coating coming away in sheets, the clean wall underneath, the sense of a fresh start. Lead with the worst-looking corner, end with it stripped back and ready. Punchy and fast.

Why it works: It hooks on the "ugly to clean" instinct people cannot scroll past, and it speaks directly to the exact homeowners googling "pebble dash removal near me".
Idea 04
"The customer's reaction said it all"
ReactionTrustLocal

A quick clip of a happy customer seeing their finished home for the first time, or a few words to camera on why they chose you. Cut it with the before-and-after of their house. Tag the local area. Real people, real results.

Why it works: It turns your 170 reviews into something people feel. Genuine local reactions are the most persuasive content a trades business can post, and they build trust before anyone calls.
