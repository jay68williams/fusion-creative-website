Where The Opportunity Is
Three Areas With Real Growth Potential

You are already in an incredibly strong position. These are the areas where focused effort could take Circo Italian even further.

1

TripAdvisor Listing Needs Claiming and Fixing

This is the most important finding in the entire audit. Your Google rating is 4.7 but your TripAdvisor is showing 3.4 with only 20 reviews. That is a massive mismatch that does not reflect the reality of the restaurant at all. It appears the TripAdvisor listing may be miscategorised under Gosforth rather than your actual Quorum Business Park location, which could be causing confusion and attracting reviews from the wrong context.

When someone checks TripAdvisor before visiting and sees 3.4 with 20 reviews versus your 4.7 on Google, it creates doubt. Some potential customers will choose a competitor based on that TripAdvisor score alone, even though it does not accurately represent Circo Italian. Claiming and fixing this listing, getting the location correct, and generating a wave of genuine reviews from happy customers could transform this score within a few months. This is the single biggest quick win available to you right now.

2

Suburban Location Needs "Worth the Trip" Content

Quorum Business Park is not a typical restaurant destination. That is not a weakness, but it means your content strategy needs to actively address the question every potential customer asks: "Is it worth the drive?" The answer is clearly yes, given your 4.7 Google rating and 22,000 followers, but you need content that makes that case explicitly.

"Worth the drive" content is a proven format on TikTok and Instagram. It acknowledges the location head-on and then blows viewers away with the food, the space, and the experience. It turns what could be perceived as a disadvantage into a selling point. "You would not expect this in a business park" is exactly the kind of surprise that drives viral content and gets people tagging friends with "We need to go here."

3

No Email Marketing or Loyalty Programme

With 22,000 Instagram followers and a clearly loyal customer base, you have an audience that would respond incredibly well to email marketing and a loyalty programme. Right now, if Instagram changes its algorithm tomorrow and your posts reach fewer people, you have no backup channel to reach your customers directly.

An email list gives you a direct line to your most loyal customers. Birthday offers, new menu announcements, Sunday roast pre-orders, exclusive events. A loyalty programme incentivises repeat visits and increases average spend. For a suburban location where repeat customers are the backbone of the business, these tools would drive consistent revenue that does not depend on any social media algorithm.

Ready-Made Content
5 Video Ideas You Could Film Today

These are formats that are working right now for restaurants on TikTok and Instagram. Every single one plays to Circo Italian's unique strengths.

Idea 01
"Watch our open pizza oven in action"
Theatre Process Viral Format

The full pizza journey. Dough being stretched by hand. Toppings being placed. The pizza sliding into the open oven. Flames licking around the edges. The pizza spinning. Coming out perfectly charred and bubbling. Being cut and served. Close-up of the first slice being pulled with the cheese stretching. No voiceover. Just the sounds of the fire, the kitchen, and the food.

Why it works: Open pizza oven content is some of the highest-performing food content on the internet. The fire, the skill, the transformation from raw dough to finished pizza is genuinely mesmerising. It triggers both ASMR and food craving responses. This format drives massive saves and shares because people want to show their friends where they are eating next.
Idea 02
"The giant chicken parm you have to see to believe"
Reveal Wow Factor Shareable

Start with a regular-looking plate arriving at the table. Then reveal the giant chicken parm in all its glory. Show the scale compared to the plate, the table, the person eating it. Cut through it to show the inside. The cheese pull. The reaction of the person seeing it for the first time. End with "Circo Italian. Quorum Business Park. You are welcome."

Why it works: Oversized food reveals are one of the most consistently viral formats on social media. The surprise factor drives comments, shares, and tags. People cannot resist tagging friends with "We are going here." For Circo Italian, the giant chicken parm is a signature item that differentiates you from every other Italian restaurant in the area. One video of this could reach hundreds of thousands of people.
Idea 03
"48 hours for one pizza dough"
Process Time-Lapse Craft

A time-lapse showing the 48-hour dough proofing process from start to finish. The initial mix. The first rise. The fold. The second rise. The overnight rest. The final shaping. Then the pizza being made and going into the oven. Text overlay showing the hours passing. "48 hours of patience for 90 seconds of perfection."

Why it works: Process and craft content positions Circo Italian as a place that takes food seriously. It differentiates you from every pizza chain and quick-service restaurant. The time-lapse format is inherently satisfying and the "48 hours for one pizza" hook is the kind of fact that makes people stop scrolling. It tells a story of quality and dedication that justifies the trip to Quorum.
Idea 04
"You would never expect this in a business park"
Worth the Drive Surprise Location

Open with a wide shot of the business park exterior. Boring, corporate, nothing special. Then walk through the door into Circo Italian. The transformation. The open pizza oven. The food arriving at tables. Close-ups of the most stunning dishes. The buzzing atmosphere. The contrast between the outside and the inside is the entire hook. "Worth the drive. Every single time."

Why it works: Expectation-versus-reality content is one of the most shared formats on social media. The contrast between a business park and an exceptional restaurant creates genuine surprise. It directly addresses the one barrier potential customers have ("it is in a business park, why would I go there?") and demolishes it in 30 seconds. This video would reach people who have never considered visiting because they did not know what was inside.
Idea 05
"Our Sunday roast spread"
Sunday Roast British Classic Weekend

A beautifully shot Sunday roast sequence. The meat being carved. Yorkshire puddings coming out golden. Roast potatoes being plated. Gravy being poured. The full spread hitting the table. The first forkful. Warm lighting, cosy vibes, a Sunday afternoon feel. "Sunday sorted. 12 til 5. Circo Italian."

Why it works: Sunday roast content performs exceptionally well in the UK market, especially on Saturdays and Sundays when people are deciding where to eat. A well-shot roast creates immediate cravings and drives same-day bookings. It also broadens your audience beyond pizza lovers to include everyone looking for a quality Sunday lunch, which is a massive market.

TripAdvisor Listing Audit and Recovery

This is the single most important quick win. Your TripAdvisor shows 3.4 with 20 reviews while Google shows 4.7. That gap is almost certainly hurting you. The listing may be miscategorised under Gosforth, which needs correcting.

  • Claim and verify the TripAdvisor listing if not already done
