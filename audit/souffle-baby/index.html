Where The Opportunity Is
Five Areas With Real Potential

The product, the niche, and the Instagram community are all strong. You have built something that people love. These are the areas where a focused push could take things significantly further.

1

TripAdvisor Needs Immediate Attention

Your TripAdvisor listing currently shows 2.5 out of 5 from just 2 reviews. This does not reflect the reality of your business at all. With over 7,000 Instagram followers and a loyal customer base, the actual customer satisfaction is clearly much higher than what TripAdvisor suggests. The problem is simply that only 2 people have left reviews, and the sample size is far too small to be representative.

This is one of the most urgent things to address because TripAdvisor is a major discovery platform. When someone searches "cafes in Newcastle" or "desserts Newcastle," a 2.5 rating can turn potential customers away before they even consider visiting. The fix is straightforward: encourage your happy customers to leave reviews. A QR code at the counter, a gentle prompt from staff, a small card with the bill. Within a couple of months, you could have 30 to 50 reviews that accurately reflect the quality of what you are serving, and the rating would shift dramatically.

2

No Website Is Limiting Your Discoverability

When someone Googles "souffle pancakes Newcastle" or "Japanese pancakes near me," you need a website to capture that search traffic. Right now, without a website, you are relying on Instagram and delivery apps to be your digital storefront. A simple, beautiful website with your menu, your story, location and hours, delivery links, and your Instagram feed would give you a permanent home on Google and build credibility with new customers.

For a unique concept like souffle pancakes, a website is also an opportunity to educate people who have never tried them before. A page explaining what souffle pancakes are, how they are made, and why they are different would capture search traffic from curious people and convert them into customers.

3

TikTok Is Massively Underused

With 1,329 followers and 14K likes, your TikTok account has shown early signs of traction but has not yet had its breakout moment. This is a huge missed opportunity because souffle pancakes are one of the most viral food categories on TikTok globally. The jiggle, the fluffiness, the toppings, the cutting moment. These are exactly the formats that generate millions of views.

Japanese souffle pancake videos regularly hit millions of views on TikTok worldwide. The format is proven. The audience is there. You just need to post consistently with high-quality, short-form video content that showcases the product in all its jiggly glory. A consistent schedule of 4 to 5 videos per week could transform your TikTok from early stage to a genuine growth engine within weeks.

4

Build a Content Engine Around the Product

Your product is inherently visual and shareable. Every pancake that comes out of the kitchen is a potential piece of content. But turning that potential into actual growth requires a deliberate content strategy: planned shoots, consistent posting schedules, trending audio, hashtag strategy, and community engagement. Right now, the content is not matching the potential of the product.

A structured approach to content creation, where you film multiple pieces of content during each service and schedule them across the week, would keep your feeds active and your audience growing. The content is already sitting in your kitchen. It just needs to be captured and distributed strategically.

5

Promote the Dietary-Inclusive Angle

Having a gluten-free and lactose-free base is a genuine differentiator that many customers are actively searching for. This should be a major part of your marketing, not just a small note on the menu. Content like "Did you know our souffle pancakes can be made gluten-free?" or "Lactose-free Japanese pancakes in Newcastle" would reach an audience that struggles to find desserts they can enjoy.

The dietary-inclusive community is highly engaged on social media. When they find a place that caters to them, they share it enthusiastically with others in the same situation. One well-targeted video about your gluten-free option could reach thousands of people who have never heard of Souffle Baby but have been looking for exactly what you offer.

Ready-Made Content
5 Video Ideas You Could Film Today

These are formats that are working right now for dessert cafes and Japanese food content on TikTok and Instagram. All of them play to Souffle Baby's strengths as the only souffle pancake cafe in Newcastle.

Idea 01
"The jiggliest pancakes in Newcastle"
Satisfying ASMR Hero Content

Close-up on a perfectly cooked souffle pancake. Gently tap the plate or the pancake itself. Watch it jiggle. Slow motion replay. Different angles. Stack them up. Tap again. The wobble is mesmerising. No voiceover needed. Just the visual and a trending audio track. End with the full plate, beautifully topped.

Why it works: Jiggly souffle pancake videos are one of the most viewed food formats on TikTok globally. The satisfying wobble triggers replays, shares, and saves. This single format could be your most consistent content pillar and has the highest probability of going viral. People cannot stop watching them.
Idea 02
"What are souffle pancakes? Let me show you."
Educational Discovery Approachable

A quick, friendly explainer for people who have never tried souffle pancakes. "They are not like normal pancakes. They are fluffy, jiggly, and made with whipped egg whites." Show the process. Show the result. Show someone tasting one for the first time. Make it welcoming and exciting. "You can only get them at Souffle Baby in Newcastle."

Why it works: Many people in Newcastle have never heard of souffle pancakes. Educational content removes the unfamiliarity and turns curiosity into visits. It also gets saved and shared at high rates, which the algorithm rewards with more reach. Every person who learns what souffle pancakes are from your video is a potential customer.
Idea 03
"Every flavour on our menu, ranked"
Menu Showcase Opinion Engagement

Walk through every souffle pancake variant on the menu. Show each one. Give a quick opinion or description. Rank them from good to best. Make it personal and fun. End with "What is your favourite? Comment below." This drives massive engagement because everyone has an opinion and wants to share it.

Why it works: Ranking content is one of the most engaging formats on social media because it invites debate. People comment to agree or disagree, which drives the algorithm. It also showcases your full menu in a single video, which helps customers decide what to order. Comments and shares equal reach.
Idea 04
"POV: You have never had a souffle pancake before"
First Timer Reaction Relatable

Film a first-time customer's genuine reaction. The anticipation as it arrives. The surprise at how tall and fluffy it is. The first cut. The first bite. Their face says everything. Multiple first-timers in a compilation. Real people, real reactions. "This is what happens when you try souffle pancakes for the first time."

Why it works: Genuine reactions are incredibly powerful social proof. When people see others being surprised and delighted by your product, they want to experience it themselves. Reaction content is also highly shareable because people tag friends with "we need to try this." It turns viewers into customers through pure emotion.
Idea 05
"Gluten-free and it tastes THIS good?"
Dietary Inclusive Niche Audience

Highlight the gluten-free and lactose-free options. Show the pancake being made. Show it looking just as incredible as the regular version. Someone with dietary requirements trying it and being genuinely impressed. "You do not have to miss out. Souffle Baby does gluten-free and lactose-free." The message is inclusive and welcoming.

Why it works: The gluten-free and dairy-free community is highly engaged online. When they find a dessert spot that caters to them, they share it with everyone they know. This single video could reach thousands of people who have been searching for exactly this. It also positions Souffle Baby as a thoughtful, inclusive brand.
Digital Enhancements
Beyond Social Media

These are strategic changes that would make a real difference alongside the social media work.

Fix TripAdvisor Urgently

A 2.5 rating from 2 reviews does not reflect your actual customer satisfaction. This is the single most urgent thing to address because it is actively turning potential customers away. The fix is straightforward and fast:

  • Place a QR code at the counter and on tables linking directly to your TripAdvisor review page
  • Train staff to ask happy customers: "If you enjoyed it, we would love a TripAdvisor review"
  • Add a gentle prompt on receipts or packaging for delivery orders
  • Target: 30+ positive reviews within 2 months, which would dramatically shift the rating

Why this is urgent: Every day that TripAdvisor shows 2.5 stars is a day where potential customers are being put off before they ever try your pancakes. Your 7,000 Instagram followers prove the real story. TripAdvisor just needs to catch up.

Build a Simple, Beautiful Website

A website for Souffle Baby should do three things: showcase the product with stunning visuals, explain what souffle pancakes are for people who have never tried them, and make it easy to find you or order delivery.

  • Full menu with photos of every pancake variant
  • "What are souffle pancakes?" section for newcomers
  • Location, hours, and direct links to Deliveroo and Just Eat
  • Gluten-free and lactose-free options highlighted prominently
  • Your Instagram feed embedded to show real customer content

Google Business Profile Optimisation

Make sure your Google Business Profile has professional food photography, accurate hours, your full menu, and regular posts about new toppings or seasonal specials. Respond to every review. Post weekly updates. When someone searches "desserts Newcastle" or "pancakes near me," your Google listing is often the first thing they see. It needs to look as good as your pancakes.
