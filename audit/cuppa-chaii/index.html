Where The Opportunity Is
Five Things to Nail for the Newcastle Launch

You are joining a brand that already has the product, the trends instinct and the momentum. For your Newcastle store, the job is local: launch loud, build a Newcastle audience, and fill the place from day one. These are the five areas we would focus on.

1

Make the Launch a Citywide Moment

The brand's Purley opening reel did around 8,600 views, which proves the format works. "Newcastle's first Cuppa' Chaii" is a hook in itself. We would build the launch as a proper moment: a countdown series in the run-up, the fit-out reveal, the queue down the street, the first karak pour, and real opening-day reactions, all filmed in person.

A launch only happens once. Done right, it is the single biggest piece of content the store will ever make, and it sets the tone for everything after.

2

Build a Local Newcastle Audience

The main @cuppachaiiuk account is national. Your store needs reach with the people who will actually walk through the door: Newcastle locals, the student crowd, the desi community, the city's food lovers. That means Newcastle location tags, local food creators, and content that feels like it belongs to this city, not a generic brand feed.

A few thousand engaged Newcastle followers is worth far more to your store than a national number that never visits. Local is the whole game for a single site.

3

Lead With the Pour, the Reaction and the Heritage

"They said it's just chaii. I said you've never had Cuppa' Chaii." That line is perfect. The chaii pour is pure sensory content: the steam, the stretch, the karak. Pair that with real reactions, first sips, and the Karachi dhaba story, and you have the most shareable content in the category.

Reaction plus heritage plus a satisfying pour is exactly what the For You page rewards, and it sells the experience far better than a flat product shot.

4

Own Newcastle's Moments

Newcastle has a huge student population and a strong late-night culture, and you are open until midnight, which is gold. Tie the content to what the city is actually doing: the uni crowd after lectures, late-night catch-ups, matchdays, Ramadan and Eid, the games and community nights. Content that plugs into local life gets shared locally.

This is where a single store can punch well above a national brand account, because it can be genuinely, specifically Newcastle.

5

Local Ads to Fill It From Day One

A launch has a fixed date and a fixed catchment, which makes paid ads almost a sure thing. A small, well-targeted budget around the Newcastle area in the run-up puts your best opening reel in front of everyone nearby, so the doors open to a queue, not an empty room.

Then keep a modest local budget running behind the best content, so the store stays front of mind across the city. For a new site, this is the fastest way to build footfall.

Digital Enhancements
Beyond the Main Feed

A few extra wins that would complement the content work for a brand expanding this fast.

Scale TikTok and Shorts

TikTok is where a brand like Cuppa' Chaii can break out nationally, and it rewards exactly the reaction and trend content you already make. Every reel should be cut for TikTok and YouTube Shorts too, for free extra reach.

  • Post the reaction, pour and trend content to TikTok consistently, not just Instagram
  • Cross-post every reel to TikTok and YouTube Shorts
  • Use city and location tags so each post reaches the right local audience
  • Lean into the formats that over-perform on TikTok: ASMR, reaction, trends

A Simple Content System Across Every Store

With nine sites and growing, the footage is everywhere, it just needs capturing. A simple shot-list and posting system means every location knows what to film, and we turn it into polished brand content.

  • A quick what-to-film guide for each store, so nothing is missed
  • The brand account carries hero content; locations feed in local moments
  • A repeatable opening series for every new franchise store
  • Monthly reporting on reach, growth and best-performing content

Run Ads Around Every Launch

Because you open stores on fixed dates, paid ads are a near-guaranteed win: put your best opening reel in front of everyone nearby in the run-up, and the store opens to a queue.

  • Target the local area around each new site in the weeks before opening
  • Use your best opening and reaction reels as the ads
  • Retarget anyone who engages but does not visit
  • Track cost per visit so the spend is always accountable

Why it fits Cuppa' Chaii: For a franchise opening this often, a launch always has a fixed date and a fixed catchment. That makes it the easiest, highest-return ad spend there is.
