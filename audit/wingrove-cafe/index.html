What Is Already Working Well
Credit Where It Is Due

✓ Authentic South Indian is genuinely rare in Newcastle

Newcastle has plenty of North Indian and Bangladeshi restaurants. Authentic South Indian, with proper masala dosas, idli, vada, sambhar, biryani done the right way, is rare. That niche is yours to own. There is no real competition for "South Indian Newcastle" right now and that is a gap most restaurants would dream of having.

✓ The £16 unlimited weekend buffet is a viral hook waiting to happen

Unlimited authentic South Indian for £16 is one of the best food deals in the city. People love value-reveal content. Buffet content performs incredibly well on TikTok and Reels. Right now this offer is barely visible online. Once it is, it has the potential to fill the cafe every Saturday and Sunday on its own.

✓ Family run is a story most chains cannot copy

You and your dad running it together is the kind of brand story that resonates hard on social media right now. Father and daughter, real recipes, real people in the kitchen. Independent food businesses with a clear family story are some of the most followed accounts on UK food TikTok. The story is already there. It just needs to be told.

✓ Multi-platform delivery presence is solid

Listed on Uber Eats (4.2), Just Eat, Deliveroo, FoodHub, and Too Good To Go. That is a strong foundation. The "South Indian Surprise Bag" on Too Good To Go is a great hook for content too. Most cafes in Newcastle would love to have your delivery footprint. The discovery problem is on social, not on the platforms.

✓ The Fenham location is full of the right audience

Westgate Road and Fenham have a huge South Asian community, a big student population from Newcastle and Northumbria universities just up the road, and a busy multicultural high street. The audience that loves authentic South Indian food is already living and walking past the cafe every day. They just need to know it is there.

✓ Halal, dog friendly, and the only restaurant on Westgate Road with parking

Three major differentiators that almost nobody finding the cafe online actually knows about. Halal opens up a whole audience that currently assumes it is not. Dog friendly gives you weekend brunch families and dog walkers. Hundreds of parking spaces on Westgate Road, where parking is genuinely a problem, is a massive convenience hook. None of this is communicated anywhere obvious online and that is fixable in week one.

Where The Opportunity Is
Five Areas With Real Potential

Wingrove has a great product, a strong unique angle, and a location surrounded by the perfect audience. These are the five areas where focused effort would turn a quietly loved cafe into a Newcastle name people travel for.

1

The £16 Weekend Buffet Is the Single Biggest Untapped Hook

Unlimited authentic South Indian for £16 is one of the best food deals in Newcastle and it is currently invisible online. There is no Reel showing the spread, no TikTok showing what you actually get for £16, no weekly content series built around it. The single most under-used asset in the entire business.

Buffet reveal content is one of the highest-converting formats in food TikTok globally. A weekly "Saturday at Wingrove" reel showing the full spread, the dosa station live, the curries arriving, the price reveal at the end, would build a habit with the audience and a directly trackable lift in weekend covers within 60 days. This is the format we would lead with from week one.

2

Instagram and TikTok Are Both Effectively Empty

@dosasatwingrovecafe sits at around 46 followers with a handful of static menu posts. There is no TikTok account at all. For a cafe that has been trading for 4+ years with a genuinely authentic product, that is leaving the entire short-form discovery layer untouched. Every time someone in Newcastle searches "best dosa near me" or "South Indian Newcastle" on TikTok, you do not come up.

The good news is starting from a tiny base means the growth curve is wide open. Indian food, particularly dosa content, is one of the most viral categories on TikTok right now. Dosa pour videos regularly hit hundreds of thousands of views. With consistent posting and the right format, going from 46 followers to 5k+ is realistic inside 3 months. Going from 5k to 25k is realistic inside 12 months if the content stays disciplined.

3

The Father-Daughter Family Story Is Not in the Content

You and your dad running Wingrove together is the kind of story food TikTok eats up. The audience loves family-run, real recipes, real people behind the food. Right now the social profile is product photos. There is no founder content, no kitchen footage of your dad cooking, no story of how the cafe started, no behind-the-scenes.

A "meet the family behind Wingrove" series would humanise the brand instantly. Once people know your faces and your story, they are not just ordering a dosa, they are supporting Navya and her dad. That is the difference between a one-off customer and someone who comes back every other week and brings their friends. This is one of the easiest content lanes to fill and one of the most effective long-term.

4

The Brand Identity Is Split Across Three Names

The Instagram handle is @dosasatwingrovecafe, the Google listing says Wingrove Cafe, the website says Wingrove Cafe. From a content and SEO point of view, this is causing confusion. People searching for "Wingrove Cafe Instagram" are not finding the actual account. Followers from one platform are not flowing to the other. Every piece of content is fighting against the brand fragmentation.

Tightening the handle to a single consistent name across Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, and the website would close that gap immediately. It also makes new content discoverable to the existing customer base, who currently search the wrong name and assume the cafe is not on social. Easy fix, big payoff.

5

The Student and South Asian Audience Around You Is Untapped

Fenham and the West End sit between Newcastle University, Northumbria, and a large established South Asian community. That is three audiences that all over-index on TikTok and Instagram and all care deeply about authentic, well-priced food. None of them currently know about Wingrove unless they have walked past or had a friend recommend it.

Geo-targeted content on Reels and TikTok, plus collabs with Newcastle student food creators and local food review accounts, could put the cafe directly in front of tens of thousands of locals every week. The ad spend needed to do this paid would be huge. Done right organically through content, the same reach is essentially free.

Ready-Made Content
4 Video Ideas You Could Film This Week

These are formats working right now on TikTok and Instagram Reels, tailored specifically to Wingrove's dosas, the £16 weekend buffet, and the father-daughter story. Even before we officially start, any one of these could be filmed on a phone and posted this weekend.

Idea 01
"£16 unlimited South Indian buffet in Newcastle, here is everything you get"
Value Reveal Buffet Hero Format

Open with a wide overhead shot of an empty plate. Cut to the buffet line at Wingrove. Dosas hitting the pan. Curries bubbling. Rice and biryani steaming. Plate fills up one item at a time, voiceover or text overlay naming each dish. Cut to a close-up of the £16 price. End on a hand reaching in. Trending audio. Under 30 seconds.

Why it works: Value-reveal buffet content is one of the most consistently viral formats in food TikTok globally. The hook "£16 unlimited" stops the scroll instantly. This is the format that does the heavy lifting for filling Saturday and Sunday covers and it should be the first reel posted.
Idea 02
"How a proper masala dosa is made, start to finish"
Process ASMR Signature

Close-up on the dosa batter hitting the hot tawa. The spread, the swirl, the sizzle. Ghee or oil drizzled around the edge. Crispy edges forming. Spiced potato filling spooned in. The fold. The final lift onto the plate. Sambhar and chutneys landing alongside. Pure ASMR audio or trending sound. Under 25 seconds.

Why it works: Dosa-making is one of the most reliably viral process formats in food TikTok. The pour, the swirl, the crisp, the fold. Globally it has billions of views. Owning that format locally puts Wingrove in front of every Newcastle and North East feed that has ever liked Indian food content.
Idea 03
"Father and daughter built Newcastle's only proper South Indian cafe"
Family Story Founder Emotional

You, Navya, on camera in the cafe. Your dad cooking in the background. Why South Indian. Why Newcastle. What the cafe means to your family. Cut between your words and shots of him at the dosa pan, your hands plating up, the regulars laughing in the dining room. Keep it warm and honest. 45-60 seconds. Text overlay: "Our family. Our food." Emotional trending audio.

Why it works: People follow people, not menus. A father-daughter story is one of the most emotionally engaging formats in food TikTok. Once your audience knows the story, they are not just customers, they are fans. This is the reel that creates regulars rather than one-off visitors.
Idea 04
"Newcastle students, a £16 weekend buffet just opened on your doorstep"
Student Geo-Targeted Group

Quick, punchy. Open with a hook on screen "If you study in Newcastle and you have not been here yet". Pan around the dining room. Cut to the buffet spread. Group of students sitting down, plates piled. Close-up of the £16 price. End with a map pin to The Beacon, Westgate Road. Fast cuts. Trending student-friendly audio.

Why it works: Students share content like this constantly. One viral reel showing a £16 unlimited buffet 10 minutes from campus could fill the cafe every Saturday for a term. Combined with collabs with Newcastle student food accounts, the local geo audience is the easiest first 5,000 followers to acquire.
Digital Enhancements
Beyond Social Media

A few extra digital wins that would complement the content work.

Tighten the Brand Handle Across All Platforms

Currently the brand sits across Wingrove Cafe (Google, website, Facebook) and @dosasatwingrovecafe (Instagram). Picking one and aligning everything would make the cafe far easier to find and follow.

  • Pick one consistent handle across Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, website
  • Update the bio with the £16 buffet front and centre
  • Add Reserve, Order, Call, Directions action buttons on Instagram (business account)
  • Add a Linktree-style bio link covering buffet booking, menu, and delivery platforms

Quick win: Brand consistency directly improves discoverability and search ranking. A 30-minute fix that compounds with every piece of content posted afterwards.

Launch TikTok and Claim "South Indian Newcastle"

There is no Indian restaurant in the West End currently dominating TikTok. A consistent posting cadence of dosa pour videos, buffet reveals, and the family story would put Wingrove front and centre when anyone in the region searches "Indian Newcastle" or "best dosa near me". The earlier we move, the harder it is for anyone to catch up later.

  • Set up TikTok account with consistent handle
  • Post 3-4 short videos per week covering buffet, dosas, family, and student angles
  • Use Newcastle, Fenham, and West End location tags on every post
  • Cross-post the strongest TikToks straight to Instagram Reels

Build a Weekend Buffet Booking Funnel

Right now, the £16 buffet is the strongest hook in the business but there is no clean way for someone seeing a viral reel to actually book a table for it. Adding a simple booking flow, an Instagram action button, and a dedicated landing page for the buffet would make sure that when content lands, the conversions follow.

The opportunity: A viral reel that drives 50,000 views needs somewhere for those viewers to land. A £16 buffet booking page with photos, the spread, opening hours, and a Reserve button would convert that traffic instead of letting it bounce.
