The one thing I would focus on

You are clearly serious about content, you are hiring for it. So here is the thing I would lean into. Your food is some of the most filmable in the city, but the format that actually travels is the hook-first, reaction-led reel: the syringe going in, the first bite, the basket reveal, real faces. That, across four branches, is a content goldmine. The job is not making it look good, you already do. It is making it stop the scroll.

A few ideas I would love to try

Film the theatre, not the plate.The gol gappa syringe, the fish baskets, the kulhad, the smoke. Your dishes have a moment built in. Lead with that moment in the first second, then let the food do the rest.
Reactions and first bites.Film real diners on the first gol gappa, the heat hitting, the "oh my god". Reaction is the most shareable thing in food content, and your food gets a genuine reaction every time. It is free to capture and it sells the experience.
Lean into trends.Indian food is having a huge moment on TikTok and Reels. Plug your dishes into trending formats and sounds while they are hot. It is the fastest, cheapest way to land in front of people who have never been in, and it is most of how we grow other Newcastle food brands.
The story and the people.The Delhi street food story, the journey from one site to four, the team behind the pass. People follow people, and an award-winning restaurant with a story this good should be telling it on camera.
Four branches, four times the content.Newcastle, Sunderland, Leicester and Leeds is not four times the work, it is four times the raw material. The brand account carries the hero reels, each city feeds in local moments, all pointing back to the same booking link.
