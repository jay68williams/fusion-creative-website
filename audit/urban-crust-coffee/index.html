Where The Opportunity Is
Five Things We Would Focus On

You have the product, the location and the timing. The job is local: get Urban Crust in front of Stockton, become the coffee shop people already know about, and turn that into a steady stream of customers through the door. These are the five areas we would focus on.

1

Make "New in Stockton" the Moment

A new opening only happens once, and "Stockton just got a new coffee shop" is a hook in itself. We would build it into a proper local launch: the reveal, the first coffees, the fit-out, real reactions, all filmed in person and pushed to people right here in town.

Done well, this is the single biggest piece of content you will ever make, and it sets the tone for everything after.

2

The Coffee and Bake Content Engine

This is the answer to "I do not know what to post". The pour, the latte art, the steam, the bakes out of the oven, the first bite. Sound up, close in, no talking needed. It is the most satisfying, rewatchable content in the category, and you produce it every single day without trying.

We turn your counter into a steady stream of videos built to travel, so the not-knowing-what-to-post problem disappears for good.

3

Own Local Discovery in Stockton

Your customers live within a few miles. That means Stockton location tags, local food pages, and content that plugs into the town, so when someone searches or scrolls for "coffee in Stockton" or a new spot to try, you are the name that comes up. A few thousand engaged locals is worth far more to you than a big national number that never visits.

Local is the whole game for a single cafe, and being early gives you a real head start.

4

Put You and the Story Front and Centre

People back independents, and they back people. Your story, why you opened, the early mornings, the regulars, the human side of running a new cafe, is exactly what makes a local following feel personal. It turns Urban Crust from "a coffee shop" into "Ery's coffee shop", the one people are rooting for.

That connection is what turns a first visit into a regular, and a regular into someone who brings their friends.

5

Get Findable: Website, Google and Local Ads

Right now you have no website, which means when someone googles you or looks for coffee nearby, there is little to find. A simple site and a proper Google profile fix that fast, so you show up on the map at the exact moment someone is deciding where to go. Pair that with a small, tightly targeted local ad budget and you put your best content in front of Stockton directly.

For "more customers", being easy to find and constantly in front of locals is the most direct route there is.

Ready-Made Content
4 Video Ideas You Could Film This Week

These are formats working right now on TikTok and Instagram Reels, built around what is already on your counter. Each one could be filmed on a phone behind the bar, no extra time off the floor.

Idea 01
"The perfect flat white, start to finish"
ASMRSatisfyingCoffee

Close and slow: the grind, the tamp, the pour, the milk stretch, the latte art landing. Sound up, no talking, a hook on frame one ("the most satisfying flat white in Stockton"). Trending audio. Under 20 seconds.

Why it works: The coffee ritual is pure sensory content, endlessly rewatchable, and it sells the quality of your product without a single word.
Idea 02
"Stockton, you have a new coffee shop"
LocalDiscoveryPOV

A quick, warm tour: the door, the counter, the bakes, a coffee being made, the seat by the window. Lead with the hook "POV: you just found your new favourite spot in Stockton". Tag the location. Made for locals to send to a mate.

Why it works: People love discovering and sharing a new local spot, and "new in town" is a built-in reason to watch and tag a friend.
Idea 03
"Fresh bakes, straight out the oven"
FoodSatisfyingMorning

The morning bake: the tray coming out, the steam, the pull-apart, the first one with a coffee. Warm, simple, mouth-watering. Hook it on "this is what 7am at Urban Crust looks like". Under 25 seconds.

Why it works: Fresh food content is some of the most shared on the feed, and it gives people a specific reason and a specific time to come in.
Idea 04
"Why I opened a coffee shop"
StoryOwnerLocal

You to camera, honest and short: why you opened Urban Crust, what you want it to be for Stockton, the early starts and the bit you love. Cut with shots of the cafe and the coffee. Real, human, easy to root for.

Why it works: People back independents and the person behind them. Your story turns a new cafe into one the town wants to see succeed.
