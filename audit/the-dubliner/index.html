Where The Opportunity Is
Five Areas With Real Potential

The Dubliner already punches above its weight. These are the areas where small changes would unlock a lot more reach, more bookings, and more regulars through the door.

1

Instagram Is Lagging Behind TikTok

You have 138K on TikTok and 35K on Instagram. That is a huge gap. The same audio, the same clips, the same moments that are hitting hundreds of thousands of views on TikTok should be landing on Instagram Reels and building that follower count to match. Cross-posting alone would close half the gap.

The bigger win is native Instagram content. Reels with tighter hooks, carousel posts that work on the feed, and Stories that turn your regulars into ambassadors. Instagram drives bookings for pubs more directly than TikTok does, especially for the after-work and weekend crowd. You are currently leaving the higher-intent platform underused.

2

The Website Is Still a Placeholder

thedublinernewcastle.com is currently a holding page. For a pub with this much social momentum, that is a serious leak in the funnel. People discover you on TikTok, go to Google, see no proper website, and end up on a third-party listing where the Guinness review is a 2-star from 2024.

A proper site with live music schedule, menu, private hire enquiries, gift vouchers, and an email capture would turn that social traffic into bookings and repeat visits. It also stops competitors from ranking above you when someone searches "Irish pub Newcastle." Right now you are the loudest brand on socials but the quietest in Google results.

3

TripAdvisor Listing Is Unclaimed and Hurting You

The TripAdvisor listing sits at 3.3 stars with 19 reviews and is marked unclaimed. That is a red flag to any tourist searching "Irish pub Newcastle" on TripAdvisor, which is still one of the biggest sources of walk-in trade from out-of-towners during football weekends, stag dos, and the Christmas market.

Claiming the listing, responding to every review, and running a simple review-generation push from your happiest regulars could move that rating to 4.3+ within three months. Google is strong, but TripAdvisor needs the same love because different audiences look in different places.

4

Match Days Are Under-Monetised on Social

The Dubliner is a short walk from St James' Park. Match day content showing a packed pub before kickoff, Geordies singing Irish trad tunes, big screens, fans spilling onto the Quayside afterwards, this is the exact stuff that goes viral during any NUFC weekend. It is also the exact content that fills your pub on the next match day.

Right now, match days feel like a missed content opportunity. A dedicated match day content loop (pre-game hype, in-game atmosphere, full-time celebrations) would lock in the away fan and tourist market permanently. You would become the default "watch the match with proper craic" venue in Newcastle.

5

Staff and Musicians Are Underused as Characters

Your best performing content is already the live music clips with musicians visibly enjoying themselves. That is not an accident. People do not follow pubs, they follow people. The Dubliner has a cast of characters (bartenders, resident musicians, door staff) who could each become recognisable faces on the account.

A bartender who always pulls the "perfect Guinness" shot. A musician who always opens with the same song. A face people start looking out for when they visit. That turns your pub from a venue into a community people feel part of before they have even walked in.

Ready-Made Content
4 Video Ideas You Could Film This Week

These are formats that are working right now on TikTok and Instagram Reels and that are tailored specifically to an Irish pub with live music. Film any one of these properly and it could push The Dubliner into the next bracket of reach.

Idea 01
"The perfect Guinness pour: every step explained by our bartender"
Craft POV Satisfying

Single shot, close up on the tap. Bartender walks through the two-part pour, the 119.5 second rest, the shamrock finish. Narration is calm and confident. End on the slow push of the settled pint across the bar. Audio is either the ambient sound of the pub in the background or a trending moody audio. Text overlay: "This is why it matters."

Why it works: Guinness content consistently pulls massive engagement because people genuinely care about a properly poured pint. It is craft, it is satisfying to watch, and it positions The Dubliner as the authority on Guinness in Newcastle. This is a repeatable series: different bartenders, different common mistakes, Guinness myths debunked. Each one will pull views from the wider Guinness-loving audience across the UK and Ireland.
Idea 02
"POV: you walk into The Dubliner on a live music night"
POV Atmosphere FOMO

First person camera walking in from the Quayside. Cross the threshold. Music is already playing, the room is packed, a musician is in the middle of a crowd singalong. Keep walking, approach the bar, order a Guinness. Hero shot of the pint with the band in soft focus behind it. Finish with a quick pan across the crowd. One take, no cuts, trending audio under a faded live vocal.

Why it works: POV atmosphere videos are the single highest-converting format for hospitality right now. They show exactly what someone is about to experience. People save these to send to friends as a "we are going here." Done right, this is the video that fills your next Friday night. Film a different version for match days, Sunday sessions, and Paddy's Day and you have a whole library.
Idea 03
"Match day at Newcastle's most Irish pub, 3 hours before kickoff"
Match Day Atmosphere NUFC

Fast-cut montage of a match day. Doors open at 11. Pints being pulled in a line. Big screen getting switched on. Fans in black and white filing in. A chant breaks out. The ref blows the whistle. A goal goes in, the place erupts. Full time, fans spilling onto the Quayside still singing. Text overlay: "Every match day at The Dubliner, NE1." Trending NUFC or anthemic audio.

Why it works: NUFC content gets picked up by one of the biggest, most engaged fan bases in the Premier League. Tourists and away fans actively search for "best pub to watch Newcastle" before they travel. This video plants a flag. It also builds a sense of tradition around the pub (this is what match day looks like here) which drives repeat visits from locals who want to be part of it.
Idea 04
"Meet the Dubliner regulars: the bartenders who basically live here"
People Series Personality

Short intro clip for each bartender. Name, how long they have worked at The Dubliner, their signature drink, their favourite song when the band plays it, one funny story from behind the bar. Shot in the bar during opening. Keep it fast, warm, and honest. Make each one its own post so followers start to recognise faces. Pin them to a highlight called "The Team."

Why it works: People return to pubs because of the people, not the pub. Giving your bartenders a public identity turns them into destinations in their own right. Regulars start visiting "on a night Aoife is working." Tourists walk in and feel like they already know the staff. It also helps you recruit better (everyone wants to work at a pub where the staff are mini local celebrities) and directly counters TripAdvisor complaints about unfriendly service.
Digital Enhancements
Beyond Social Media

These are strategic changes that would make a real difference alongside the content work.

Ship a Proper Website

The current holding page is costing you bookings, private hire enquiries, press pickups, and SEO ground. A simple but well-built site would turn social traffic into direct business. Nothing fancy. It just needs to do the job.

  • Homepage with live music schedule, atmosphere video, and a clear "what's on" calendar
  • Private hire and group booking enquiry form (feed to email and a CRM)
  • Email capture for "this week's live music" newsletter
  • Match day page ranking for "watch Newcastle match pub" searches
  • Gift voucher sales (a genuine revenue stream for Irish pubs at Christmas)

Quick win: Even a one-page site with live music schedule and contact details, launched this month, would immediately improve Google search presence and capture bookings you are currently losing.

Claim TripAdvisor and Run a Review Push

19 reviews and 3.3 stars on an unclaimed listing is the biggest under-served asset in your digital estate. Out-of-town visitors, especially during football weekends and stag dos, absolutely look at TripAdvisor before choosing a pub. This is fixable fast.

  • Claim the TripAdvisor listing and respond to every existing review, positive and negative
  • Add a QR code on every table linking to TripAdvisor and Google
  • Train bar staff to casually mention reviews after a happy moment (after a great song, a perfect pint)
  • Target: move TripAdvisor to 4.3 and Google to 4.5 within 90 days

Close the Instagram Gap

Your TikTok is doing four times the follower count of your Instagram. Closing that gap is the single biggest short-term win available. Instagram drives higher-intent traffic for pubs, and your existing TikTok library is already doing the creative work. It just needs to be pushed through the other platform properly.

  • Cross-post every TikTok as a Reel, reformatted with correct safe zones
  • Native Instagram content: carousels with the week's musicians, Stories showing live sessions in real time
  • Turn on Broadcast Channels for "tonight at The Dubliner" updates
  • Build a UGC collection strategy with a branded hashtag for regulars

Turn Live Music Into a Content Machine

Seven nights a week, you have musicians performing to a packed room. Every night is a filming opportunity, but right now the content capture feels opportunistic rather than systematic. A simple filming rota (two short clips per night, one carousel per week, one recap reel per month) would create a constant content flow with almost no extra effort.

The reality: No other Newcastle pub has nightly live music. The first Irish pub in the North East to turn that into a consistent content engine owns the "live music Newcastle" search forever. That should be The Dubliner.
