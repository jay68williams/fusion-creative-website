1

No Review Profiles Yet

Google Business and TripAdvisor listings are two of the most powerful free tools for any food business. Right now, Fuel'd does not appear to have either. This is completely normal for a new business, but getting them set up early is one of the highest-impact things you can do.

When someone in North Shields searches "healthy food near me" or "cafe near Fish Quay," Google shows businesses with active listings and reviews first. Without a listing, you are invisible in those searches. Even a handful of early reviews at a high rating would make a big difference in local discovery. Your customers are clearly enjoying the food. Giving them an easy way to share that publicly starts a flywheel that compounds over time.

2

Social Content Could Be More Consistent

The social accounts are set up and active, which is great. The opportunity is in consistency and format. Health food content, protein shake builds, smoothie preparation, meal prep, and behind-the-scenes kitchen footage are all formats that perform incredibly well on TikTok and Instagram right now.

With the bold Fuel'd brand, the coastal North Shields setting, and the health-focused menu, you have so much content potential. A consistent posting schedule, even three to four times a week, with high-quality short-form video would start building real momentum. The brand voice is already there. The content just needs to match that energy consistently.

The Coastal Location Is an Untapped Content Advantage

Being near the Fish Quay in North Shields is a genuine asset for content. The coastal vibe, the harbour, the morning light. Health food and coastal living is a powerful combination on social media. "Morning protein shake by the coast," "Post-walk fuel at the Fish Quay," "Healthy eating in North Shields." These are all content angles that would resonate with a health-conscious, active audience.

Most health food brands post generic kitchen content. You have the opportunity to tie your content to a place, a lifestyle, and a community. That makes it more shareable, more memorable, and more likely to build a loyal local following.

Ready-Made Content
5 Video Ideas You Could Film Today

These are formats that are working right now for health-focused food brands on TikTok and Instagram. All of them play to Fuel'd's strengths: bold branding, healthy food, and a coastal location.

Idea 01
"What a day of eating at Fuel'd looks like"
Menu Showcase Health Lifestyle

Start with a morning coffee. Show breakfast being prepared. Then a mid-morning smoothie. Lunch being plated. A protein shake to finish. Each item beautifully shot with the Fuel'd branding visible. Fast cuts, trending audio, clean editing. End with "This is what real food looks like."

Why it works: "What I eat in a day" is one of the most popular formats in the health and fitness space on TikTok and Instagram. It showcases your full menu in one video, positions Fuel'd as a lifestyle brand, and gives viewers multiple reasons to visit. It is shareable, saveable, and drives direct footfall.
Idea 02
"Building the ultimate protein shake"
Process Satisfying Product

Overhead camera. Each ingredient goes in one at a time. Protein powder. Banana. Berries. Milk. Ice. The blender spins. Pour it out. The colours, the texture, the perfect pour. Keep it short, satisfying, and visually clean. End with someone taking the first sip.

Why it works: Smoothie and shake-building videos are some of the most watched food content on social media. The process is inherently satisfying, the colours pop on screen, and it makes people thirsty. For a health kitchen, this is your bread and butter content. Quick to film, endlessly repeatable, and consistently high-performing.
Idea 03
"Post-run fuel at the Fish Quay"
Lifestyle Location Community

Start with a shot of someone running along the coast. They finish at the Fish Quay. Walk into Fuel'd. Order. The food arrives. A well-earned healthy meal after a coastal run. Use a motivational or upbeat audio track. End with the Fuel'd branding and location.

Why it works: This ties Fuel'd to the coastal lifestyle and the fitness community in North Shields, Tynemouth, and Whitley Bay. It positions the cafe as the reward after exercise, which is exactly how health-conscious customers think. It also showcases the location advantage that no competitor inland can replicate.
Idea 04
"Real food that gives a fu*k (here is what that means)"
Brand Story Behind the Scenes Values

A behind-the-scenes look at what goes into the food. Fresh ingredients arriving. Meals being prepped by hand. No shortcuts. No processed nonsense. Show the care, the quality, the effort. Let the kitchen do the talking. End with the tagline on screen. Confident, unapologetic, Fuel'd.

Why it works: Brand story content is one of the most powerful ways to build loyalty on social media. People buy from brands they believe in. Showing the "why" behind the food connects with the health-conscious audience on a deeper level than just showing a pretty plate. It turns customers into advocates.
Idea 05
"Saturday morning at North Shields' health kitchen"
Atmosphere Social Proof Energy

Capture a busy Saturday morning. The kitchen in full swing. Orders going out. Customers enjoying their food. Coffee machine steaming. Shakes being poured. The energy of a place that is alive and thriving. Use a track that builds. Show that Fuel'd is the place to be.

Why it works: Busy service footage is social proof in video form. When people see a packed, buzzing cafe, they want to be part of it. This format drives footfall because it creates urgency and FOMO. It also gives potential customers confidence that this is a place worth visiting.
Digital Enhancements
Beyond Social Media

These are strategic changes that would make a real difference alongside the social media work.

Set Up Google Business and TripAdvisor

This is the single highest-impact thing you can do right now. A Google Business listing is free, takes minutes, and immediately puts Fuel'd on the map for local searches. "Healthy food North Shields," "cafe near Fish Quay," "protein shake near me." These are all searches that would lead people straight to your door.

  • Create a Google Business profile with professional photos, hours, and menu highlights
  • Claim or create a TripAdvisor listing
  • Encourage every happy customer to leave a review
  • Respond to every review to show you are active and engaged

Quick win: Even 10-15 five-star Google reviews would put Fuel'd on the local search map. With a 5/5 food hygiene rating and the quality of food you serve, those reviews will come quickly once you make it easy for customers to leave them.

Consistent Content Calendar

The social accounts are set up and the brand voice is clear. The next step is a consistent posting schedule. Three to four posts per week across Instagram and TikTok, mixing food content, behind-the-scenes, lifestyle shots, and the brand story. Consistency is what turns occasional visitors into loyal followers and regular customers.

  • Monday: weekly menu highlight or new item reveal
  • Wednesday: behind the scenes or brand story content
  • Friday: weekend energy, busy service, or customer moments
  • Saturday: lifestyle content tied to the coastal location

Local Community Partnerships

North Shields has a thriving fitness and wellness community. Partnering with local gyms, running clubs, yoga studios, and personal trainers for cross-promotion would put Fuel'd in front of exactly the right audience. A "post-workout fuel" deal, a collaboration with a local PT, or sponsoring a community run would all build awareness with people who are already looking for what you offer.
