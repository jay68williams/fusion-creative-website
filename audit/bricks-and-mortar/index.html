Where The Opportunity Is
Five Areas With Real Potential

The brand, the experience, and the service portfolio are all strong. You have built a property business that people trust. The opportunity now is about making sure more people in Newcastle find you before they find a competitor. Here is where the biggest potential lies.

1

Instagram Has Real Room to Grow

3,200 followers after 938 posts means the content strategy could be optimised to work harder. The posting consistency is there, which is the hardest part, but the content mix and format could be shifted to attract a much larger audience. Property content, area guides for students and renters, moving tips, "what your budget gets you in Newcastle" posts, and student life content all perform incredibly well on Instagram.

The algorithm rewards content that gets saved and shared. Informational property content ("5 things to check before signing a lease", "best student areas ranked") gets saved at a much higher rate than standard listing posts. That signals to Instagram that your content is valuable, and it pushes it to more people. A shift in content strategy could transform the growth trajectory without posting any more frequently than you already do.

2

TikTok Could Be Taken Much Further

You are already posting property tours on TikTok, which is great. But the student lettings market lives on TikTok, and there is so much more you could be doing with the platform. Educational content like "best student areas in Newcastle", "what to look for in a student house", and "day in the life of a letting agent" builds trust and positions Bricks & Mortar as the agent that actually helps rather than just sells.

TikTok rewards personality, authenticity, and usefulness. The property tours are a solid foundation, but layering in behind-the-scenes content, Newcastle area guides, and advice-driven videos would massively increase your reach. Students share this kind of content in group chats and freshers groups. One well-made video about the best student areas could reach every incoming Newcastle University and Northumbria University student organically.

3

Google Rating of 3.9 Could Be Improved

442 reviews is a strong volume, which shows lots of people are engaging with the brand. The opportunity is in proactively collecting reviews from your happiest tenants and landlords to push that average up. In a trust-based business like lettings and property sales, Google reviews are often the first thing a potential client checks. The difference between 3.9 and 4.3+ is significant in terms of click-through rates and initial trust.

Responding to every review, positive and negative, also makes a big difference. It shows potential clients that you are engaged and care about the experience. A systematic review collection process, asking satisfied tenants at key moments like after a smooth move-in or a well-handled maintenance request, would steadily push the rating upward over the next 6 months.

4

Facebook Is Underweight for a 20+ Year Business

598 likes for a business that has been operating since 2003 means Facebook has been underutilised. This matters because Facebook is where landlords, property investors, and older renters spend their time. While TikTok and Instagram reach students, Facebook reaches the people who own the properties. Landlords looking for a management company, families looking to buy, and professionals looking to rent are all on Facebook.

The good news is that Facebook rewards consistency, and you already have the content habit. Cross-posting key content to Facebook, running targeted local ads, and engaging in Newcastle property groups would build this platform out alongside TikTok and Instagram. A multi-platform approach means you are reaching tenants, landlords, and buyers wherever they spend time online.

5

No Visible Paid Advertising

Targeted ads to Newcastle University and Northumbria University students during peak letting season (January to March) would drive direct enquiries at the most critical time of year. This is when students are actively searching for next year's housing, and the agents who show up in their feeds win the business. A well-targeted campaign during this window could generate significant inbound interest for relatively modest spend.

Beyond student lettings, paid social can also target landlords looking for management services, first-time buyers in specific Newcastle postcodes, and professionals relocating to the city. Each audience gets a different message and a different call to action. Organic content builds the brand. Paid advertising drives immediate, measurable enquiries when you need them most.

Ready-Made Content
5 Video Ideas You Could Film Today

These are formats that are working right now for property businesses on TikTok and Instagram. All of them play to Bricks & Mortar's strengths as an established Newcastle estate and letting agent with deep local knowledge.

Idea 01
"Best student areas in Newcastle ranked"
Area Guide Student Shareable

Walk through Jesmond, Heaton, Sandyford, Fenham, and other popular student areas. Rate each one honestly. Show the streets, the local shops, the vibe. Give genuine opinions on which suits different types of students. Keep it fast-paced with on-screen text rankings.

Why it works: This will get shared in every freshers group chat, every university housing thread, and every "where should I live" conversation. It positions Bricks & Mortar as the local expert and drives enquiries from students who trust the recommendation. Area ranking content is one of the highest-performing property formats on TikTok.
Idea 02
"What your budget actually gets you in Newcastle"
Price Reveal Property Tour Local

Show properties at different monthly price points. Start at the lower end and work up. Walk through each one. Show the rooms, the kitchen, the area. Let viewers see exactly what their budget gets them in Newcastle. End with a call to action to get in touch for available properties.

Why it works: Budget comparison content is one of the most viral property formats. Students and renters love knowing exactly what they can expect for their money. It drives comments ("I pay that much for half the space"), saves (people bookmark for later), and shares (people tag flatmates). This format works every single time.
Idea 03
"5 things to check before signing a tenancy"
Educational Trust Building Advice

Walk through the key things every renter should check: inventory condition, damp signs, water pressure, energy rating, deposit protection. Be genuinely helpful. Do not sell. Just give honest, useful advice that positions the brand as the agent that actually looks out for tenants.

Why it works: Educational content builds trust faster than any other format. When an agent gives advice that helps tenants rather than just pushing listings, it creates a completely different relationship. This is the kind of content that gets saved thousands of times and makes people think "I want to rent through these people." Trust is everything in lettings.
Idea 04
"POV: You just found your new flat"
Property Reveal Aspirational TikTok Native

Door opens. Camera slowly pans through the rooms. Natural light coming through the windows. The kitchen, the living space, the bedroom. Reactions from the viewer's perspective. Use a trending audio track. End with the location and a "link in bio" prompt. Property reveals are huge on TikTok.

Why it works: Property reveal videos are one of the most watched formats on TikTok. People watch them even when they are not looking for a property. That means massive reach. When someone who is actually searching sees a beautiful flat in Newcastle from Bricks & Mortar, they enquire immediately. This format turns passive viewers into active leads.
Idea 05
"Day in the life of a Newcastle letting agent"
Behind the Scenes Brand Building Personality

Follow a member of the team through a real day. Morning briefing. Heading out to viewings. Showing a property to students. Handling a key handover. Checking on a property. Back to the office. Genuine, unpolished, and human. Show the people behind the brand.

Why it works: People rent from people, not logos. Behind-the-scenes content humanises the brand and builds a connection before someone even walks through the door. It also shows the level of care and professionalism the team puts in. In a market where tenants often feel like just a number, showing the real people who will look after their home is incredibly powerful.

Google Review Management Strategy

With 442 reviews and a 3.9 rating, there is a clear opportunity to push this higher through proactive management. Responding to every review, both positive and negative, shows potential clients that you care about the experience. Proactively asking happy tenants and landlords for reviews at key moments will steadily shift the average upward.

  • Respond to every Google review within 48 hours, positive and negative, with genuine, personalised responses
  • Identify key moments to ask for reviews: successful move-in, quick maintenance resolution, smooth tenancy renewal, completed sale
  • Address common complaints publicly and constructively to show prospective clients how issues are handled
  • Target: push from 3.9 to 4.2+ within 6 months through consistent review collection and management

Why this matters: In a trust-based business like lettings and property sales, Google reviews are often the first thing a potential client checks. The difference between 3.9 and 4.3 can be the difference between getting the enquiry and losing it to a competitor.
