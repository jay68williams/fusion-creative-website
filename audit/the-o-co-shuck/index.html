Where The Opportunity Is
Five Areas With Real Potential

The O Co. has a product that is built for social media. These are the five areas where focused effort would turn a brilliant concept into a brand that people book months in advance.

1

TikTok Does Not Exist Yet

Oyster shucking, charcoal grilling, live food theatre. This is the exact content that goes viral on TikTok every single day. The satisfying sound of a shell cracking open, the sizzle of an oyster hitting the Konro, the first bite reaction from a guest who has never tried one before. You are sitting on a goldmine of content and there is no TikTok account to capture it.

Mobile food businesses that crack TikTok see a direct impact on bookings because event planners and brides scroll TikTok for inspiration. One viral oyster shucking video could fill your calendar for the rest of the year. The format is already proven. You just need to start posting.

2

No Website Means No Search Presence

When someone in Newcastle searches "mobile oyster bar hire" or "oyster catering North East" or "unique wedding food ideas Newcastle," The O Co. does not show up. You have no website, so Google has nowhere to send those people. Right now, every one of those searches is going to a competitor or just nowhere.

A simple, beautiful one-page site with your story, photos, the menu, testimonials, and a booking enquiry form would immediately capture traffic from people who are actively looking for exactly what you offer. It also gives you a professional link to put in every email, every Instagram bio, every DM when someone asks "do you have a website?"

3

Instagram Is Active But Not Growing

1,621 followers after 188 posts means the content is not reaching beyond your existing audience. The posting is consistent, which is great, but the formats need to shift towards what the algorithm actually pushes. Reels under 20 seconds with strong hooks in the first frame. Right now, the feed looks good to someone who already follows you, but it is not built to be discovered by new people.

The fix is not posting more. It is posting smarter. One well-crafted 15-second Reel of an oyster being shucked with the right audio and a strong opening frame will reach more people than ten static posts combined. The product is already cinematic. The content strategy just needs to match.

4

The Wedding Market Is Under-Exploited

You have a perfect 5.0 on Hitched, you are listed as a wedding supplier, and your product is exactly the kind of "wow moment" that brides want at their reception. But the online presence does not lean into this hard enough. There is no dedicated wedding content series on Instagram, no wedding highlight reel, no "what it looks like when we set up at your wedding" video.

Wedding suppliers who create aspirational content on Instagram and TikTok get booked 6-12 months in advance. A single video showing The O Co. cart at a beautiful venue, guests gathering around for freshly shucked oysters, the bride and groom taking their first bite together. That is the video that gets saved, shared, and sent to a wedding planner with the message "we need this."

5

Corporate and Brand Events Are an Untapped Revenue Stream

Oyster bars are a premium, experiential food offering. That makes them perfect for corporate events, brand launches, gallery openings, and private parties where the host wants something memorable. But if someone at a brand agency searches for "experiential food catering Newcastle," The O Co. does not appear anywhere.

Building content around corporate and brand events, even if you have only done a handful, positions you as the go-to for that market. A short case study reel showing a setup at a gallery or a brand launch, with a text overlay like "corporate oyster bar hire, North East," would start capturing enquiries from a market with much higher average booking values than weddings.

Ready-Made Content
4 Video Ideas You Could Film This Week

These are formats working right now on TikTok and Instagram Reels, tailored specifically to a mobile oyster bar. Film any one of these at your next pop-up and it could be the video that fills your booking calendar.

Idea 01
"How we shuck a Lindisfarne oyster in under 10 seconds"
Craft Satisfying ASMR

Close-up on the hands. The knife slides in. Twist. Pop. The shell opens. Clean the oyster. Place it on ice. Squeeze of lemon. Push it across to the guest. All in one continuous close-up shot. Pure ambient sound or trending ASMR audio. Text overlay: "Lindisfarne oysters. Shucked fresh in front of you." Under 15 seconds.

Why it works: Shucking videos are one of the most reliably viral formats in food content. The skill, the sound, the reveal. People watch them on repeat. It also answers the question every potential customer has: "what actually happens when I book The O Co.?" This is both a brand video and a conversion tool in one shot.
Idea 02
"POV: you hired an oyster bar for your wedding"
Wedding POV Aspirational

The cart arrives. Set up timelapse. Ice goes down. Oysters stacked. First guests approach. Wide shot of the scene. Bride walks over. First oyster of the night. Guests laughing, clinking glasses, the sun going down. Text overlay: "The O Co. Wedding packages from just 300 pounds." Cinematic trending audio. 20-30 seconds.

Why it works: Brides plan weddings on Instagram and TikTok. This is the video that gets saved to a "Wedding Ideas" folder and sent to a wedding planner. The price point in the text overlay removes the "I bet I can not afford this" barrier immediately. This single video could generate more wedding enquiries than a month of static posts.
Idea 03
"Grilled oysters on a Japanese Konro: the process"
Process Fire Premium

The Konro lit with Bincho charcoal. Oysters placed on the grill. Truffle butter melting into the shell. Close-up of the bubbling. Lift it off with tongs. Garnish. Serve. The charcoal glow in the background. Text overlay: "Not your average oyster. Grilled on Japanese Bincho charcoal." Trending moody audio. Under 20 seconds.

Why it works: Fire and food is one of the most engaging combinations on social media. The Konro grill sets you apart from every other oyster bar in the country. Most people have never seen a Japanese charcoal grill. That novelty is the hook. People will ask "what is that grill?" in the comments, which drives engagement and makes the algorithm push the video further.
Idea 04
"First time trying an oyster: guest reactions at our pop-up"
Reactions People Fun

Quick cuts of different guests trying their first oyster. The nervous face. The slurp. The reaction. Some love it, some pull a face, everyone laughs. Keep it fast and fun. Text overlay: "First oyster reactions at The O Co." Upbeat trending audio. 15-20 seconds. Film this at every pop-up and you have an endless series.

Why it works: Reaction content is one of the highest-engagement formats on both platforms. It is relatable, funny, and shareable. People tag friends with "this would be you." It also humanises the brand and shows that The O Co. is not just about oysters, it is about the experience and the energy around it. This is the series that builds a community, not just an audience.

Beyond Social Media

Build a Simple Website

You need a home on the internet that is not Instagram. A one-page site with your story, the menu, testimonials, photos, and a booking enquiry form. This is the page you send when someone asks "can you do our event?" and it is the page Google shows when someone searches "mobile oyster bar hire North East."

  • Hero section with your best event photo and a clear "Book Us" CTA
  • What We Offer section showing the different packages
  • Testimonials pulled from Hitched (5.0 rating, 100% recommendation)
  • Photo gallery from weddings, pop-ups, and events
  • Simple enquiry form (event type, date, guest count, email)

Quick win: We can build this for you. A clean, mobile-first site that loads fast and converts enquiries. It would pay for itself with the first booking it generates.

Launch TikTok Before Summer

Summer is your peak season. Pop-ups, festivals, weddings, outdoor events. Every one of those is a filming opportunity. If you launch TikTok now and post consistently through May, June, and July, you will have a library of content and a growing audience before the busiest months of the year. Waiting until next year means missing this entire season.

  • Create @theocoshuck on TikTok (match the brand)
  • Post 3-4 short videos per week from pop-ups and events
  • Use food, wedding, and North East location hashtags
  • Cross-post every TikTok as an Instagram Reel

Create a Wedding Content Series

Your perfect Hitched score is brilliant but only reaches people who are already on Hitched. A dedicated wedding content series on Instagram and TikTok puts you in front of brides who are scrolling for inspiration months before they start looking at supplier directories.

The opportunity: Wedding content has a long shelf life. A beautiful 20-second video of The O Co. at a wedding venue gets saved, shared, and revisited for months. One bride sees it, sends it to her maid of honour, who sends it to her friend who is also getting married. That compound sharing is how wedding suppliers fill their calendars a year in advance.
