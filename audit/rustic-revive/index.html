Where The Opportunity Is
Five Areas With Real Potential

Rustic Revive has the product, the showroom, the press, and the founder story. These are the five areas where focused content effort would turn a craft business with great work into a brand that people in the North East and beyond know by name.

1

The Content Is Not Built For The Algorithm

You are posting almost daily, which is fantastic. But the format is mostly static product photos with functional captions like "message for details" or price drops. That style of content does not get pushed by Instagram or TikTok any more. The algorithm rewards Reels with a strong hook in the first second, motion, transformation, and a reason to keep watching. Static photos sit at the bottom of the feed and get seen by almost nobody. The good news: the raw material for great Reels is already in the workshop every single day. Sparks flying. Hot metal being shaped. A flat piece of timber becoming a dining table over the course of 30 seconds of timelapse. That content is the difference between 11 likes and 100,000 views.

2

The Maker Story Is Not Being Told On Video

Twenty years of fabrication. Local steel. Local wood. A real workshop in County Durham. That is the kind of story that the "buy British, buy local, buy handmade" audience is actively searching for, and the algorithm pushes hard when it surfaces. But right now there is no founder content. No face. No voice. No process. The brands winning in the craft furniture space (Benchmark Furniture, A Rustic Garden, House of Hackney, smaller makers on TikTok with hundreds of thousands of followers) all lead with the maker. People do not buy a table. They buy from the person who made the table. Once your face is in the content, everything changes.

3

TikTok Is Massively Underused For Furniture Content

Right now TikTok is the biggest discovery engine for craft and home content in the UK. #FurnitureRestoration, #WoodworkingTikTok, #BespokeBuild, all have millions of views. People are obsessed with watching a piece of furniture be built from scratch. The transformation arc, raw material to finished piece, is the most binge-able format on the platform. You have a separate @rusticrevive TikTok handle that is listed but barely being used. That is leaving the biggest free distribution channel in the UK completely on the table. One properly produced build video on TikTok could reach more people than 12 months of Instagram posts combined.

4

Captions Are Selling Hard When They Should Be Storytelling

Most of the current captions read like classified ads. Prices, "message for details", direct calls to buy. That style worked five years ago, but the platforms now penalise it. The captions that win in 2026 are the ones that tell a story or share a thought. "This table started life as a pile of scrap steel from a yard in Durham. Twelve hours later, this." That kind of caption rewards the scroll. It makes people read, share, and remember. Once the captions match the quality of the work, the engagement starts climbing.

5

The Website Is Doing The Job, Not Selling The Story

rusticrevive.co.uk is clean, has a shop, has the basics, but it is not doing the brand justice. The press features should be front and centre. The bespoke process should be a dedicated page with photos and a video walkthrough. There should be customer testimonials, finished installations in real homes and businesses, and a gallery of past commissions that proves the breadth of what you can build. Right now the site asks people to take a leap of faith. It could be doing the heavy lifting and converting curious visitors into deposit-paying enquiries before they even pick up the phone.

Ready-Made Content
4 Video Ideas You Could Film This Week

These are formats working right now on TikTok and Instagram Reels, tailored specifically to a bespoke craft furniture brand. Film any one of these and it could be the video that puts Rustic Revive in front of hundreds of thousands of new people.

Idea 01
"Scrap to centrepiece. 12 hours condensed into 30 seconds."
Build Timelapse Transformation

Pick one bespoke piece currently being built. Lock a phone on a tripod in the workshop. Film every stage: raw material delivery, cutting, welding (sparks!), sanding, finishing, the final install in the customer's space. Cut it down to a 30-second timelapse with a satisfying audio track. Text overlay: "Scrap steel to dining table. Made in County Durham." That is it.

Why it works: Transformation timelapses are the single highest-performing format on TikTok and Reels right now. The hook is built into the format: the viewer cannot help but watch to see the finished result. This one video on its own could reach more people than every Instagram post Rustic Revive has ever published, combined.
Idea 02
"I've been making furniture for 20 years. Here is the piece I am most proud of."
Founder Story Authenticity

You, Jonathan, talking to camera in the workshop. Keep it raw and honest. Pick one piece you have built that means the most to you and tell the story. Who was it for? What was the brief? What challenged you? What did the customer say when they saw it? 45-60 seconds. Cut to shots of the piece being made and the finished install. Trending workshop audio underneath.

Why it works: Founder story videos are the single most effective format for building a craft brand on social media. People follow people. Once the audience knows your name, your voice, and the level of care that goes into every piece, the brand stops being "another furniture maker" and starts being "Jonathan from Rustic Revive in Durham." That is a completely different sales conversation.
Idea 03
"The R2 droid bar. The full story behind the most bizarre thing we have ever built."
Standout Story Shareable

The novelty pieces are gold. R2 droid drinks unit. Motorbike bar. These are the pieces that make people stop and ask "wait, you can do THAT?" Pick one. Tell the full story. Who asked for it. How you built it. The mad welds, the wiring, the finish. Pan around the finished piece in the showroom or in the customer's space. End on "what would you commission us to build?" Comments will explode.

Why it works: Novelty and "I did not know you could commission that" content is one of the most shared formats in the bespoke craft space. The R2 droid alone has the potential to go viral on its own merits. These are not just product videos. They are conversation starters. And every share puts Rustic Revive in front of someone with a "wouldn't it be cool if..." idea of their own.
Idea 04
"This is what £4,000 of dining table actually looks like."
Value Reveal Justification

Camera on you, Jonathan, beside a finished dining table in the showroom. Talk through what makes it cost what it costs. The hardwood. The hand-finished steel base. The hours of welding. The fact that it will outlast a flat-pack equivalent by 50 years. End with "or you can buy three IKEA tables and bin them every five years. Up to you." Light humour, real authority.

Why it works: Price-justification content quietly does some of the heaviest lifting in any high-ticket product business. People scroll past expensive furniture because they cannot see why it costs what it costs. The moment you show them, the price stops being a barrier and starts being a feature. This content also pre-qualifies enquiries: anyone watching to the end already understands the value. They are ready to buy.
Digital Enhancements
Beyond Social Media

These are strategic changes that would make a real difference alongside the content work.

Surface The Press On The Website

Featured in Living North magazine. Featured in Absolutely Yorkshire. Exhibited at Living North LIVE. None of this is visible the moment someone lands on rusticrevive.co.uk. That is press credibility being wasted. A simple "As Featured In" banner on the homepage with the magazine logos, plus a dedicated press page, would meaningfully shift how visitors perceive the brand within the first three seconds of being on the site.

  • Add "As Featured In" logo strip to homepage hero
  • Create a dedicated /press page with images of the magazine spreads
  • Reference the press in every product description
  • Add a quote from the Living North feature to the about page

Quick win: Even a single line under the logo on the homepage saying "Featured in Living North magazine" would lift conversion without any other change required.

Build A Dedicated "Bespoke Process" Page

Right now the website assumes visitors already know how to commission a piece. A dedicated bespoke process page would walk them through it: initial enquiry, consultation, design, materials, build, install. Photos of every stage. A timeline. A "starting from" price. That single page would turn casual browsers into qualified enquiries because it removes the friction of "I'd love something but I don't know where to start."

  • Step-by-step process with photos from a real recent commission
  • Indicative pricing bands ("Dining tables from £1,500. Bar builds from £3,000.")
  • A 60-second video walkthrough of the process
  • An enquiry form at the bottom optimised for high-intent leads

Add Customer Testimonials And Real Installs

You have a showroom, but the website does not show the pieces in real customer spaces. That is a missed trust signal. A "Past Commissions" gallery showing finished pieces in real homes, bars, and businesses, with a short caption about what the customer asked for, would be one of the highest-converting changes possible on the site.

The opportunity: The next time you deliver a piece, take 10 minutes to photograph it in the customer's space and grab a one-sentence quote. That single habit, applied to every install, builds a content and trust library that compounds for the lifetime of the business.
