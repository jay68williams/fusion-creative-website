Where The Opportunity Is
Five Areas With Real Potential

The product, the following, and the delivery infrastructure are all strong. You have built a brand that people genuinely love. These are the areas where a focused push could take things to the next level.

1

No Website Is a Missed Opportunity

Right now, when someone searches "juice bar Newcastle" or "smoothie bowls near me," you are relying entirely on social media and delivery apps to be found. A simple, well-designed website would give you a permanent home on Google, somewhere to showcase your full menu, your story, your locations and hours, and a direct ordering link. It also builds credibility. When someone hears about you and Googles "Priory Press Newcastle," they expect to find a website.

A one-page site with your menu, locations, hours, delivery links, and your Instagram feed embedded would be enough to start. It does not need to be complicated, but it needs to exist.

2

No TripAdvisor Presence

TripAdvisor is one of the first places tourists and visitors check when looking for places to eat in Newcastle. Not being listed means you are invisible to a significant audience, especially the Tynemouth location where visitor traffic is high during weekends and summer months.

Setting up a TripAdvisor listing is free and straightforward. Once it is live, a simple system of encouraging happy customers to leave reviews could build your profile quickly. With the quality of your products, the reviews will take care of themselves. You just need the listing to exist.

3

TikTok Is Still in Its Early Stages

With 1,176 followers, your TikTok account exists but has not yet had its breakthrough moment. The good news is that health food content is one of the highest-performing categories on TikTok. Smoothie bowls being assembled, juices being pressed, acai bowls being topped with colourful ingredients. These are exactly the kind of visually satisfying videos that go viral on the platform.

A consistent posting schedule of 3 to 5 short videos per week, using trending sounds and formats, could grow this account rapidly. One viral video showing a beautiful smoothie bowl being built from scratch could put Priory Press in front of hundreds of thousands of people in the North East overnight.

4

A Stronger Digital Marketing Engine

Right now, your growth is largely organic, which is great, but it means you are leaving a lot on the table. A structured approach to content creation, posting schedules, hashtag strategy, and community engagement would amplify everything you are already doing. Paired with targeted paid ads reaching health-conscious people within 5 miles of each location, you could significantly increase both footfall and delivery orders.

The content practically creates itself. Your products are already beautiful. It is about capturing that consistently and putting it in front of the right people at the right time.

5

Turn Influencer Coverage Into a Strategy

You are already getting organic influencer visits, which is fantastic. The next step is turning that into a deliberate strategy. Inviting local food creators in for a complimentary bowl in exchange for content, creating an "Instagrammable moment" in-store that encourages user-generated content, and reposting customer content to your own feed. Every piece of content someone else creates about Priory Press is free marketing that reaches their entire audience.

A small influencer programme, even just reaching out to 5 local creators per month, could generate a steady stream of high-quality content and new customer discovery.

Ready-Made Content
5 Video Ideas You Could Film Today

These are formats that are working right now for health food brands on TikTok and Instagram. All of them play to Priory Press's strengths as a fresh, visual, health-focused juice bar and cafe.

Idea 01
"How we build a smoothie bowl from scratch"
Behind the Scenes ASMR Process

Start with the empty bowl. Blend the base. Pour it in. Then the satisfying part: each topping placed one by one. Granola, fresh fruit, drizzles of honey, coconut flakes. Overhead angle. No voiceover needed, just the sounds and a trending audio track. End with a spoon breaking through the surface.

Why it works: Bowl-building videos are one of the most viral food formats on TikTok. The process is inherently satisfying and the colourful end result is instantly shareable. This single format could be your most consistent content pillar.
Idea 02
"Fresh pressed juice in 30 seconds"
Process Satisfying Product

Show the whole fruit going in, the press doing its work, and the vibrant juice pouring into the bottle or glass. Quick cuts. Bright colours. The contrast between whole fruit and the finished juice is incredibly visual. End with someone taking the first sip.

Why it works: Juicing content is endlessly rewatchable. The colours, the sound of the press, the transformation from fruit to juice. It triggers the satisfying content algorithm on both TikTok and Instagram, which means massive organic reach without spending a penny.
Idea 03
"What to order at Priory Press if you want a protein hit"
Educational Menu Guide Wellness

A quick, helpful guide walking through the best high-protein options on the menu. Show each item being made or served. Text overlay with the key nutritional highlights. Friendly, informative tone. "If you are looking for protein, here is what I would order." Make it a series: protein, energy, post-workout, gluten-free.

Why it works: Educational food content performs brilliantly because people save it for later. Every save signals to the algorithm that it is valuable content, which pushes it to more people. It also positions Priory Press as a go-to for health-conscious customers, not just a cafe.
Idea 04
"Morning at Priory Press before we open"
Behind the Scenes Aesthetic Brand Story

The quiet calm of early morning prep. Fruit being washed and sliced. The first juice of the day being pressed. Machines warming up. The cafe coming to life before the first customer walks in. Soft lighting. A lo-fi or ambient soundtrack. It is peaceful, aspirational, and shows the care that goes into everything.

Why it works: "Day in the life" and "opening routine" videos are consistently among the highest-performing content for cafes and food businesses. People love seeing the behind-the-scenes process. It builds connection and trust, and it humanises the brand in a way that product shots alone cannot.
Idea 05
"We deliver to your door. Here is what arrived."
Delivery Unboxing Revenue Driver

Film from the customer's perspective. The Deliveroo bag arrives. Open it up. Pull out each item one by one. Show the quality of the packaging, the colours of the food, the presentation even through delivery. End with everything laid out on the table looking incredible. Tag your Deliveroo and Uber Eats pages.

Why it works: Unboxing and delivery reveal content is huge on TikTok. Most people do not realise you deliver, so this single video format could drive a significant increase in delivery orders. It also shows that your products look just as good delivered as they do in-store, which removes a major hesitation for new delivery customers.
Digital Enhancements
Beyond Social Media

These are strategic changes that would make a real difference alongside the social media work.

Build a Simple Website

A clean, one-page website with your menu, both locations and hours, delivery platform links, your Instagram feed, and your brand story. It does not need to be complex. It needs to exist so that when someone Googles "Priory Press Newcastle" or "juice bar near me," they find you directly rather than having to go through Instagram or a delivery app.

  • A simple domain like priorypress.co.uk would be ideal
  • Mobile-first design since most searches happen on phones
  • Direct links to Deliveroo and Uber Eats for instant ordering
  • Your 5/5 food hygiene rating displayed prominently as a trust signal

Quick win: Even before a full website, setting up a Google Business Profile for both locations (if not already done) with professional photos, accurate hours, and your menu would significantly improve local search visibility.

Claim Your TripAdvisor Listing

TripAdvisor is free to list on and reaches a massive audience, especially for the Tynemouth location where tourist and visitor traffic is significant. Setting up a listing and encouraging your happiest customers to leave reviews could open up a whole new discovery channel.

A QR code at the till or on the counter linking to your review pages makes it effortless for customers to leave a review while they are still enjoying their smoothie bowl.

Google Business Profile Optimisation

Make sure both locations have fully optimised Google Business Profiles with professional food photography, accurate hours, your full menu, and regular posts about new items or specials. Respond to every review. Post weekly updates. This is often the very first thing people see when they search for you, and it needs to be as impressive as the products you serve.
