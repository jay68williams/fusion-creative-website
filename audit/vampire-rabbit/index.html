Only 41 TripAdvisor Reviews Is Holding Back Your Ranking

Your TripAdvisor score of 4.8 is phenomenal. But with only 41 reviews, you are ranked #35 of 160 in Newcastle Nightlife. With that score and more reviews, you should be in the top 10. TripAdvisor's algorithm heavily weights review volume alongside rating. A bar with 200 reviews at 4.5 will outrank a bar with 41 reviews at 4.8.

The fix is straightforward: a systematic review generation approach. A small card with the bill, a follow-up message, a gentle prompt from staff. Your customers clearly love the experience. They just need a nudge to share it on TripAdvisor. Getting to 150+ reviews would likely push you into the top 10, which dramatically increases visibility to tourists and locals planning nights out.

Ready-Made Content
5 Video Ideas You Could Film Today

These are formats that are working right now for cocktail bars and nightlife venues on TikTok and Instagram. All of them play to Vampire Rabbit's greatest strengths: the brand story, the cocktails, and The Rabbit Hole.

Idea 01
"There is a secret piano bar hidden under Newcastle. Here is how to find it."
Discovery Hidden Gem Viral Potential

Start on Collingwood Street. Walk into Vampire Rabbit. Show the main bar briefly. Then reveal the staircase going down. The camera descends into The Rabbit Hole. The lighting changes, the piano starts playing, the atmosphere shifts completely. End with someone sitting down with a cocktail. "The Rabbit Hole. Under the Vampire Rabbit. Newcastle."

Why it works: "Hidden bar" and "secret room" content is one of the most reliably viral formats on TikTok. People love discovering places they did not know existed in their own city. This video would reach people across Newcastle, the wider North East, and tourists planning visits. The Rabbit Hole is a genuine hidden gem and this video would put it on the map permanently.
Idea 02
"The story behind Newcastle's most mysterious carving (and why we named a bar after it)"
Storytelling History Brand Origin

Tell the story of the actual Vampire Rabbit carving on St Nicholas Cathedral. The mystery, the theories about its origin, why nobody knows who put it there or why. Then connect it to the bar: "We built a cocktail bar inspired by it." Show the venue, the themed interiors, the cocktails named after the legend. History meets hospitality.

Why it works: Local history and mystery content performs incredibly well on TikTok. The Vampire Rabbit carving is already one of Newcastle's most searched landmarks. A video connecting the real carving to your bar captures that search traffic and tells people a story they will remember and share. It is the ultimate brand origin story.
Idea 03
"Bartender makes 5 signature cocktails in 60 seconds"
Cocktails Fast-Paced Satisfying

Quick cuts of your bartender making five different signature cocktails. The measuring, the shaking, the pouring, the garnishing. Each one more visually stunning than the last. No voiceover. Just the sounds of cocktail making and a driving audio track. End with all five lined up on the bar. "30+ signatures. Which one is yours?"

Why it works: Cocktail-making content is one of the most watched categories on TikTok and Instagram. The visual appeal is enormous: the colours, the techniques, the glassware, the garnishes. A fast-paced montage of five cocktails showcases your range and skill in a format that people watch on repeat. It directly drives the "I need to try these" response that fills bars.
Idea 04
"POV: Friday night at one of Newcastle's highest-rated cocktail bars"
Atmosphere Social Proof Weekend Vibes

Capture the energy of a Friday or Saturday night. The busy bar, cocktails being served, the live music upstairs, the piano playing in The Rabbit Hole downstairs. Panning shots of groups enjoying themselves, glasses clinking, the bartender in action. Use a warm, atmospheric audio track. Let the vibe sell itself.

Why it works: Atmosphere content drives FOMO, which drives bookings. When people see a packed, vibrant bar with beautiful cocktails and live music, they want to be there. This content targets people planning their weekend and gives them a reason to choose Vampire Rabbit over every other option in Newcastle. The combination of the main bar and The Rabbit Hole gives you two completely different atmospheres in one video.
Idea 05
"We asked the bartender to make us something off-menu. This is what happened."
Off-Menu Skill Surprise

Ask the bartender to create something special based on a flavour preference or mood. Film the process: the thinking, the selecting of ingredients, the crafting, the tasting. Show the genuine reaction to a custom cocktail. End with "Tell your bartender what you are in the mood for. They will make something you have never had before."

Why it works: "Off-menu" and "custom order" content drives enormous engagement because it showcases skill and creates a unique experience that people want to replicate. It positions your bartenders as craftspeople, not just drink pourers. It also encourages customers to visit and try the same thing, which creates a recurring content cycle as more people film their own custom cocktail experience.

Beyond Social Media

TripAdvisor Review Volume Strategy

Your 4.8 rating is exceptional but 41 reviews is keeping you at #35 when you should be in the top 10. A focused review generation programme would change that within a few months.

  • A small card with the bill or a QR code on cocktail menus linking directly to TripAdvisor
  • Staff trained to ask guests who compliment the experience to share it online
  • A follow-up message after events or private bookings with a review link
  • Target: 150+ TripAdvisor reviews within 6 months while maintaining 4.7+

Quick win: Getting from 41 to 100 reviews at your current rating would likely push you into the top 15 for Newcastle Nightlife on TripAdvisor, massively increasing visibility to tourists and locals searching for the best bars in the city.

"The Rabbit Hole at Vampire Rabbit" has its own identity, its own atmosphere, and its own audience.
