Ready-Made Content
5 Video Ideas You Could Film Today

These are formats that are working right now for food brands on TikTok and Instagram. All of them play to Deep North's strengths as an artisan doughnut brand with a loyal, passionate following.

Idea 01
"How we make 500 doughnuts before you wake up"
Behind the Scenes Process Satisfying

Time-lapse and close-up footage of the early morning production run. Dough being mixed, shaped, fried to golden perfection, glazed, filled, and lined up in rows. The sheer volume and the craftsmanship. End with the shop doors opening and the first customer of the day.

Why it works: "How it is made" content is one of the most reliable viral formats in food. It satisfies curiosity, showcases quality, and the visual appeal of doughnut production is almost impossible to scroll past. This type of content regularly hits millions of views for bakeries around the world.
Idea 02
"Ranking every Deep North doughnut (honest tier list)"
Engagement Menu Feature Debate

Lay out every doughnut on the menu. Taste each one. Rank them into tiers from "incredible" to "life-changing." Be genuine and playful. Let the audience argue in the comments about what should be number one. Feature the Homer prominently.

Why it works: Tier list and ranking content drives enormous comment engagement because everyone has an opinion. People will tag friends, argue about their favourite, and save the video for next time they visit. It showcases the full range and creates repeat viewing as people come back to check the rankings.
Idea 03
"We just dropped our new seasonal flavour. Here it is."
Product Launch Hype FOMO

Build anticipation with a teaser showing ingredients being prepped but not revealing the final product. Then the big reveal: the finished doughnut in all its glory. Close-up of the first cut, showing the filling. First customer reaction. "Available now. Limited time only."

Why it works: Product launch content creates urgency and FOMO. "Limited time" and "seasonal" are powerful triggers that drive immediate action. People will share it with friends, save it for the weekend, and physically visit the nearest location to try it before it is gone. It turns every new flavour into a content event.
Idea 04
"The doughnut that made us famous (The Homer)"
Origin Story Signature Product Brand

Tell the story of the Homer doughnut. Where the idea came from, how you perfected the recipe, why it became the one everyone talks about. Show it being made from start to finish with the care and craft that goes into it. End with a montage of customers biting into it for the first time.

Why it works: Every great food brand has a hero product story. The Homer is already iconic in Newcastle. Giving it a proper origin story video creates a piece of evergreen content that defines your brand. New customers will discover it and immediately understand what Deep North is about. It humanises the brand and gives people a story to share.
Idea 05
"We gave 100 doughnuts to strangers in Newcastle. Their reactions were priceless."
Giveaway Reactions Feel-Good

Take boxes of doughnuts out onto the streets of Newcastle. Hand them out to strangers. Film their genuine reactions. The surprise, the first bite, the immediate "where is this from?" question. Include the location and handle at the end. Pure feel-good content.

Why it works: Giveaway and reaction content is one of the most shareable formats on social media. It feels generous, it is entertaining, and every person who receives a doughnut becomes a potential new customer. The reactions sell the product better than any advertisement ever could. It also positions Deep North as a brand that gives back to the community.
Digital Enhancements
Beyond Social Media

These are strategic improvements that would make a real difference alongside the social media work.

Google Review Strategy Across All Locations

A 4.1 Google rating is good, but with the level of love Deep North receives, it should be higher. Each location needs its own review generation system. QR codes at every counter linking directly to the Google review page for that specific location. Staff trained to prompt happy customers. Follow-up messages after delivery or pickup orders.

Quick win: Focus on your highest-traffic location first. Getting to 4.5+ on Google at one location creates a template you can roll out across all four. The difference between 4.1 and 4.5 in search results is enormous for new customer acquisition.

UGC Collaboration Programme

Creators are already making content about Deep North. Formalise that relationship. Create a simple programme where local creators get early access to new flavours, a behind-the-scenes look at production, or a free box in exchange for content. You are not paying for ads. You are fuelling the community that is already championing your brand.

  • Create a "Deep North Creators" list of local food content creators who already post about you
  • Invite them for early-access tasting sessions before seasonal launches
  • Repost their content on your official accounts with credit, building a collaborative relationship
  • The result: a constant stream of high-quality content from multiple perspectives, all organic

Delivery and Subscription Exploration

A "Deep North Doughnut Box" subscription service, even starting small with a weekly or monthly option, could open up a new revenue channel. Pre-orders for seasonal drops, corporate gift boxes, and next-day delivery to the wider North East are all possibilities that the brand can support. Each of these also generates its own content: unboxing videos, gift reactions, and "what is in the box this month" reveals.
