Where The Opportunity Is
Five Things Holding You Back

The product, the reputation, and the personal brand are all strong. You have built a genuine community. The issue is that several platforms are significantly underperforming relative to what you have already proven you can do. Here is where the biggest gaps are.

1

TikTok Is Massively Underweight

30,500 followers on TikTok versus 108,000 on YouTube and 62,000 on Instagram. TikTok is getting the least attention of your three main platforms, and for a sport that skews younger and is exploding in the UK right now, that is a huge missed opportunity.

277,600 total likes across the entire account is low. For context, a single well-performing TikTok in the sports equipment space can do that in one video. Your content quality is clearly there, as proven by YouTube and Instagram. TikTok just needs a proper strategy and consistent posting cadence.

The second account @everythingpadel5 with 11,400 followers also creates confusion. Potential followers land on the wrong account, engagement is split, and it weakens your brand presence. That needs consolidating.

2

Instagram Engagement Does Not Match the Follower Count

62,000 followers is a strong number. But when a recent post gets 172 likes and 6 comments, that is an engagement rate well below 1%. For an account your size in a niche sport, you should be consistently hitting 500-1,500 likes per post.

The content mix is good in terms of variety, but the strategy could be sharper. More interactive content like polls, Q&As, this-or-that comparisons between rackets, and community challenges would wake up the existing audience. You have 62,000 people who chose to follow you. Most of them are not seeing your posts because engagement signals to the algorithm that the content is not compelling enough to push further.

The audience is already there. They already care about padel. The content just needs to be engineered to drive more interaction, not just views.

3

Facebook Is Almost Invisible

1,350 likes on Facebook with "12 people talking about this." That is essentially a dead platform for you. For comparison, you have 62,000 on Instagram and 108,000 on YouTube.

Facebook might not be where your core audience discovers you, but it is where a significant portion of padel club members, league organisers, and older players spend time. Local padel groups on Facebook are growing rapidly. If you are not showing up in those conversations, your competitors will.

The decision here is binary: either invest properly in Facebook with community-led content and group engagement, or deprioritise it entirely and redirect that energy into TikTok. Either way, the current approach of mirroring a few Instagram posts is worse than doing nothing, because it makes the brand look inactive.

4

Blog Content Is Outdated

You have buying guides and racket comparison articles on the website, which is exactly the right type of content for SEO and conversion. But some of these reference 2021 and 2023 products. In a fast-moving sport where new racket models drop every season, outdated buying guides actively hurt credibility.

Someone landing on a "Best Padel Rackets 2023" article in 2026 immediately questions whether the store is still current. Every one of those articles should be refreshed with 2026 products, updated prices, and current recommendations. You already have the product knowledge from your YouTube reviews. The content just needs to be translated into updated written form.

This is also a missed SEO opportunity. "Best padel racket 2026" and "padel racket buying guide 2026" are search terms with growing volume in the UK, and you should be ranking for all of them.

5

No Visible Paid Advertising

We checked the Facebook and Instagram Ad Library. No ads running. For an e-commerce business selling products with healthy margins and a proven organic content machine, paid social is the accelerant you are missing.

You already know what content resonates because you have years of YouTube and Instagram data. Taking your best-performing organic content and putting paid spend behind it is the fastest way to reach new padel players who have never heard of Everything Padel. Retargeting website visitors who browsed but did not buy, seasonal campaigns around new racket launches, and lookalike audiences based on your existing customers would all drive direct revenue.

Ready-Made Content
5 Video Ideas You Could Film Today

These are formats that are working right now in the sports and equipment space. All of them play to your strengths as a player, coach, and retailer.

Idea 01
"I played with the cheapest vs most expensive racket in my store"
Comparison Product Entertainment

Take a beginner racket and a top-end model onto court. Play the same shots with both. Show the genuine difference in control, power, and spin. Be honest about whether the expensive one is worth it. End with your actual recommendation.

Why it works: Cheap vs expensive is one of the highest-performing formats on TikTok and Reels. It triggers debate in the comments, drives saves from people researching purchases, and positions you as an honest expert rather than someone just trying to sell the expensive option.
Idea 02
"POV: You just ordered from Everything Padel"
Behind the Scenes Unboxing Brand

Film the full journey of an order. The notification comes in. Walk to the warehouse. Pick the racket. Add the free gifts. Package it up. Write the note. Drop it at the courier. Cut to the customer opening it. Show why Trustpilot says 4.9 stars.

Why it works: Behind-the-scenes e-commerce content humanises the brand. It shows care and quality. The "free gifts" reveal creates a moment that makes viewers want to order just to see what they get. It turns your customer service into content.
Idea 03
"3 shots every beginner gets wrong (and how to fix them)"
Educational Coaching Tips

Quick-cut coaching content. Show the common mistake, then the correct technique. Use slow motion on the racket face. Keep it under 60 seconds. This is the kind of content people save, share with their playing partners, and come back to before their next game.

Why it works: Educational content in niche sports has the highest save rate of any format. Saves signal quality to the algorithm. Every save is someone who will remember your brand when they need equipment. You are a qualified coach, so this is uniquely authentic to you.
Idea 04
"I asked 100 padel players what racket they use"
Community Survey Engagement

Go to a club, tournament, or league night. Film quick clips asking people what racket they play with and why. Tally the results. Show the winner. Tag the brands. Let the comments section debate whether the right racket won.

Why it works: Survey and ranking content drives massive comment engagement because everyone has an opinion. It creates natural debate, which the algorithm rewards. It also subtly showcases the range of brands you stock without being salesy.
Idea 05
"What 1 year of padel looks like - beginner to intermediate"
Transformation Aspirational Journey

Compile footage from a beginner's first session through to a year later. Show the awkward first serves, the missed volleys, and then the progression to confident rallies and match play. Use a trending audio track. Make it aspirational for anyone thinking about starting.

Why it works: Transformation content is universally compelling. For a growing sport, showing that progression is achievable inspires new players to start, and every new player needs equipment. This sells the sport and the brand simultaneously.
Digital Enhancements
Beyond Social Media

These are strategic changes that would make a real difference alongside the social media work.

Refresh Blog Content for SEO

Your buying guides and comparison articles are the right type of content, but they reference outdated products from 2021-2023. Refreshing these with 2026 models, current prices, and updated recommendations would immediately improve search rankings.

  • "Best padel racket 2026" is a growing search term in the UK with relatively low competition
  • Your YouTube reviews already contain the product knowledge needed to update these articles
  • Each refreshed article should link directly to the relevant product pages on your store

Quick win: Update your top 5 buying guides with 2026 products and you could be ranking for high-intent purchase keywords within weeks. Every article is a potential customer who was actively searching for what you sell.

Consolidate TikTok Accounts

Having @everythingpadel (30,500 followers) and @everythingpadel5 (11,400 followers) splits your audience, confuses potential followers, and weakens your brand presence on the platform.

Pin a video on the secondary account directing followers to the main account, then stop posting to it. All future TikTok energy should go into one account with one clear identity.

Email Capture and Nurture

With the traffic you are driving from YouTube and Instagram, an email list would give you a direct line to customers that does not depend on any algorithm. Post-purchase sequences, new product launches, exclusive discount codes for subscribers, and seasonal buying guides sent via email would drive repeat purchases.

The racket quiz is a perfect email capture point. "Get your personalised racket recommendation" in exchange for an email address. You are already doing the quiz. You just need to capture the lead.
