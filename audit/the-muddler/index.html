Where The Opportunity Is
Three Areas With Real Growth Potential

You have a strong product, a prime location, and an active Instagram. These opportunities are about taking what you have already built and pushing it to the next level.

1

No Owned TikTok When Massive UGC Is Being Created

This is the single biggest opportunity. Food bloggers are already creating TikTok content about The Muddler, and that content is generating views and engagement. But all of those views, all of those followers, and all of that momentum are building on other people's accounts. Not yours.

An official TikTok account would let you own the conversation, build a following, and convert that organic buzz into direct bookings. You could also collaborate with the food bloggers who are already posting about you, amplifying their content while building your own audience. Pan-Asian food is one of the most visually engaging cuisines on social media, and The Muddler's cocktails add another content dimension. The potential is huge.

2

TripAdvisor #121 Could Be Much Higher

Being ranked #121 out of 1,163 restaurants in Newcastle puts you in the top 11%, which is respectable. But with the quality of your food, a prime Grey Street location, and the volume of positive feedback, there is room to climb significantly. Higher-ranked restaurants often have a more systematic approach to encouraging reviews and responding to every single one.

A focused review generation strategy, combined with responding to every review (especially recent ones), would push The Muddler up the rankings. Breaking into the top 50 would meaningfully increase the number of people who discover you through TripAdvisor, particularly tourists and visitors to Newcastle who rely heavily on rankings.

3

Instagram Needs More Video Content

With 12K followers and 1,300+ posts, the commitment to Instagram is clear. The opportunity now is in the type of content. Instagram in 2026 heavily prioritises Reels over static images. Short-form video content reaches significantly more people than photos, and it is what drives new follower growth.

A shift towards more Reels, showcasing the food, the cocktails, the 1920s interior, and the Grey Street location, would accelerate growth and drive more direct engagement. The visual appeal of Pan-Asian small plates and creative cocktails like the Dragon Fruit Mojito and Szechuan Collins are perfect for video content.

Ready-Made Content
5 Video Ideas You Could Film Today

These are formats that are performing right now for restaurants on TikTok and Instagram. All of them play to The Muddler's strengths as a Pan-Asian restaurant on one of Newcastle's most famous streets.

Idea 01
"We ordered the entire small plates menu at The Muddler"
Menu Showcase Variety Visual

Table loaded with every small plate. Quick cuts of each dish arriving, close-ups of the colours and textures, reactions to each bite. The variety and visual appeal of Pan-Asian small plates makes this content gold. End with an overhead shot of the full spread on the table.

Why it works: "We ordered everything" is one of the most viral restaurant formats on social media. The sheer variety and colour of Pan-Asian small plates makes it visually stunning. People save these videos, tag friends, and plan visits. The comments section becomes a conversation about which dishes to try.
Idea 02
"A Bento Box lunch for under 14 pounds on Grey Street"
Value Hook Lunch Deal Local

Walk into The Muddler on a lunchtime. Sit down. Show the Bento Box arriving: the presentation, the variety, the quality. Close-up of every element. End with the price on screen. "All of this. 13.95. Grey Street. You are welcome."

Why it works: Price reveal content consistently goes viral. People are always searching for good-value lunches in the city centre, and showing the quality you get for under 14 pounds creates instant demand. This video would drive lunchtime bookings directly and get saved by people planning their next work lunch or city visit.
Idea 03
"The Dragon Fruit Mojito might be the best cocktail in Newcastle"
Cocktails Colour Signature

Close-up of the bartender making the Dragon Fruit Mojito. The muddling, the pour, the vivid pink colour, the garnish. Slow, satisfying shots. Then show the Szechuan Collins for contrast. Bold, colourful, unique. Let the visuals carry the video.

Why it works: Colourful cocktails are some of the most saved and shared content on social media. The Dragon Fruit Mojito is inherently eye-catching, and pairing it with a Szechuan Collins creates a "which would you pick?" dynamic that drives comments and shares. Cocktail content brings in customers who might not have been searching for a restaurant but end up booking a table anyway.
Idea 04
"Pre-theatre dinner on Grey Street, right opposite Theatre Royal"
Pre-Theatre Location Experience

Start outside Theatre Royal. Cross the street. Walk into The Muddler. Show the pre-theatre menu arriving quickly. Time-lapse of the meal. Walk back across to the theatre with time to spare. "Pre-theatre sorted. Grey Street." This sells convenience, quality, and location in one video.

Why it works: People going to the theatre actively search for nearby restaurants. This video directly answers that search and positions The Muddler as the obvious choice. It would perform well both organically and as a promoted piece during theatre season, reaching people who are already planning their evening out.
Idea 05
"Inside Newcastle's most beautiful 1920s restaurant"
Interior Atmosphere Discovery

A slow, cinematic tour of The Muddler's 1920s interior. The details, the fixtures, the bar, the lighting, the seating. Let the atmosphere build. Layer in shots of food and drinks being served in the space. End with a wide shot of the restaurant in full swing. No voiceover, just a moody, elegant audio track.

Why it works: Venue reveal content performs exceptionally well because people choose restaurants based on atmosphere as much as food. The 1920s interior is a genuine differentiator, and most people walking past on Grey Street have no idea what is inside. This video changes that and positions The Muddler as a destination, not just a restaurant.
Digital Enhancements
Beyond Social Media

These are strategic changes that would complement the social media work and strengthen your overall digital presence.

Launch an Official TikTok Account

This is the highest-priority action. Food bloggers are already building an audience around The Muddler on TikTok. You need to join that conversation and lead it. An official account lets you own the narrative, build a following, and convert views into bookings.

  • Set up the account with consistent branding matching your Instagram
  • Reach out to food bloggers who have already posted about you for collaborations
  • Post 3 to 5 times per week to build momentum quickly
  • Focus on visually striking content: sushi, cocktails, plating, the 1920s interior

Quick win: Duet or stitch the best existing UGC TikToks about The Muddler on day one. This gives you instant content and builds goodwill with creators who are already fans.

TripAdvisor Review Strategy

Climbing from #121 to the top 50 would make a noticeable difference in visibility. A systematic approach to encouraging reviews, responding to every review promptly, and addressing any recurring feedback themes would push the ranking up over time. Every positive review is an investment in long-term discoverability.

Leverage the Theatre Royal Proximity

Being directly opposite Theatre Royal is a competitive advantage that should be featured in every piece of marketing. Google Ads targeting "pre-theatre dinner Newcastle," dedicated landing page content, and social media posts timed around major shows would all drive incremental bookings from theatre-goers who are actively looking for somewhere to eat.
