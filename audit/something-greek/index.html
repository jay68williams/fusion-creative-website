Where The Opportunity Is
Five Areas With Real Potential

Something Greek has a brilliant product. These are the five areas where focused effort would turn a well-loved local takeaway into a destination people drive to from across the region.

1

Social Media Is Almost Invisible

187 Instagram followers and 126 on TikTok with only 6 videos posted. For a food business with reviews this strong, that is a massive missed opportunity. Greek food is one of the most visually satisfying cuisines on social media. Gyros being carved, halloumi frying until golden, tzatziki being drizzled over a loaded plate. This is the exact content that goes viral on food TikTok every single day.

The gap between how good your food is and how many people know about it online is enormous. Closing that gap is the single biggest growth lever available to you right now. One well-filmed gyro video could reach more people in a week than your shop sees in a year.

2

No Branded Website, Just an Ordering Platform

somethinggreekdh6.co.uk is a FeedMeOnline ordering page. It does the job for placing orders, but it does nothing for your brand. There is no story, no photography, no menu presentation that makes someone think "I need to try this place." When someone searches for Greek food near Durham, you need to show up with something that looks as good as the food tastes.

A proper branded site with professional food photography, your story, and a direct ordering link would convert browsers into customers. It also gives you a home base for SEO so you rank for "Greek food Durham," "gyros near me," and "halloumi fries County Durham." Right now, that search traffic is going nowhere.

3

TikTok Is Completely Untapped

6 videos and 126 followers. Greek food content is one of the best performing niches on TikTok. Gyro compilation videos regularly pull millions of views. Halloumi cheese pulls, meat being carved off the spit, sauces being drizzled. This is content people watch on repeat, save, and send to friends. You are sitting on a content goldmine and barely touching it.

The benchmark is clear: post three to four short-form videos per week, each under 30 seconds, focused purely on the food. No complicated production, just clean close-ups with good lighting and trending audio. Within 90 days of consistent posting, you would be reaching tens of thousands of people who have never heard of Coxhoe but would drive there for a gyro that looks like yours.

4

Duplicate Facebook Pages Are Confusing Customers

There are at least two Facebook pages for Something Greek. One appears to be a newer business page, the other an older personal or community page. When a customer searches for you on Facebook, they do not know which one is real. That erodes trust, splits your audience, and means any reviews or check-ins are scattered across two places instead of building on one.

Merging or removing the duplicate page and consolidating everything under one verified business page is a quick win. It also unlocks Facebook and Instagram integration so you can cross-post, run ads, and track insights from one place.

5

Review Volume Is Low on TripAdvisor

12 reviews on TripAdvisor at 3.8 stars. That is a decent score but the sample size is tiny. A handful of bad reviews from delivery complaints could drag you below 3.5, which is the threshold where people start filtering you out. Meanwhile, Restaurant Guru has 220 reviews proving people love the food.

A simple review generation push, a QR code on the counter, a "loved your meal? Leave us a review" card in the bag, would move TripAdvisor to 4.5+ within two months. That is especially important for the tourist and visitor crowd in Durham who default to TripAdvisor when looking for somewhere to eat.

Ready-Made Content
4 Video Ideas You Could Film This Week

These are formats that are working right now on TikTok and Instagram Reels and that are tailored specifically to a Greek street food takeaway. Film any one of these properly and it could be the video that puts Something Greek on the map.

Idea 01
"How we build the perfect chicken gyro, step by step"
Process Satisfying ASMR

Close-up camera. Start with the warm pita. Layer the chicken carved fresh off the spit. Add the salad, the red onion, the tomato. Drizzle the tzatziki. Wrap it tight. Cut it in half to reveal the cross-section. Hold the hero shot for two seconds. Trending audio or pure ASMR sound. Text overlay: "Our chicken gyro. Made fresh every time."

Why it works: Gyro assembly videos are one of the most reliably viral formats in food content. The layering, the drizzle, the cross-section reveal. People cannot scroll past it. This is also the video that answers the question "what does their food actually look like?" for every potential customer who finds you online. It is both a brand video and a conversion tool.
Idea 02
"The halloumi fries that have 220 five-star reviews"
Hero Product Close-Up Cheese Pull

Halloumi going into the fryer. Sizzle. Golden brown coming out. Stack them on the plate. Slow motion cheese pull as you break one in half. Dip into the sauce. Text overlay: "Halloumi fries. Coxhoe, County Durham. Yes, really." Trending audio. Keep it under 15 seconds.

Why it works: Cheese pull content is one of the highest-engagement food formats on both platforms. The "Yes, really" hook in the text creates curiosity because people do not expect this kind of food from a County Durham village, which makes them save it, share it, and tell their friends. The location surprise is part of the hook.
Idea 03
"POV: you order the mixed box and this is what arrives"
POV Unboxing Portion Flex

First person POV. Open the bag. Pull out the box. Open the lid to reveal three portions of meat with seasoned chips, salad, tzatziki, the full spread. Camera slowly pans across the entire box. Reaction audio or trending sound. Text overlay: "The Something Greek mixed box. Under a tenner."

Why it works: Food unboxing and portion-size reveals are huge engagement drivers. When the portion is generous and the price is right, people tag their friends and say "we need to go here." The value angle is especially powerful because it positions Something Greek as the place that gives you more than you expected. That is the kind of reputation that drives word of mouth both online and off.
Idea 04
"The story behind Something Greek: how a village takeaway became the best gyro in Durham"
Origin Story Founder Personality

You, Dimitrios, talking to camera. Keep it raw and honest. Why Greek food? Why Coxhoe? What makes your gyros different? Cut between your words and shots of the food being prepared. Show the passion. Show the kitchen. Show the queue outside on a Friday night. Text overlay: "Something Greek. Our story." Trending emotional audio underneath.

Why it works: People follow people, not businesses. A founder story humanises the brand and creates an emotional connection that no food shot can match. Once your audience knows your name and your story, they are not just ordering a gyro. They are supporting Dimitrios and the dream behind Something Greek. That is the difference between a customer and a fan.

Beyond Social Media

The FeedMeOnline ordering page is functional but it is not doing anything for your brand. A proper site with professional food photography, your menu presented beautifully, your story, and a direct link to order would convert significantly more browsers into customers.

Quick win: Even a one-page site with a hero image, the menu, and an order button, launched this month, would immediately improve your Google search ranking for "Greek food near me" in the Durham area.
