What Is Already Working Well
Credit Where It Is Due

✓ The brand has genuine heritage and credibility

Over 40 years in luxury fashion retail, featured in Vogue, a Drapers Lifetime Achievement Award, and six physical stores. That is not something you can fake or fast-track. It is the kind of trust that, once shown off properly on social, makes everything else land harder.

✓ The curation is a content goldmine

Over 180 brands, hand-picked by your buyers. That is 180 reasons to post, 180 stories to tell, and a constant stream of "what is new this week" content. Most fashion retailers post product shots. Jules B can tell the story behind why each brand made the edit.

✓ You have beautiful physical stores

Six stores means six sets for filming. The Jesmond store alone is a content backdrop most fashion brands would pay for. In-store content, styling sessions, new arrivals unpacked on camera, try-on hauls surrounded by real product: that is the content people stop scrolling for.

✓ The editorial eye is already there

Your website and Instagram already have a polished, editorial feel. The photography is strong, the styling is considered, and the tone is right. The foundation is solid. The next step is translating that same eye into short-form video, where the reach lives.

✓ E-commerce is strong

A well-built online store with international shipping means that when content does travel, the path to purchase is already there. You are not building from scratch. The infrastructure is ready, it just needs more people finding it through social discovery.

Where The Opportunity Is
Five Things We Would Focus On

Jules B already has the heritage, the brands and the stores. The job is to turn that into reach and revenue: get in front of fashion-conscious shoppers who have never walked into a Jules B store (or visited the website), and make the brand impossible to scroll past. These are the five areas we would focus on.

1

Turn New Arrivals Into Weekly Content Events

With 180+ brands and constant new drops, you have a built-in content calendar that never runs dry. A weekly "what just landed" reel, filmed in store, showing the standout pieces styled and on a person rather than a hanger, gives followers a reason to check back every week and gives the algorithm a consistent signal.

This is the single fastest way to make your Instagram and TikTok feel alive rather than like a lookbook.

2

Let People See Behind the Buying

Independent retailers have one thing the big chains never will: a real person choosing every brand on the floor. The story of why a brand made the Jules B edit, what your buyers spotted at a trade show, what nearly made the cut but did not, that is the kind of insider content that builds loyalty and positions you as the authority rather than just another stockist.

It also differentiates Jules B from every other retailer carrying the same labels. The brands are the same. The curation is yours.

3

Use TikTok as a Discovery Engine

TikTok is where fashion discovery happens now. Outfit inspiration, styling tips, hauls, "what I would wear" and brand deep-dives are some of the highest-performing content categories on the platform. Jules B has the product range, the stores and the expertise to own this space locally and beyond.

The TikTok presence is early, which means there is a clear runway to build it properly from the start rather than retrofitting later.

4

Put the Team and the Stores on Camera

People buy from people, especially in luxury fashion. The staff who know the product, the store managers with their own style, the founders who built the business over four decades: that is the personality that turns followers into customers. A face on camera builds familiarity, and familiarity is what makes someone choose Jules B over ordering from a faceless marketplace.

Personality-led content is consistently the highest-performing format in fashion retail on both Instagram and TikTok.

5

Short-Form Video for Reach, Targeted Ads for Conversion

Organic short-form video builds the audience and the brand recognition. Pair that with a targeted ad strategy, retargeting people who have engaged with your content and lookalike audiences of your existing customers, and you turn that attention into website visits and sales.

For a retailer with a strong e-commerce platform already in place, the path from "saw a reel" to "placed an order" is short, which makes the return on content and ads very direct.

Ready-Made Content
4 Video Ideas You Could Film This Week

These are formats working right now on TikTok and Instagram Reels, built around what Jules B already has: the brands, the stores, the styling expertise and the people. Each one could be filmed in store on a phone.

Idea 01
"What just landed at Jules B"
New ArrivalsWeeklyDiscovery

A fast, visually led reel of three to five new pieces that just hit the floor. Styled, on a person, filmed in store. Strong hook on frame one ("these just arrived and they will not last"). Trending audio. Under 30 seconds. A weekly appointment your followers look forward to.

Why it works: It creates a reason to follow, a reason to check back, and a direct path to purchase. New arrivals content is one of the highest-performing formats in fashion retail.
Idea 02
"Why we picked this brand"
BuyerBehind the ScenesAuthority

A buyer or store manager on camera, holding a piece, explaining what made a brand earn its place at Jules B. Keep it honest, keep it short. "We have carried Paul Smith since 1992. Here is why." The insider perspective no chain store can replicate.

Why it works: It positions Jules B as the authority, not just a stockist. It builds trust, differentiates from competitors carrying the same labels, and gives the audience a reason to buy from you specifically.
Idea 03
"3 ways to style this jacket"
StylingOutfitShoppable

Take one hero piece and show three complete outfits built around it, from casual to dressed up. Quick cuts, good music, each look tagged with the brands. Filmed on a real person in the store, not on a mannequin. Practical, watchable, and built to be saved.

Why it works: Styling content is the most saved and shared format in fashion. It helps the viewer picture themselves in the outfit, which is the step right before they buy.
Idea 04
"40 years of Jules B in 40 seconds"
HeritageFounderStory

A fast-paced montage of the Jules B story: archive photos, the first store, the brands that have come and gone, the team today, the Jesmond shop floor. Overlay with a voiceover or text. A brand story that no competitor can match, told in a format built to travel.

Why it works: Heritage is rare. Most fashion brands on social are new and disposable. Showing four decades of staying power, while the trends changed around you, builds the kind of trust that converts browsers into loyal customers.
Digital Enhancements
Beyond the Main Feed

A few extra wins that would compound the content work and turn more of your visibility into website visits and sales.

Influencer and Styling Collaborations

Jules B carries brands that fashion influencers already wear and talk about. Structured collaborations, whether in-store styling sessions filmed as content, or gifted pieces with a brief, let you tap into established audiences that already trust the recommender.

  • Partner with North East and national fashion creators who align with your brand positioning
  • Film collaborations in store so the content doubles as a Jules B showcase
  • Repurpose collaboration content across your own channels

Leverage Trustpilot and Customer Reviews

You have a substantial review base on Trustpilot. Turn the best reviews into social content: screenshot reels, "our customers said this" story highlights, and review-led ads. Real customer voices are more persuasive than any copy you could write.

  • Turn five-star reviews into branded quote graphics or short video overlays
  • Use customer reviews in ad creative for social proof at the point of conversion
  • Encourage post-purchase reviews with a follow-up flow

Seasonal and Sale Campaigns as Content Moments

Sales and seasonal launches are natural content peaks. Rather than just updating the website banner, treat each one as a content event: a behind-the-scenes "setting up the sale" reel, a "what I would buy in the sale" pick from a team member, a countdown to new season arrivals.

  • Build anticipation with teaser content before each drop or sale launch
  • Film the team preparing for the sale or unpacking new season stock
  • Post "staff picks" to personalise the moment and drive urgency

Why it fits Jules B: You already have these calendar moments built into the retail cycle. Turning them into content events costs nothing extra and drives traffic at the exact point when purchase intent is highest.
