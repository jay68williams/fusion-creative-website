What Is Already Working Well
Credit Where It Is Due

✓ A five-star reputation that most places never earn

A five-star Just Eat rating with thousands of reviews, strong TripAdvisor feedback, and a 4.7 on Restaurant Guru with over a thousand reviews. That level of consistency across multiple platforms is genuinely rare. People do not just like the food, they go out of their way to say so.

✓ You are known for something specific

The Sunday carvery is the thing people talk about. "Best carvery you will find" is an actual customer review. Having a signature dish that people associate with your name is exactly the kind of identity that travels on social, because it gives people a reason to share it.

✓ Delivery is properly set up

You are on Just Eat, Deliveroo, FoodHouse and your own app. That is a serious delivery operation for a three-day-a-week kitchen, and it means you are already reaching customers beyond the pub itself. The infrastructure is there.

✓ A strong Facebook following for a local pub

Over 8,600 likes and more than 5,200 check-ins on Facebook is a solid local audience. People already follow you. The job is giving them something that makes them share you with the people who have not found you yet.

✓ The posting habit is already there

Over 5,000 Instagram posts is a serious commitment. The discipline of showing up consistently is the hardest part for most businesses, and you have done it for years. The issue is not effort. It is format, and format is the easy thing to fix.

Where The Opportunity Is
Five Things We Would Focus On

You already have the reputation and the loyal customers. The job is to turn that into reach, getting in front of the people in Wallsend and across the North East who have not tried your food yet, without it eating into your time. These are the five areas we would focus on.

1

Short-Form Video of the Food and the Kitchen

A carvery is made for video. The meat being carved, the gravy being poured, the plate being loaded up. That kind of food content is what stops the scroll on TikTok and Instagram Reels, and it is the single fastest way to turn a loyal local following into a much wider audience. Right now, none of that is being captured as video content.

One short video of a Sunday carvery plate being built, filmed on a busy service, has more reach potential than a hundred photo posts. The food already looks incredible. It just needs to move.

2

Get on TikTok

Jillys is not on TikTok at all right now, and that is a genuine gap. TikTok is the platform where local food businesses blow up, because the algorithm pushes content to people nearby regardless of follower count. A pub carvery with five-star reviews and proper home-cooked food is exactly the kind of thing TikTok users share and save.

You do not need to be on camera or learn the platform yourself. We would handle the account, the filming and the posting. The opportunity is simply too big to leave on the table.

3

Turn Reviews Into Content

You have thousands of five-star reviews across Just Eat, TripAdvisor and Google. That is your best marketing, and right now it is buried on review sites where only people who are already looking for you will see it. Turning those reviews into short, shareable content (a great quote over footage of the dish, a quick "what our customers say" reel) puts that proof in front of people who have not heard of you yet.

Social proof is the most persuasive thing a food business can post, and you have more of it than almost anyone.

4

Convert Instagram Effort Into Reach

Over 5,000 posts and around 546 followers tells you the effort is right but the format is not. Photo-led menu posts and text updates do not get picked up by the algorithm. Switching to short-form video with proper hooks, trending audio and food-first visuals would turn the same amount of posting into significantly more reach, without posting any more often.

The habit is already there. The fix is switching from posts that inform to content that travels.

5

Make the Weekend a Content Event

You open Friday, Saturday and Sunday. That three-day window is actually an advantage for content, because it creates natural anticipation and urgency. "We are open this weekend" is a weekly event people can look forward to, not just an announcement. The Friday build-up, the Saturday buzz, the Sunday carvery in full swing, each day is a content moment.

A concentrated schedule means concentrated energy, and that is exactly what works on social. The busiest, most atmospheric footage comes from those three days, and that is all you need.

Ready-Made Content
4 Video Ideas You Could Film This Weekend

These are formats working right now on TikTok and Instagram Reels, built around what you already do: the carvery, the kitchen, the food and the pub. Each one could be filmed during a single service.

Idea 01
"This is a proper Sunday carvery"
FoodCarverySignature dish

A fast, satisfying build-up of a carvery plate: the meat being carved, the veg going on, the Yorkshire pudding, the gravy pour. Strong hook on frame one ("This is what a five-star carvery looks like"). Trending audio. Under 20 seconds. Pure food content that stops the scroll.

Why it works: Food being plated up is one of the most saved and shared formats on TikTok and Reels. A proper carvery plate is visually satisfying and makes people hungry, which is exactly what you want.
Idea 02
"5 stars on Just Eat. Here is why."
ProofReviewsBehind the scenes

Open with a screenshot of the five-star rating, then cut to the kitchen: the fresh prep, the home cooking, the care that goes into it. Overlay a few of the best review quotes. End on a finished plate going out. Let the reviews and the kitchen tell the story.

Why it works: It answers "is this place actually good?" with proof, not a promise. Combining real reviews with real kitchen footage is the most persuasive thing a food business can post.
Idea 03
"What goes into a Jillys roast"
ProcessHome-cookedStorytelling

A short journey from raw ingredients to finished plate: the meat going in, the veg being prepped, the gravy being made from scratch, the final plate. Show the home-cooked process that makes you different from a chain pub carvery. No narration needed, just the sounds of the kitchen and a clean edit.

Why it works: "Made from scratch" content performs brilliantly because it shows the effort behind the food. It separates you from every frozen-and-reheated pub chain, and people share it because it feels real.
Idea 04
"Friday, Saturday, Sunday. That is it."
BrandUrgencyPersonality

A quick, punchy reel that leans into the three-day schedule as a feature, not a limitation. Shots of the pub packed on a Friday night, the kitchen flat out on Saturday, the carvery queue on Sunday. The message: this is not a seven-day-a-week chain, this is a local spot that opens three days and fills up every time.

Why it works: Scarcity creates urgency. Framing a limited schedule as exclusive rather than restrictive makes people feel like they need to get in before the weekend is over, and it is the kind of thing people tag their mates in.
Digital Enhancements
Beyond the Main Feed

A few extra wins that would compound the content work and turn more of your local visibility into orders and covers.

Google Business Profile

When someone searches "carvery near me" or "takeaway Wallsend", Google decides who shows up. A well-maintained Google Business Profile with fresh photos, regular posts and a steady stream of reviews is the simplest way to win that search.

  • Keep the profile updated with your Friday to Sunday hours and seasonal menus
  • Add fresh photos of the food regularly (the algorithm rewards it)
  • Encourage happy customers to leave a Google review at the point of sale
  • Turn your best Google reviews into shareable social content

Drive Delivery Orders Through Content

You are already on Just Eat, Deliveroo, FoodHouse and your own app. Short-form video can drive orders directly, by showing the food being made, packed and delivered. A reel that ends with "order now on Just Eat" or "download our app" turns a view into a sale.

  • Film the packing and dispatch process for delivery orders
  • Show what a Jillys delivery looks like when it arrives
  • Use your five-star Just Eat rating as a hook in every delivery-focused reel

A Simple Website That Converts

Your current site handles online ordering well through FoodHouse. The next step is making sure people who find you through social can land on a page that shows off the food, the reviews and the story, then funnels them straight to an order or a table booking.

  • A clean landing page built around the food, the five-star reviews and a clear order button
  • Link it from every social bio so content converts into orders
