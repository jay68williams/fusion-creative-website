Where The Opportunity Is
Five Areas With Real Potential

ABOVE has a lot going for it. The gaps are not about the venue or the experience. They are about how the story is being told online. Fix these and the growth will follow.

1

Engagement Is Low Relative to 18K Followers

18K followers is great on paper, but if the content is not driving likes, comments, saves, and shares, the algorithm buries it. Right now, most of your posts are polished photos and reposts. They look nice, but they do not stop people scrolling. Instagram in 2026 rewards personality-driven video content with strong hooks in the first second. That is what gets pushed to the Explore page and into new people's feeds.

The fix is not complicated. It means shifting from "pretty pictures of cocktails" to "content people actually want to watch and share." Staff on camera, behind-the-scenes moments, reaction content, POV formats. The venue looks incredible. You just need to show it in a way the algorithm actually rewards.

2

TikTok Is a Massive Missed Opportunity

A rooftop bar with 360-degree views of Newcastle is literally perfect TikTok content. Sunset timelapses, cocktail making, the reveal of stepping out onto the rooftop for the first time. These are formats that rack up millions of views for venues far less impressive than yours.

Right now, your TikTok has barely started. A handful of videos and a low follower count. The platform is still the easiest place to grow organically in hospitality. One good video of the view at golden hour with a trending sound could put ABOVE in front of hundreds of thousands of people overnight. Every day without a TikTok strategy is reach you are leaving on the table.

3

No UGC Strategy Despite a Huge Opportunity

People film themselves at rooftop bars. It is one of the most filmed categories in hospitality. Your customers are already creating content when they visit ABOVE. They are filming the view, the cocktails, the sunset. But there is no visible strategy for repurposing that content.

With 18K followers, you should be resharing customer Stories daily, running a branded hashtag, and encouraging UGC with prompts at the venue. Every customer video is free content and social proof. A simple system for collecting and reposting UGC would multiply your content output without filming a single extra thing.

4

Events Are Not Being Promoted Through Video

Sky High Cinema Club. Skyline Bottomless Brunch. Summer in the Sky. These are brilliant events that people would absolutely share on social media. But they are not being promoted through short-form video content. A 15-second Reel showing the cinema setup on the rooftop at sunset would sell more tickets than any static graphic ever could.

Each event should have a content series around it: teaser videos before, behind-the-scenes during, highlight reels after. That gives you a constant stream of content that promotes the events and fills seats, while also showing the personality and energy of ABOVE.

5

Review Narratives Need Countering With Content

Some of your TripAdvisor reviews mention slow service and loud music. Whether that is fair or not does not matter. What matters is that people read those reviews before deciding to visit. The best way to counter negative review narratives is not by arguing online. It is by creating content that shows the opposite.

Videos of staff being attentive and friendly, cocktails arriving quickly, customers having genuine fun, the atmosphere looking right. Content that shows the real experience overwrites negative perceptions far more effectively than a management response ever could. Show, do not tell.

Ready-Made Content
4 Video Ideas You Could Film This Week

These are formats that are working right now on TikTok and Instagram Reels. All of them are built around what makes ABOVE unique. Film any one of these properly and it could outperform everything you have posted in the last year.

Idea 01
"POV: You step out onto Newcastle's only rooftop bar for the first time"
Reveal POV Discovery

Start in the lift or walking through the corridor. Build the anticipation. Then the door opens and you step out onto the rooftop. Wide shot of the 360-degree view over Newcastle at sunset. Hold on the view for a beat. Cut to a cocktail being placed on the bar with the skyline behind it. Text overlay: "Newcastle's only rooftop bar." Trending audio underneath.

Why it works: Reveal content is one of the highest-performing formats on TikTok and Reels. The anticipation of "what is behind this door" keeps people watching. The payoff of that rooftop view is genuinely jaw-dropping. This is the kind of video that gets shared in group chats with "we need to go here." One well-filmed version of this could hit six figures in views easily.
Idea 02
"Ranking every cocktail on the menu from worst to best"
Staff Ranking Personality

Get two or three members of staff on camera. Line up every cocktail on the menu. Taste each one and rank them honestly. Let them disagree. Let them have opinions. Film it fast, cut it tight. Each cocktail gets a quick reaction and a score. The "worst" one still looks incredible. The best one gets the slow-motion hero shot with the Newcastle skyline behind it.

Why it works: Ranking content drives massive engagement because people want to argue. "How is that at the bottom?!" Comments like that are algorithm gold. It shows off the full menu, puts your staff's personalities front and centre, and makes the audience feel like they know the team. It is also a series, because you can do cocktails, bar snacks, even customer favourites vs staff favourites.
Idea 03
"What does 50 quid get you at Newcastle's highest bar?"
Value Challenge Relatable

Start with the concept: "I have got 50 pounds to spend at the highest bar in Newcastle. Let us see what I can get." Order cocktails, a light bite, maybe a second round. Show every item with a running total on screen. Show the view between each order. End with the full spread laid out against the skyline and the total. Make it feel like a genuine experience someone could have on a night out.

Why it works: Money challenge content performs incredibly well because it answers a question everyone secretly has: "How much does it actually cost?" Some people think rooftop bars are out of their price range. This video either confirms good value or creates aspiration. Either way, it gets watched, shared, and commented on. It also directly addresses pricing concerns from reviews.
Idea 04
"Things people say when they see the view for the first time"
Reactions UGC Social Proof

Film genuine reactions of people stepping out onto the rooftop for the first time. You can do this as a compilation of real customer reactions or as a skit where staff act out the most common things people say. "Oh my God." "You can see everything from up here." "Why have I never been here before?" Mix in shots of the actual view between each reaction. Keep it fast-paced and fun.

Why it works: Reaction content is social proof in its purest form. When potential customers see real people being blown away by the view, they want to experience it themselves. It humanises the brand, it is endlessly repeatable as a series, and it positions ABOVE as a "must-visit" destination rather than just another bar. The reactions also counter any negative review narratives because they show real enjoyment.
Digital Enhancements
Beyond Social Media

These are strategic changes that would make a real difference alongside the content work.

Build a UGC Collection System

Your customers are already filming content at ABOVE. The view alone guarantees it. But without a system to collect and repurpose that content, it disappears into their personal feeds and you get zero benefit from it.

  • Create a branded hashtag and promote it at the venue (table cards, signage, menu inserts)
  • Reshare customer Stories to your own Stories daily to encourage more tagging
  • Run a monthly "best customer photo/video" feature to incentivise quality UGC
  • Use UGC in your Reels as social proof. Real customer reactions are more persuasive than any polished brand content

Quick win: Just adding "Tag us @abovencl for a chance to be featured" to your table cards would immediately increase the volume of customer content you can repurpose. Free content, free social proof.

Turn Events Into Content Machines

Sky High Cinema Club, Skyline Bottomless Brunch, and Summer in the Sky are all events that would look incredible on video. Each one should have a content cycle: teaser content before the event, behind-the-scenes and atmosphere content during it, and highlight reels after. That gives you a constant stream of content that promotes future events while showcasing the ABOVE experience.

  • Film a 15-second teaser for every upcoming event and post it a week in advance
  • Capture atmosphere content during events (crowd shots, reactions, the setup with the view)
  • Create "recap" Reels the day after each event to build FOMO for the next one
  • Use event content for paid ads targeting Newcastle nightlife audiences

Push TripAdvisor Rating and Ranking

4.0 out of 5 with 482 reviews is a solid base, but you are ranked #161 out of 1,163 restaurants in Newcastle. A focused review generation push could move that ranking significantly. More recent positive reviews also push down the visibility of older negative ones.

  • Add a QR code to receipts or table cards linking directly to the TripAdvisor review page
  • Train staff to mention reviews to happy customers at the right moment
  • Respond to every review, positive and negative, to show you are actively engaged
  • Target: push into the top 100 within 6 months with consistent review collection
