Where The Opportunity Is
Five Areas With Real Potential

Wren is only five months old, which means you are in the perfect position to build your digital presence the right way from the start. These are the areas where a focused push would have the biggest impact.

1

Build a Review Presence on TripAdvisor and Google

Right now, Wren has no TripAdvisor listing and no established Google review presence. For a new venue, this is completely normal, but the sooner you start building reviews, the sooner you start appearing in local search results. When someone searches "cocktail bars Newcastle" or "bars Ouseburn," Google and TripAdvisor show results based partly on review volume and ratings.

Setting up a TripAdvisor listing is free and straightforward. Encouraging every happy customer to leave a Google review builds your presence quickly. Within 3 to 6 months, a consistent effort could give you 50+ reviews across both platforms, which would put you on the map for anyone searching for a great bar in Newcastle.

2

Create a TikTok and Reels Strategy

Wren does not have a TikTok account yet, and Instagram is still in its early stages. This is actually an opportunity because you can build both from scratch with a clear strategy rather than trying to fix an inconsistent history. Cocktail content is one of the highest-performing categories on TikTok. The process of making a beautiful cocktail, the pour, the garnish, the presentation, is inherently satisfying and shareable.

A consistent posting schedule of 3 to 5 short videos per week across TikTok and Instagram Reels, using trending audio and cocktail-bar formats, could build a significant following quickly. The Ouseburn setting and Wren's design give you a visual advantage that most bars simply do not have.

3

Establish a Facebook Page

Facebook might feel less exciting than TikTok or Instagram, but it remains one of the most effective platforms for local businesses. A Facebook page allows you to appear in local searches, share events, collect reviews, and reach an older demographic that may not be active on Instagram. It also lets people check in when they visit, which creates organic reach to their entire friend network.

For a cocktail bar in the Ouseburn, Facebook Events are particularly valuable. Sharing special nights, seasonal menu launches, or live music evenings through Facebook Events drives direct attendance and appears in people's feeds as their friends express interest.

4

Leverage the Hidden Garden

A hidden garden in the Ouseburn is an incredible asset, especially as the weather improves. Outdoor spaces are highly sought after in Newcastle and they create some of the most shareable content on social media. "Hidden gem" and "secret garden" content formats perform exceptionally well because they trigger curiosity and discovery.

A video tour revealing the garden, seasonal content showing it in different weather and lighting, and customer photos shared from the space could generate significant reach. As summer approaches, this becomes your most powerful marketing asset. People actively search for outdoor drinking spots, and showing yours before they find a competitor's is essential.

5

Cross-Promote With Cook House

Cook House has an established audience who already trust the brand. Wren should be actively cross-promoting to that audience. Joint content, shared Stories, "after dinner at Cook House, head next door to Wren" messaging, and collaborative events would funnel Cook House's loyal customers directly to your door.

This is one of the easiest growth levers available to you. The audience is already warm. They already trust the team. They just need to know Wren exists and why they should visit. A deliberate cross-promotion strategy could drive immediate traffic without spending a penny on advertising.

Ready-Made Content
5 Video Ideas You Could Film Today

These are formats that are working right now for bars and venues on TikTok and Instagram. All of them play to Wren's strengths as a beautifully designed cocktail bar with a hidden garden in the Ouseburn.

Idea 01
"There is a hidden garden bar in the Ouseburn"
Discovery Reveal Location

Start outside on Foundry Lane. Walk towards the entrance. Step inside. Show the interior. Then the big reveal: walk through to the hidden garden. Slow pan across the outdoor space. Drinks on the table. Golden hour lighting. "You did not know this existed." End with the Wren logo or a simple text overlay with the address.

Why it works: "Hidden gem" and "secret spot" content is one of the highest-performing formats on TikTok. People love discovering places they did not know existed, especially in their own city. This single video could put Wren on the map for thousands of people in Newcastle who walk past the Ouseburn every day without knowing you are there.
Idea 02
"Watch our bartender make the best cocktail in Newcastle"
Craft Satisfying Product

Close-up on the bartender's hands. Ice going into the shaker. Spirit being measured and poured. The shake. The strain into a beautiful glass. The garnish being placed with care. A slow push-in on the finished cocktail. The first sip. No voiceover needed, just the sounds and a trending audio track.

Why it works: Cocktail-making content is endlessly rewatchable. The process is satisfying, the end result is beautiful, and it shows the skill and care behind every drink. This format positions Wren as a serious cocktail bar, not just another place to get a drink. It also creates a series opportunity, as every cocktail is a new video.
Idea 03
"POV: Friday evening in the Ouseburn starts here"
Lifestyle Atmosphere Social Proof

Capture the transition from quiet afternoon to buzzing Friday evening. The first customers arriving. Cocktails being poured. The garden filling up. Music playing. Friends laughing. The warm glow of the interior as it gets dark outside. Let the vibe tell the story. Use a warm, atmospheric soundtrack.

Why it works: People choose where to go on a Friday based on vibe. If they see a video of Wren looking warm, busy, and beautiful on a Friday evening, they want to be there. This creates fear of missing out and drives direct footfall. It also shows potential customers what the experience actually feels like, which is more powerful than any description.
Idea 04
"New season, new cocktails. Here is what is on the menu."
Menu Launch Seasonal Recurring

A quick walkthrough of the new seasonal menu. Each cocktail made and presented in quick succession. Name, key ingredients, and a beautiful shot of each one. Make it feel like an event. "The new spring menu just dropped at Wren." End with a call to action to come try them.

Why it works: Menu launches are built-in content moments that give you a reason to post and a reason for people to visit. Framing it as an "event" or a "drop" borrows from fashion and streetwear culture, which resonates with younger audiences. It creates urgency and excitement around something that might otherwise go unnoticed.
Idea 05
"The best bar snacks in Newcastle are not where you think"
Food Cook House Surprise

Most people go to a cocktail bar expecting average bar snacks. Show the reality at Wren: dishes coming out of the Cook House kitchen that look like they belong in a restaurant. The quality, the plating, the care. "These are bar snacks? At a cocktail bar? In the Ouseburn?" The contrast between expectation and reality is the hook.

Why it works: Expectation-versus-reality content is one of the most engaging formats on social media. The surprise of finding restaurant-quality food in a cocktail bar makes people stop scrolling. It also cross-promotes Cook House's reputation and differentiates Wren from every other bar that serves bowls of nuts and frozen pizza.
Digital Enhancements
Beyond Social Media

These are strategic changes that would make a real difference alongside the social media work.

Set Up TripAdvisor and Google Business

As a new venue, the sooner you establish review profiles on TripAdvisor and Google, the sooner you start appearing in search results. Both are free to set up and have a direct impact on discoverability.

  • Claim and optimise your Google Business Profile with professional photos, accurate hours, and your cocktail menu
  • Create a TripAdvisor listing and encourage early visitors to leave reviews
  • A QR code at the bar or on receipts linking to review pages makes it effortless for customers
  • Target: 30+ Google reviews within 3 months to establish a strong search presence

Quick win: Google Business Profile posts (weekly updates about events, new cocktails, or special nights) appear directly in search results and are free. This is one of the easiest wins for a new venue.

Create a Facebook Page and Events Strategy

A Facebook page gives you access to local search, check-ins, reviews, and most importantly, Facebook Events. For a bar in the Ouseburn, events are a powerful tool for driving attendance on specific nights. Seasonal menu launches, special tasting evenings, or weekend garden sessions can all be promoted through Events, which appear in people's feeds when friends show interest.

Summer Garden Campaign

With summer approaching, the hidden garden becomes your most powerful marketing asset. A dedicated campaign leading into the warmer months, showcasing the garden through video content, Stories, and targeted paid ads, would position Wren as the go-to outdoor drinking spot in the Ouseburn. People actively search for outdoor bars as soon as the weather turns. Being visible and top of mind before that moment is essential.
