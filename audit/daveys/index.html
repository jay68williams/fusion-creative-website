Where The Opportunity Is
Five Things Holding You Back

The brand, the audience, and the product are all strong. But there are some structural issues and strategic gaps that are limiting your growth. Here is where the biggest opportunities are.

1

Your Account Is Not Set Up as a Business Account

This is the easiest win on this entire list. Your Instagram is currently running as a personal account, not a business or creator account. That means you are missing out on Instagram Insights (detailed analytics on who your audience is, when they are online, which posts perform best), the ability to run ads directly from posts, contact buttons, and category labels.

Switching takes about 30 seconds in settings and costs nothing. But the data it unlocks is invaluable for making smarter content decisions. Right now you are flying blind on who your audience actually is and what content drives the most profile visits and website clicks.

2

Matcha and Coffee Content Is Dragging Down Your Average Engagement

Your burger content averages 250-400 likes. Your matcha and coffee content averages 24-85 likes. That is a 4-5x gap in performance between your strongest and weakest content categories.

This is not because matcha is bad content. It is because the format is wrong. Static photos of matcha lattes in a feed built around bold, high-energy burger content feel out of place. The matcha audience exists, but they need a different visual treatment. Think aesthetic Reels with the pour, the swirl, the colour reveal. Not just a static photo with a caption.

Low-performing posts signal to the algorithm that your content is not engaging, which reduces the reach of your next post, even if it is a banger burger shot. Every underperforming post drags down the ones that follow it.

3

Only 144 Posts with 38K Followers

38,000 followers from 144 posts is an incredible ratio. It shows that when you post, people respond. But 144 posts total means you are leaving a huge amount of potential reach on the table.

You are posting 3-4 times per week. For a food business with a menu as diverse as yours, there is easily enough content for daily posting. Every day you do not post is a day your 38,000 followers do not see you in their feed, and a day where a competitor fills that space instead.

The food truck alone generates a new content vertical: behind-the-scenes prep, event days, customer reactions, location reveals. Combined with breakfast, burgers, drinks, and the restaurant itself, you have more than enough material to post every single day without repeating yourself.

4

The Food Truck Is a Content Machine You Are Not Fully Using

Your food truck reveal got 1,302 likes. The Battle of the Burger recap got 248 likes and 21 comments. The food truck journey post got 565 likes and 21 comments. People are invested in this story.

But between events, the truck goes quiet on content. The food truck is not just a revenue stream, it is a content engine. The build-up to each event, the location reveal, the behind-the-scenes prep, the queue on the day, the customer reactions, the wrap-up. That is 5-7 pieces of content per event, not just one recap post.

Festivals, fan zones, and food fairs throughout 2026 give you a content calendar that writes itself. Each event is a mini campaign. Right now, you are posting about the truck reactively instead of building anticipation and documenting the journey in real time.

5

No Paid Social Strategy

We checked the Facebook and Instagram Ad Library. No ads running. With 38K followers, proven content that hits 1,300+ likes, and a food truck now expanding your reach beyond the restaurant, paid social is the natural next step.

Your best organic content already tells you exactly what resonates. Taking your top-performing burger Reels and putting paid spend behind them to reach Newcastle locals who do not follow you yet would drive footfall directly. Promoting the food truck at upcoming events, retargeting people who visited your website but did not book, and running seasonal campaigns around new menu items would all deliver measurable returns.

You have already done the hard work of proving what content works. Paid social just puts it in front of more people.

Ready-Made Content
5 Video Ideas You Could Film This Week

These are formats that are working right now for food brands on Instagram and TikTok. All of them play to Davey's strengths as a bold, high-energy burger and breakfast brand.

Idea 01
"From food truck dream to food truck reality"
Journey Brand Story Emotional

A 30-second Reel telling the Davey's story. Quick cuts: opening in 2020, early days, first burger served, building the following, the moment you decided to get the truck, the reveal, Battle of the Burger, and a teaser of what is coming next. Use a trending motivational audio. End on the yellow truck driving away.

Why it works: Brand origin stories are some of the most saved and shared content on social media. People root for independent businesses. Your journey from opening during a pandemic to launching a food truck is genuinely compelling. This humanises the brand and gives new followers a reason to invest emotionally.
Idea 02
"The perfect smash burger in 15 seconds"
Process ASMR Food Porn

Close-up, satisfying footage of a smash burger being made from start to finish. Ball on the grill. The smash. The sizzle. Cheese on top. The melt. The stack. Sauce drizzle. Bun on. The lift. Shot tight, no talking, just sounds and a trending audio underneath. Pure visual satisfaction.

Why it works: Burger build content is one of the most viral food formats on both TikTok and Reels. The ASMR quality of the sizzle and the visual satisfaction of the cheese melt trigger saves and shares. This type of content regularly hits millions of views for burger brands. You have 38K followers waiting to share this.
Idea 03
"Ranking everything on our menu from worst to best"
Ranking Engagement Debate

Stand in front of the menu board. Rank every item from bottom to top. Be honest. Be controversial. Put something unexpected at number one. Let the comments section explode with people disagreeing. Film reactions to the rankings from customers in the restaurant.

Why it works: Ranking content drives massive comment engagement because everyone has an opinion. "How is the birria tacos not number one?!" creates exactly the kind of debate the algorithm rewards. It also subtly showcases your full menu to people who might only know you for burgers.
Idea 04
"We asked 50 customers: what should we add to the menu?"
Community Interactive Series

Film quick clips asking customers what they want to see on the menu next. Tally the most popular suggestions. Then post a follow-up where you actually make the top suggestion and serve it as a special. Turn it into a series: the community builds the menu.

Why it works: Community-driven content makes your audience feel ownership of the brand. When people see their suggestion being made, they share it, tag friends, and come in to try it. The series format keeps people coming back. It turns passive followers into active participants.
Idea 05
"Day in the life: running a burger restaurant and a food truck"
Day in the Life Behind the Scenes Founder

Follow the team through a day where the restaurant is open AND the food truck is at an event. Split screen energy. Breakfast service at Market Street. Loading up the truck. Setting up at the festival. Serving from both locations simultaneously. Show the chaos, the hustle, and the pride. End exhausted but smiling.

Why it works: Day-in-the-life content consistently outperforms on TikTok and Reels because it feels raw and real. People are fascinated by what goes on behind the scenes of a food business. Showing both the restaurant and the truck on the same day tells the growth story visually without saying a word.
Digital Enhancements
Beyond Social Media

These are strategic changes that would make a real difference alongside the social media work.

Switch to a Business Account Immediately

This takes 30 seconds and is free. Go to Settings, Account, Switch to Professional Account, select Business. You will immediately unlock Instagram Insights, the ability to boost posts, contact buttons on your profile, and category labels.

  • See exactly when your audience is online so you can post at optimal times
  • See which posts drive the most profile visits and website clicks
  • See the age, gender, and location breakdown of your followers
  • Add a "Book" or "Order Food" button directly on your profile

Quick win: This is genuinely the single most impactful thing you can do today. You have 38,000 followers and zero data on who they are or what they respond to. That changes the moment you switch.

Fix the Content Format Gap

Your matcha and coffee content is getting 4-5x fewer likes than your burger content. The product is not the problem. The format is. Static photos of matcha work for aesthetic cafe accounts, but in a feed built around bold, high-energy burger content, they feel like a different brand.

The fix: shoot matcha and coffee as short video content. The pour, the swirl, the colour change, the first sip. Make it feel as dynamic and satisfying as your burger content. Same energy, different product.

Build a Food Truck Content Calendar

Every festival, food fair, and fan zone you attend in 2026 should have its own mini content campaign planned in advance. Not just a single recap post after the event.

  • 5-7 days before: teaser post announcing the event
  • 2-3 days before: menu reveal for the event
  • Day before: behind-the-scenes prep and loading up
  • Event day: Stories and Reels from the queue, the service, the reactions
  • Day after: recap, thank you, and tease the next one

That is 5+ pieces of content from a single event. Over a festival season, that is a content strategy that runs itself.
