1

TripAdvisor Ranking Has Huge Upside

You are currently #241 of 1,154 restaurants in Newcastle on TripAdvisor. With a 4.4 rating and 131 reviews, that is respectable. For tourists visiting Newcastle and searching TripAdvisor for the best pizza, moving from #241 to the top 50 would be transformative.

2

Own TikTok Content Could Go Much Further

The TikTok account exists and is active, which is great. But with the visual appeal of wood-fired pizza, the legendary dipping sauce, and the authentic Naples story, there is so much more potential on the platform. Pizza content is one of the most watched food categories on TikTok. Dough stretching, oven shots, cheese pulls, and sauce drizzles are the kind of content that gets millions of views.

There is already loads of UGC about Scream for Pizza on TikTok from customers and food bloggers. That is brilliant social proof. The opportunity is to complement that with consistent, high-quality content from your own account. Two to three posts a week showcasing the craft, the fire, and the finished product would grow your TikTok audience significantly and drive new customers through the door.

The Naples Story Could Be Told More

Being trained in Naples is an incredible brand asset. But from what we have seen online, it is not the central narrative of the brand yet. Most customers probably know about the great pizza. Fewer probably know the story of how and why it is made the way it is. The training, the tradition, the specific techniques that make Neapolitan pizza different.

Story-driven content massively outperforms generic food content on social media. A video showing the Naples connection, the oven technique, the dough process, and the philosophy behind the pizza would not only perform well, it would deepen the emotional connection customers have with the brand. People do not just want good pizza. They want to feel like they are eating something with meaning.

Ready-Made Content
5 Video Ideas You Could Film Today

These are formats that are working right now for pizza restaurants on TikTok and Instagram. All of them play to Scream for Pizza's strengths: authentic Neapolitan craft, a cult dipping sauce, and a passionate following.

Idea 01
"From dough to oven in 60 seconds"
Process Craft Satisfying

Start with the dough ball. Stretch it by hand. Sauce it. Top it. Slide it into the wood-fired oven. Watch the flames. Pull it out charred and bubbling. Slice it. Serve it. All in under 60 seconds. Fast-paced, satisfying, trending audio. Pure pizza craft.

Why it works: Pizza-making process videos are some of the most watched food content on TikTok. The speed, the skill, the fire. It is inherently satisfying and it shows the craft behind every pizza. This format consistently gets millions of views for pizzerias and it communicates the authentic Neapolitan technique that sets Scream for Pizza apart.
Idea 02
"The dipping sauce people call 'crack'"
Cult Product Mystery Engagement

Start with text on screen: "This sauce has a waiting list." Show the sauce being poured. A pizza slice being dipped. The first bite. Someone's genuine reaction. Keep the recipe a mystery. "We cannot tell you what is in it. But you will understand when you try it." Let the comments go wild trying to guess.

Why it works: Mystery and cult product content drives enormous engagement. People will comment, debate, tag friends, and share. The "crack" sauce is already a talking point. Turning it into a content series would generate the kind of conversation and curiosity that drives new customers to visit just to try it for themselves.
Idea 03
"Trained in Naples. Making pizza in Newcastle."
Brand Story Heritage Authenticity

A cinematic brand story piece. The connection to Naples. Why Neapolitan pizza is different. The oven, the dough, the technique. Show the craft and the passion. Keep it honest and genuine. End with a packed restaurant and the words "Scream for Pizza. Sandyford. Quayside." This is the kind of video that defines a brand.

Why it works: Brand story content builds emotional connection. It gives people a reason to choose you beyond just liking pizza. It communicates authenticity, craft, and heritage in a way that a menu photo never could. This kind of content turns casual customers into loyal advocates who tell everyone they know.
Idea 04
"Friday night at Scream for Pizza"
Atmosphere Social Proof Energy

Capture the energy of a packed Friday evening. The oven roaring. Pizzas sliding in and out. The dining room buzzing. Plates going to tables. Groups laughing. Staff in the zone. Use a track that builds. Show that Scream for Pizza is the place to be on a Friday night in Newcastle.

Why it works: Busy service footage is the ultimate social proof. When people see a packed, energetic restaurant, they want to be part of it. This format drives bookings because it creates FOMO. It also works brilliantly for both locations, showing that the brand is thriving across Sandyford and the Quayside.
Idea 05
"The early evening deal you need to know about"
Value Hook Offer Conversion

Walk into Scream for Pizza early evening. Order. Show the pizza arriving. Show the quality. Then reveal the deal price. "This. For that price. Every weeknight." Let the value speak for itself. People tag friends, save the video, and book a table. Direct, simple, effective.

Why it works: Price reveal and deal content is one of the most viral restaurant formats. It drives immediate action because people see the quality, see the price, and want to go. This is especially effective for filling those earlier weeknight slots when the restaurant might be quieter. One viral deal video could fill tables for weeks.
Digital Enhancements
Beyond Social Media

These are strategic changes that would make a real difference alongside the social media work.

TripAdvisor Review Push

The gap between your Google presence (4.7, 605 reviews) and your TripAdvisor presence (4.4, 131 reviews) is the single biggest quick win available. Your Google reviews prove that customers love the food and the experience. You just need to redirect some of that energy towards TripAdvisor.

  • A QR code on every bill linking directly to TripAdvisor
  • Staff prompted to mention TripAdvisor specifically when customers praise the food
  • An occasional Instagram Story encouraging followers to leave a TripAdvisor review
  • Target: top 100 in Newcastle within 6 months, top 50 within 12

Quick win: Moving from #241 to the top 100 on TripAdvisor would dramatically increase visibility for tourists and visitors searching for the best pizza in Newcastle. With your food quality, this is very achievable with a consistent review push.

Cross-Promotion Between Locations

Having both Scream for Pizza in Sandyford and I Scream for Pizza on the Quayside is a huge advantage. Cross-promoting between locations, running location-specific content, and using each location to drive awareness of the other would maximise the value of having two sites. A customer who discovers the Sandyford location on TikTok might prefer the Quayside location, and vice versa.

  • Location-specific content showing the different vibes of each site
  • A "which location are you?" fun format that drives engagement and awareness
  • Consistent branding across both locations on all social platforms

Google Business Profile Optimisation

Your Google rating is 4.7 with 605 reviews, which is exceptional. Keep this momentum going by responding to every review, uploading professional photos regularly, and posting weekly updates about specials, events, or new menu items. Both locations should have fully optimised Google profiles that are actively maintained.
