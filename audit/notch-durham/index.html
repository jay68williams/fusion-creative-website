Where The Opportunity Is
Five Things Holding You Back

The food, the reviews, and the social presence all have strong foundations. You have built a bar and restaurant that people clearly love. The gaps below are not criticisms. They are areas where relatively small changes would make a disproportionately large difference.

1

No Website at All

This is the single biggest gap. Notch does not have a dedicated website. notchdurham.co.uk does not exist as a proper site. There is no central place online where someone can see your full menu, book a table, check your opening hours, enquire about private hire, or learn what the bottomless brunch includes. The only way to find this information is by piecing it together from Instagram posts, third-party scrapers, and review sites.

Every competitor in Durham city centre has a website. When someone Googles "BBQ restaurant Durham" or "bottomless brunch Durham," you are relying entirely on third-party listings to represent you. You have no control over how that information appears, whether it is accurate, or whether it converts a searcher into a customer.

A single, clean website with your menu, booking system, events calendar, bottomless brunch details, private hire information, and contact details would immediately become your most valuable marketing asset. It is the digital front door that every other channel should point to, and right now it does not exist.

2

TikTok Is Underperforming Relative to the Content Opportunity

You have a TikTok account and you are posting. That puts you ahead of most. But a BBQ restaurant with smoked meats, open flames, cocktail making, and a packed bottomless brunch is sitting on a content goldmine. The visual content practically makes itself. Brisket being pulled apart, cocktails being shaken, the energy of a Saturday brunch, smoke rolling off the grill. This is exactly the kind of content that goes viral on TikTok without any ad spend.

The opportunity is in volume and format. You should be posting 4 to 5 times per week minimum, using trending audio, proven restaurant formats like price reveals and "POV" content, and leaning into the specific things that make Notch different. Right now, the account exists but it is not being used at anywhere near its potential. The gap between where you are and where you could be on TikTok is enormous.

3

Instagram Engagement Could Be Higher for 12K Followers

12,000 followers is a strong base. But follower count only matters if those followers are actively engaging with your content. The opportunity here is to wake up your existing audience with more interactive content. Polls in Stories asking "which cocktail should we feature this weekend," Q&A sessions, "this or that" votes between menu items, behind-the-scenes kitchen content showing the smoking process, staff picks, customer shoutouts.

Interactive content does two things. First, it drives the algorithm to push your posts to more of your existing followers who have stopped seeing your content. Second, it creates the kind of engagement signals that make Instagram show your content to new people through Explore and Reels. You have the audience. Now it is about activating them.

4

No Visible Paid Advertising

We checked the Facebook and Instagram Ad Libraries. No ads running for Notch. For a bar and restaurant in a university city with a bottomless brunch offering, this is a missed opportunity. Targeted ads to Durham University students and young professionals would drive immediate bookings. The bottomless brunch practically sells itself as ad creative.

A simple campaign showing a packed Saturday brunch with cocktails flowing, targeted at 18 to 30 year olds within 10 miles of Durham, with a clear call to action like "Book your Saturday," would generate direct bookings at a very low cost per acquisition. Midweek cocktail specials, event nights, and the riverside outdoor seating are all ad-ready hooks that just need a small budget behind them.

5

Review Consistency Worth Watching

Your ratings are strong and your TripAdvisor ranking speaks for itself. This is worth mentioning gently rather than as a major concern. Some recent TripAdvisor reviews mention inconsistency in food quality and menu items being unavailable during busy periods. This is something that social media amplifies. One bad experience shared online reaches far more people than the hundred good ones that came before it.

This is not about the food being bad. Clearly, the vast majority of customers love it. It is about operational consistency during peak times. When you start pushing more people through the door via better digital marketing, making sure the experience is consistently excellent becomes even more important. The marketing brings them in. The experience brings them back.

Ready-Made Content
5 Video Ideas You Could Film Today

These are formats that are working right now for restaurants and bars on TikTok and Instagram. All of them play to Notch's specific strengths as a BBQ bar with smoked meats, craft cocktails, a riverside location, and a bottomless brunch.

Idea 01
"POV: Saturday bottomless brunch at Notch"
Atmosphere Social Proof Brunch

Cocktails being made behind the bar. Food landing on tables. The energy of a packed brunch. Glasses clinking. Groups laughing. The riverside view from the terrace. Fast cuts with a trending audio track. No voiceover needed. Just let the vibe do the work.

Why it works: "POV" and "day in the life" formats are massive on TikTok for bars and restaurants. A bottomless brunch is inherently visual, social, and aspirational. This video makes people feel like they are missing out, and that fear of missing out drives bookings directly. This is the single most effective content format for selling a brunch experience.
Idea 02
"How our brisket is smoked for 12 hours"
Process Behind the Scenes BBQ

Slow-motion shot of the meat going on the smoker. Time-lapse of smoke rolling. The kitchen working around it. Then the money shot: pulling it apart after 12 hours. Close-up of the bark, the smoke ring, the juicy pull. End with it being plated and served.

Why it works: BBQ process content is one of the most satisfying and shareable food formats on social media. The combination of smoke, time, and the final reveal triggers the same response as ASMR content. It racks up saves and shares because people want to show their friends. "We need to go here" is the comment you will see over and over.
Idea 03
"Rating every cocktail on our menu"
Interactive Drinks Series

A staff member or willing customer works through the cocktail menu. Each one gets made, tasted, and rated out of 10. Quick cuts. Honest reactions. Disagreements between staff. End with "which one should we try next?" to drive comments.

Why it works: Rating and ranking content drives enormous engagement because people have opinions. Comments like "how is that only a 7?!" and "I need to try the one that got a 10" are exactly what the algorithm rewards. It also works as a series, meaning you can break this into multiple videos across the whole menu. Each one brings people back.
Idea 04
"This is what you get at our bottomless brunch"
Price Reveal Value Hook Conversion

Start with the price on screen. Then show everything that is included. The food arriving. The cocktails. The refills. The desserts. The whole experience from start to finish. End with the group leaving happy. "Every Saturday, 12 to 5. Book via our Instagram."

Why it works: Price reveal content is the single most effective restaurant ad format on TikTok. People cannot resist finding out what they get for their money. It drives saves (people bookmark it for later), shares (people tag the group chat), and comments (people ask how to book). This one video could fill your bottomless brunch for months.
Idea 05
"Sunday roast at Notch - is it worth it?"
Review Style Food Trust

Honest review format. Walk in. Sit down. Show the Sunday roast arriving. Close-ups of each element. Taste reaction. Give it a score. "A food blogger called this one of their favourite Sunday dinners ever. I had to try it for myself." End with the verdict and a booking prompt.

Why it works: Review-style content builds trust because it feels authentic rather than promotional. The third-party endorsement from Magpie and Mash calling it "one of my favourite Sunday dinners ever" is powerful social proof. Framing it as an honest review rather than an advert makes people trust the recommendation and drives them to try it themselves.
Digital Enhancements
Beyond Social Media

These are strategic changes that would make a real difference alongside the social media work.

Build a Proper Website

This is the number one priority. Notch needs a dedicated website that handles everything in one place. Right now, there is no central hub online for your business. A customer searching for your menu, your hours, your brunch details, or private hire options has to dig through Instagram posts and third-party listing sites to find basic information.

  • Full menu with pricing, clearly laid out and easy to browse on mobile
  • Online booking integration so people can reserve a table without calling or messaging
  • Bottomless brunch dedicated page with details, pricing, and a direct booking link
  • Events calendar showing what is coming up, from live music to special menus
  • Private hire page for group bookings, corporate events, and birthday parties
  • Contact details, location map, and opening hours front and centre

Quick win: Even a simple one-page site with your menu, hours, location, and a booking link would be a massive improvement over having nothing. Every day without a website is a day where potential customers are bouncing off your Google listing because they cannot find what they need.

Paid Social Targeting Durham Students and Young Professionals

Durham is a university city. There are thousands of students and young professionals within walking distance of Notch who are actively looking for somewhere to eat, drink, and celebrate. Targeted Facebook and Instagram ads are the fastest way to get in front of them.

  • Bottomless brunch ads targeting 18 to 30 year olds in Durham during the week leading up to Saturday
  • Midweek cocktail specials ads to fill quieter evenings
  • Event promotion ads for any special nights, live music, or seasonal menus
  • Retargeting ads to people who have engaged with your Instagram or visited your page but not booked

The numbers: Restaurant ads on Facebook and Instagram typically cost between 3p and 15p per click. A bottomless brunch booking is worth significantly more than that. Even a small monthly budget would generate measurable returns.

Leverage the Riverside Location in All Content

The Elvet Bridge riverside setting is a huge differentiator that most Durham restaurants do not have. It should feature in every piece of content, not just occasionally. Outdoor seating overlooking the river, golden hour drinks on the terrace, the view at sunset. This is the kind of visual advantage that makes people choose you over a competitor before they even look at the menu.

  • Make the river view a signature element in your content calendar, not just a background detail
  • Seasonal content: summer evenings by the river, cosy autumn drinks, the first warm day of spring when the terrace opens
  • Use location as a hook in ads: "Cocktails overlooking the river. Durham's best-kept secret."
  • Encourage customers to share photos from the terrace by making it as photogenic as possible
