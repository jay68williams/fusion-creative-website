Where The Opportunity Is
Five Areas With Real Potential

The food, the reviews, and the concept are all exceptional. You have built something that people genuinely love. These are the areas where a focused push could take things to the next level.

1

Only 4 TripAdvisor Reviews Despite a Perfect Score

A 5.0 rating on TripAdvisor is incredible. But with only 4 reviews, you are almost invisible on the platform. TripAdvisor heavily weights review volume and recency in its rankings. A restaurant with 150 reviews at 4.2 will outrank a restaurant with 4 reviews at 5.0 every single time. People also trust higher review counts. When someone is choosing between two restaurants and one has 4 reviews while the other has 200, they pick the one with more reviews regardless of the rating.

The good news is that you clearly have the customer satisfaction to generate excellent reviews. Your customers love the experience. They just need a gentle nudge to share it publicly. A QR code on the table linking to TripAdvisor, a small prompt from staff after a positive comment, or a follow-up message after a booking could transform your review count within months.

2

No Website Is Holding You Back

When someone Googles "Korean BBQ Newcastle" or "best restaurant Jesmond," you need a website to capture that traffic. Right now, without a website, you are relying entirely on social media profiles and third-party review sites to represent your brand online. You cannot control how those platforms display your information, and you are missing out on direct bookings.

A clean, well-designed website with your menu, booking integration, your story, photos of the experience, and your opening hours would immediately improve your Google search ranking, give potential customers confidence, and provide a direct channel for reservations. It does not need to be complicated. It needs to be professional, fast, and mobile-friendly.

3

Turn the UGC Wave Into a Content Strategy

You are already getting fantastic user-generated content from customers filming their BBQ experience. That is a huge advantage. The next step is building a deliberate content strategy around it. Repost the best UGC to your own feed (with permission and credit). Create your own high-quality versions of the same content. Establish a branded hashtag. Make it easy and rewarding for customers to tag you.

A consistent posting schedule of your own content, combined with strategic reposting of UGC, would create a content engine that grows itself. Every customer with a phone is a potential content creator for INSA. You just need to make it part of the experience.

4

Own the TikTok Moment

Korean BBQ is one of the most viral food categories on TikTok globally. The sizzle, the smoke, the interactive element, the dramatic visual of meat cooking over charcoal at the table. This content practically films itself. While you have a TikTok presence and strong UGC coverage, a deliberate strategy with consistent, high-quality posts using trending formats and audio could rapidly grow your reach.

Restaurants that post consistently on TikTok are seeing direct, measurable increases in bookings. The format is perfect for INSA because the experience is inherently visual and dramatic. One well-produced video of Wagyu A5 hitting the charcoal grill could reach millions of people in the North East and beyond.

5

Fill the Quieter Hours With Smart Marketing

Being closed on Mondays and opening at 4pm on weekdays means your revenue windows are concentrated. While that may suit your operations, targeted marketing could help maximise every opening hour. Early evening midweek promotions, special tasting menus for quieter Tuesday and Wednesday evenings, or student-focused offers for the Jesmond demographic could fill tables that might otherwise be empty.

Paid social ads targeting people within 5 miles of Jesmond during the afternoon, just before you open, with a mouthwatering video of the BBQ experience, could drive same-day bookings. The timing is everything. Show someone a sizzling grill at 3pm and they are booking a table for that evening.

Ready-Made Content
5 Video Ideas You Could Film Today

These are formats that are working right now for restaurants on TikTok and Instagram. All of them play to INSA's strengths as an interactive, experience-driven Korean BBQ restaurant.

Idea 01
"A5 Wagyu on real charcoal. This is INSA."
Premium ASMR Hero Content

Close-up of A5 Wagyu being placed on the charcoal grill. The sizzle. The marbling melting. The smoke rising. Slow motion flip. The juice running. Someone picks it up with chopsticks and takes the first bite. No voiceover. Just the sounds of the grill and a trending audio track underneath.

Why it works: Wagyu content is one of the most viral food categories on TikTok globally. The visual of A5 marbling melting over real charcoal is hypnotic. This single video format has the potential to reach millions and position INSA as a must-visit destination in Newcastle.
Idea 02
"Your first Korean BBQ? Here is how it works."
Educational Approachable Discovery

A friendly, quick guide for first-timers. Show the grill being set up. Explain the basics: choose your meats, grill them at the table, wrap in lettuce with banchan. Make it welcoming, not intimidating. "Never been to Korean BBQ? Let us show you." End with the full spread on the table looking incredible.

Why it works: Many people in Newcastle have never tried Korean BBQ and might feel unsure about the process. An approachable guide removes that hesitation completely. Educational content also gets saved and shared at high rates, which signals to the algorithm to push it further. It turns curiosity into bookings.
Idea 03
"POV: Saturday night at Newcastle's only charcoal Korean BBQ"
Atmosphere Social Proof Vibe

Capture the energy of a busy Saturday service. Smoke rising from multiple tables. Groups laughing and grilling together. The kitchen in full flow. Meat sizzling. Glasses clinking. Use a warm, atmospheric audio track. Let the energy of the room do the work. End with a wide shot of the full restaurant.

Why it works: People choose restaurants based on vibe as much as food. A video showing a packed, buzzing INSA on a Saturday night creates fear of missing out. It shows that this is where people want to be. That social proof is more powerful than any menu description or paid advertisement.
Idea 04
"We ordered everything on the menu at INSA"
Menu Showcase Scale Shareable

A full table spread showing every dish INSA offers. Bulgogi, bibimbap, Wagyu, all the banchan, the full works. Quick cuts of each dish arriving and being placed on the table. The table getting fuller and fuller. End with the overhead shot of the entire spread. "This is INSA Korean BBQ, Jesmond."

Why it works: "We ordered everything" is one of the most popular restaurant content formats on TikTok. The abundance and variety creates an irresistible visual. People tag friends, save it for their next meal out, and share it in group chats. It showcases your full menu in a single, highly shareable video.
Idea 05
"The sound of real charcoal Korean BBQ"
ASMR Sensory Unique

No music. No voiceover. Just the pure sounds of the INSA experience. The charcoal crackling. Meat sizzling as it hits the grill. Tongs turning the meat. The gentle bubble of a stew. Chopsticks picking up a piece of perfectly grilled beef. Close-up, intimate shots. Let the sounds tell the story.

Why it works: ASMR food content generates some of the highest view counts and watch times on TikTok. The real charcoal element gives INSA a sound that no gas grill or electric plate can replicate. It is unique, authentic, and incredibly satisfying to watch and listen to. This differentiates you from every other restaurant in the city.
