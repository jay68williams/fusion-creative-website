A few ideas I would love to try

Sell the feeling, not just the lineup.A lot of posts announce what is on. The ones that travel capture the vibe: the rooftop at golden hour, the crowd, the energy of a Saturday so people feel they are missing out.
Put the reactions on camera.Film the room. Faces, the dancing, the first drink with the skyline behind you. Real people having a great time sells a venue far better than any flyer.
A vendor spotlight series.Twelve kitchens is a goldmine. "Which one are you hitting first?" Quick, mouth-watering food reels from Meatstack, I Scream for Pizza and the rest.
Meet the Island.The DJs, the chefs, the team. People follow people, and it is some of the cheapest, best-performing content you can make.
A weekly rhythm.One atmosphere recap, one what is on, one food reel a week, so the feed always carries the feeling, not just the announcements, all pointing at the booking link.
Lean into trends.Jump on trending formats and sounds while they are hot. It is the fastest, cheapest way to land in front of new people, and it is most of how we have grown other Newcastle venues.
