✓ A completely unique concept in the market

Moku is the only Hawaiian-inspired restaurant in Newcastle. That is a massive competitive advantage. In a city full of Italian, Indian, and Asian restaurants, you have carved out a category of one. Where The Opportunity Is
Three Areas With Real Growth Potential

You have an incredible product, world-class reviews, and a unique brand. These opportunities are about making sure the digital presence matches the quality of what you have built.

1

No TikTok Account for a Visually Stunning Brand

Moku is one of the most visually striking restaurants in Newcastle. Flame-grilled meats, tropical cocktails, Hawaiian-inspired plating, vibrant decor. This is the kind of content that TikTok was built for. And right now, there is heavy UGC (user-generated content) being posted about Moku by food bloggers and customers, but none of it lives on an official Moku account.

That means other people are telling your story for you, and you have no control over it. An official TikTok account would let you own the narrative, build a following, and turn all that organic buzz into direct bookings. Restaurants with strong TikTok presences are seeing measurable increases in covers. With a concept this visual, the potential is enormous.

2

Instagram at 9K Is Modest for a Top-15 Restaurant

With nearly 500 TripAdvisor reviews, a 4.9 rating, and a Travellers' Choice award, Moku has the kind of reputation that should translate into a much larger Instagram following. 9,090 followers is solid, but for a restaurant ranked #13 in Newcastle with such a visually distinctive brand, there is significant room to grow.

The opportunity is in consistent, high-quality short-form video content. Reels are what drive Instagram growth now, and Moku's food, cocktails, and atmosphere are perfectly suited to the format. A consistent Reels strategy could realistically double the following within 6 months while driving direct bookings from every post.

3

Closed Monday and Tuesday Is Untapped Revenue

This is not a criticism of your operating model. Closing on quieter days makes sense for a lot of restaurants. But it is worth exploring whether targeted social media content and promotions could make Monday or Tuesday openings viable. A "midweek special" or "industry night" promoted through short-form video could generate enough demand to justify opening on at least one of those days. The fixed costs of the premises are there regardless, so any additional covers are highly profitable.

Even if opening additional days is not on the table right now, social media content can be used to drive higher bookings on your existing open days, filling every table Wednesday through Sunday and making the most of your current hours.

Ready-Made Content
5 Video Ideas You Could Film Today

These are formats that are working right now for restaurants on TikTok and Instagram. All of them play to Moku's strengths as a unique Hawaiian fusion restaurant with outstanding food and atmosphere.

Idea 01
"There is only one Hawaiian restaurant in Newcastle. This is it."
Brand Story Uniqueness Discovery

Walk into Moku. Show the Hawaiian decor, the atmosphere, the menu. Quick cuts of the flame grill in action, cocktails being made, plates hitting the table. End with the TripAdvisor rating on screen: "4.9 stars. #13 in Newcastle. The only Hawaiian restaurant in the city."

Why it works: "Only one in the city" content is one of the highest-performing restaurant formats on TikTok. It triggers curiosity, drives saves and shares, and positions Moku as a must-visit destination. People love discovering something they did not know existed in their own city.
Idea 02
"POV: Bottomless brunch at Moku Newcastle"
Experience Bottomless Brunch Group Content

Film the full bottomless brunch experience. Arriving with friends, the first cocktails landing, plates of food coming out, reactions, laughter, more drinks arriving. Fast cuts, warm tones, upbeat audio. End with "Bottomless brunch from 39 pounds. Book now."

Why it works: Bottomless brunch content consistently goes viral because it is aspirational, shareable, and triggers group plans. People tag friends, save the post for their next brunch, and share it in group chats. This single video format could fill your brunch slots for weeks.
Idea 03
"How we flame-grill everything at Moku"
Behind the Scenes ASMR Process

Close-up shots of the flame grill in action. Meat hitting the grill, the sizzle, the flames, the flip, the char marks forming. No voiceover needed. Let the sounds and visuals do all the work. Finish with the plated dish arriving at the table.

Why it works: Fire and food is one of the most primal and engaging content combinations on social media. Grill content consistently outperforms other food formats because the sounds and visuals are inherently satisfying. This is the kind of content that stops people mid-scroll.
Idea 04
"Bringing my dog to the best-rated restaurant in Newcastle"
Dog-Friendly Lifestyle Local

A customer-perspective video showing up with their dog, getting seated, the dog being welcomed by staff, water bowl arriving, food arriving, dog sitting happily while the owner eats Hawaiian fusion food. Wholesome, warm, shareable.

Why it works: Dog content on social media is huge. Combine dogs with food content and you tap into two of the most engaged audiences online. Dog owners actively search for dog-friendly restaurants and share recommendations within their communities. One video could position Moku as the go-to dog-friendly dining spot in Newcastle.
Idea 05
"We ordered every cocktail on the menu at Moku"
Cocktails Tropical Visual

Line up every cocktail. Show each one being made: the pour, the shake, the garnish, the presentation. Bright colours, tropical garnishes, creative glassware. Each cocktail gets a quick close-up with its name on screen. End with the full lineup on the bar.

Why it works: "We ordered everything" content is a proven viral format. The variety, the colours, and the visual spectacle of Hawaiian-inspired cocktails make this irresistible to watch. It drives comments ("which one would you pick?"), shares, and most importantly, it gives people a reason to visit and try them for themselves.
