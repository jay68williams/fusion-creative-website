Where The Opportunity Is
Five Things Holding You Back

The food is incredible, bloggers are already raving, and the venue has real character. But the content strategy is not converting that quality into growth. Here is where the biggest gaps are.

1

UGC Massively Outperforms Brand Content

This is the single biggest finding in this audit. Food blogger posts about Frallo average 170+ likes. Your own brand posts average 25 likes. That is a 7x gap. When @izz.eatsout posts about her anniversary date night at Frallo, it gets 285 likes and 3,537 views. When @gourmetnortheast posts a Sunday lunch video, it gets 264 likes and 69 comments. Your own content is not coming close to those numbers.

This is not a criticism of your content. It is a massive opportunity. The demand for Frallo content is clearly there. People want to see it. They engage when they do. You just need a structured UGC and influencer strategy to turn occasional organic mentions into a consistent stream of high-performing content.

Right now, those blogger posts happen by chance. With a formal programme of 2-3 creator invites per month, you could guarantee a steady flow of content that reaches audiences you currently have no access to, all performing at 5-7x your own post average.

2

Not a Business Account

Frallo is running on a personal Instagram account. This means you have no access to Instagram Insights (so you cannot see which posts drive the most profile visits, saves, or shares), no ability to run paid ads, no contact button on your profile, no booking button, and no ability to add links to Stories.

Switching to a business account is free and takes two minutes. It immediately unlocks analytics, advertising, and features that let potential customers contact you or book directly from the app. Every day on a personal account is a day you are flying blind with no data on what is actually working.

3

Following 1,287 Accounts

With 2,503 followers and 1,287 following, your ratio is roughly 2:1. That is follow-for-follow territory. It dilutes the brand, makes the account look less established, and does not build a real audience. The people you gained through follow-for-follow are not your customers. They are not in Houghton-le-Spring. They are not booking a table.

A premium Italian steakhouse should have a following ratio that reflects authority, not reciprocity. Unfollowing non-relevant accounts and focusing on content-driven growth would improve brand perception immediately. Let the food and the venue attract followers, not a follow-back strategy.

4

Video Content Is Underutilised

Your Reels get decent view counts when you do post them. A wine and conversation Reel got 581 views and 45 likes. A steak cutting Reel got 938 views and 41 likes. But you post mostly static images. The steak being cut, the pasta being plated, cocktails being poured, the atmosphere of The Cove, the terrace at sunset. All of this is screaming for video.

In 2026, Instagram's algorithm overwhelmingly favours Reels. A static photo of your vodka pasta might get 25 likes. A 15-second Reel of that same pasta being tossed, the burrata being placed on top, and the nduja oil being drizzled could reach 10x the audience. Your Reels already prove this: they consistently outperform your static posts. You just need more of them.

5

No Paid Social Visible

With the early bird offer (2 courses for £17.95), the venue quality, and the food that bloggers already rave about, even a small paid budget targeting locals within 15 miles would drive bookings directly. A 15-second Reel showing "What you get for £17.95 at Frallo" with a booking link would convert.

Your competitors in the North East dining scene are running ads. Every impression they get is one you do not. The early bird offer is a natural paid social campaign: clear value proposition, specific price point, easy call to action. It just needs to be seen by more people.

Ready-Made Content
5 Video Ideas You Could Film This Week

These are formats that are working right now for restaurants on Instagram and TikTok. All of them play to Frallo's unique strengths as an Italian steakhouse with exceptional food, a standout venue, and a value offer that converts.

Idea 01
"What you get for £17.95 at Frallo"
Value Reveal Conversion Trending

Show the full early bird 2-course experience. Walking in. Sitting down. Starter arriving, beautifully plated. Main course arriving. The quality, the presentation, the atmosphere. Hold back the price until the very end. Text overlay: "All of this for £17.95." 20 seconds.

Why it works: Price reveal content is one of the highest-performing formats on Reels and TikTok. The gap between what people expect for £17.95 and what Frallo actually delivers is massive. That surprise is what drives shares, saves, and comments. Bloggers are already calling out the value. This format turns that reaction into a repeatable content piece that drives midweek bookings directly.
Idea 02
"Cutting into our Fiorentina T-bone"
Food ASMR Cinematic Shareable

Slow-motion, close-up of the Fiorentina T-bone being sliced. The pink centre reveal. Juices running across the board. Plating with sides. The full sharing platter assembled. Pure steak ASMR. No voiceover needed. Let the visuals and the sizzle do the work. 15 seconds.

Why it works: Your best own post (55 likes) was the Fiorentina sharing platter as a static image. Now imagine that same dish in slow-motion video with the sound of the knife, the sizzle, and the reveal. Food ASMR content gets saved and shared at extremely high rates because it is satisfying to watch on repeat. This is your hero dish. Give it hero content.
Idea 03
"Date night at The Cove"
POV Lifestyle Romantic

POV walking into The Cove. The curved walls, the intimate lighting. Sitting down at a candlelit table. Wine being poured. Starter arriving. The atmosphere. A couple clinking glasses. Trending romantic audio. 20 seconds of pure mood. End with "The Cove at Frallo. Book the table in the corner."

Why it works: Date night content consistently ranks among the highest-performing restaurant formats. The Cove is a unique space that most people do not know exists. This content turns a hidden gem into a must-visit destination. People tag their partners, save the post for later, and send it to friends. @izz.eatsout's anniversary date night post got 285 likes and 3,537 views. That is the demand. This format captures it on your own account.
Idea 04
"Our vodka pasta has food bloggers obsessed"
Trending Social Proof Reaction

Open with text: "Food bloggers keep coming back for this." Quick cuts of the blogger posts and their captions. Then cut to the kitchen. The pasta being made. The nduja going in. The rigatoni being tossed. The burrata placed on top. The first forkful. Finish with "Rigatoni Vodka e Burrata. Available now." 20 seconds.

Why it works: Social proof is the most powerful sales tool in hospitality. When potential customers see that multiple independent bloggers are raving about the same dish, it creates urgency. @spritzandstays gave the vodka pasta a 9/10 spritz score. This format stacks that proof and then shows the dish being made, turning curiosity into craving. It also rewards the bloggers with visibility, encouraging more organic visits.
Idea 05
"Friday nights at Frallo hit different"
Lifestyle Vibe Aspirational

Live music clip from Frallo Fridays Unplugged. Cocktails being shaken and poured. Food being served to a busy table. The energy of a Friday evening. Quick cuts between the musician, the bar, the food, the people. Atmospheric and aspirational. Trending audio. End with "Frallo Fridays. Every other Friday." 15 seconds.

Why it works: Atmosphere and vibe content positions Frallo as more than just a meal. It makes it an experience, a night out, a "we should go there this Friday" moment. The live music is a genuine differentiator that no other Italian restaurant in the area offers. This format sells the Friday feeling, not just the food. It drives group bookings and repeat visits.
Digital Enhancements
Beyond Social Media

These are strategic changes that would make a real difference alongside the social media work.

Switch to a Business Account Immediately

This is the single quickest win available. Switching from a personal account to a business account is free and takes two minutes. It unlocks Instagram Insights (so you can see what is actually working), the ability to run paid ads, contact and booking buttons on your profile, and link stickers in Stories.

  • See which posts drive the most profile visits, website clicks, and saves
  • Understand your audience demographics: age, location, active hours
  • Add a "Book Now" or "Reserve" button directly on your Instagram profile
  • Run targeted ads promoting the early bird offer to locals within 15 miles

Quick win: Switch to a business account today. It is free, takes two minutes, and you will immediately see data on every post you have ever made. No reason not to.

Clean Up the Following List

Following 1,287 accounts with 2,503 followers gives a ratio that signals follow-for-follow growth. A premium Italian steakhouse should look like a destination, not an account that follows back everyone. Unfollowing non-relevant accounts would immediately improve brand perception and signal authority.

Focus on following only accounts that add value: local food bloggers, suppliers you work with, industry accounts, and genuine community connections. The goal is a following list that looks curated, not accumulated.

Build a Structured Influencer Programme

You are already getting organic blogger visits. That is the hardest part, and it is already happening. The next step is to formalise it. Invite 2-3 North East food creators per month for a complimentary Frallo experience. Let them create content naturally. Repost the best pieces.

  • The North East has dozens of active food bloggers with 5K-50K engaged, local followers
  • Each visit generates 2-4 pieces of content reaching audiences you currently have no access to
  • The cost is a complimentary meal. The return is content and reach worth many times more
  • Blogger posts already average 170+ likes vs your own 25 likes. That 7x gap is the opportunity

Quick win: Reach out to @northeastcravings, @izz.eatsout, and @spritzandstays. They already love Frallo. Invite them back. Build the relationship. Turn one-off posts into ongoing partnerships.
