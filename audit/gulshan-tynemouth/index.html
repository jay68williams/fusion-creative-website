Where The Opportunity Is
Five Areas With Real Potential

Gulshan has a great product, a strong reputation, and one of the best independent restaurant audiences in Tynemouth. These are the five areas where focused effort would turn a popular village restaurant into a regional destination.

1

The Account Is Still a Personal Profile, Not a Business Account

This is the easiest fix on the list and probably the most important one. The Instagram account is set up as a personal profile, which means you have no access to insights, no contact buttons, no booking link, no ad capability, and no analytics. You are flying blind and leaving every paid growth lever on the table.

Switching to a business account is free, takes two minutes, and unlocks reach data, audience demographics, top-performing posts, action buttons (Call, Reserve, Order, Email), and the entire Meta Ads ecosystem. For a restaurant posting this much content, not being a business account is leaving a huge amount of growth on the table every single day.

2

3,155 Posts and Only 6,755 Followers Means the Content Is Not Compounding

3,155 posts to 6,755 followers is roughly two followers gained per post. For a restaurant with this much content volume, that is a clear sign the algorithm is not rewarding the format being used. Static food photos, even excellent ones, do not get pushed to non-followers in 2026. Reels do. The effort is already there. It is just being spent on the wrong format.

If 30% of those posts had been short-form vertical Reels with hooks, audio, and pacing built for the algorithm, you could realistically be at 30k or 50k followers by now. That is not exaggeration. That is what we have seen happen to clients with weaker starting positions. The good news is the runway is still there. You are not behind. You are just one format shift away from a completely different growth curve.

3

TikTok Does Not Exist Yet

There is no TikTok account for Gulshan. Indian food is one of the most viral food categories on TikTok globally. Tikka grill close-ups, the cheese pull on a peshwari naan, naga heat reactions, mixed grill reveals, cocktail pours. The algorithm rewards this content massively. There is no Indian restaurant in North Tyneside that owns this space yet. Whoever moves first wins it.

TikTok is also where the under-35 booking crowd discovers restaurants now. Tynemouth is a date-night and group-dinner destination for Newcastle locals in their 20s and 30s. Right now, when they search "best Indian Newcastle" or "where to eat in Tynemouth" on TikTok, Gulshan does not come up. That is a sealed-off audience that should be yours.

4

The Cocktail Bar Side of the Brand Is Almost Invisible Online

Gulshan is officially "Indian Kitchen, Grill and Cocktail Bar" but online the cocktail bar element is barely featured. Cocktail content performs incredibly well on Instagram and TikTok. Pours, shakes, smoking glasses, tiki garnishes, the 2 for 10 deal. This is high-volume scrollable content that doubles your appeal beyond just dinner customers.

Right now if someone searches "Tynemouth cocktail bar" they think of bars on Front Street, not Gulshan. But you have a full cocktail menu, an upmarket setting, and offers that should be pulling the pre-dinner and date-night crowd. Leaning the brand 30% harder into the bar side opens up a whole second revenue line of guests who come for cocktails and stay for food.

5

No Founder or Family Story in the Content

Mr Rahman and his two sons, Mamun and Suffian, with 45+ years of combined restaurant experience between you. That is a story most independents would build a whole brand around. Right now the content is product-led, not people-led. People follow people, not menus. The audience does not know your faces, your names, or why you opened Gulshan in the first place.

A founder-led content series, a dad-and-sons day-in-the-life, a "how we built Gulshan" reel, would humanise the brand instantly and make people feel like they know you before they ever sit down. That is the difference between a customer who came once and a customer who comes every Friday and brings their friends. The story is there. It just needs to be told.

Ready-Made Content
4 Video Ideas You Could Film This Week

These are formats working right now on TikTok and Instagram Reels, tailored specifically to Gulshan's grill, cocktail bar, and Tynemouth village setting. Film any one of these properly and it could be the video that puts Gulshan in front of hundreds of thousands of new people.

Idea 01
"Gulshan Dopiaza, start to finish"
Process Signature Dish ASMR

Close-up on the onions hitting the pan. The sizzle. Spices going in, hand-cracked. Marinated meat dropped onto the heat. Slow stirs. Steam rising. Cut to the dish being plated, garnish landing, hot naan torn open on the side. Hero shot of the finished dish on the table at Gulshan with the venue softly out of focus. Trending audio or pure pan ASMR. Under 25 seconds.

Why it works: Process content is one of the most reliably viral formats in food TikTok. The fire, the sizzle, the spice. People cannot scroll past it. Leading with a Signature Collection dish makes it impossible to copy. "You can only get this at Gulshan" is the hook that turns a saved reel into a Friday night booking.
Idea 02
"POV: 2 cocktails for 10 quid in Tynemouth village before dinner"
POV Cocktails Value

First person camera. Walk down Front Street, turn into Hotspur Street, push the door open. Sit at the bar. Two cocktails are made in front of you. Pour, shake, smoke, garnish. Hand close-up sliding the drinks across. Cut to a close-up of the menu showing 2 for 10. Cinematic trending audio. Text overlay: "2 cocktails for 10 quid before dinner. Tynemouth village. Gulshan."

Why it works: POV restaurant content is the highest-converting format for hospitality. Combining it with the cocktail bar side of the brand opens up an audience that does not currently see Gulshan as a cocktail destination. Value reveal hooks make people tag friends and book the same week.
Idea 03
"The Spicy Mega Mixed Grill: everything you get for the price"
Value Reveal Portion Group

Overhead shot. Empty table. Plates start arriving one after another. Tikka. Sheek kebabs. Lamb chops. Grilled chicken. King prawns. Naan. Rice. Salad. Yoghurt dips. Camera slowly zooms out as each item lands. Text overlay counts the items as they arrive. End on the full spread with a hand reaching in. Trending audio.

Why it works: Value-reveal content drives massive engagement because viewers tag friends and say "we need to do this." Mixed grill reveals are one of the strongest performing formats in Indian food TikTok. For groups planning weekend nights out in Tynemouth, this is the video that gets the booking.
Idea 04
"How we built Gulshan: 10+ years in Tynemouth village"
Founder Story Family Personality

Mamun and Suffian on camera in the restaurant. Why Indian food? Why Tynemouth? What did Mr Rahman teach you? What does Gulshan mean to your family? Cut between your words and the kitchen, the grill, the bar, the regulars laughing, plates leaving the pass. Keep it real and warm. 45-60 seconds. Text overlay: "Our family. Our story." Emotional trending audio.

Why it works: People follow people, not restaurants. A founder story humanises the brand and creates emotional pull. Once your audience knows your faces and the family behind it, they are not just booking a table. They are supporting Mamun, Suffian, and the Rahman family. That is the difference between a customer and a fan who comes back every month.
Digital Enhancements
Beyond Social Media

These are strategic changes that would make a real difference alongside the content work.

Switch Instagram to a Business Account (5 Minutes, Free)

Quickest win on the entire audit. Switching unlocks insights, audience demographics, action buttons (Call, Reserve, Email, Get Directions), Meta Ads access, and the ability to add a proper booking link. Right now you are running a business account as a personal profile and missing out on every growth signal Meta gives you for free.

  • Switch to Business in Instagram Settings (takes 2 minutes)
  • Connect to the Gulshan Facebook page
  • Add Reserve, Order Online, Call, and Email action buttons
  • Add a Linktree-style bio link covering booking, menu, cocktail menu, and delivery

Quick win: This single change opens up Meta Ads, real analytics, and direct booking buttons for every Instagram visitor. It pays itself back in the first week.

Launch TikTok and Claim "Indian Tynemouth" / "Indian Newcastle"

There is no Indian restaurant in North Tyneside currently dominating TikTok. The opportunity is wide open. Setting up @gulshan_tynemouth on TikTok and posting 3-4 videos per week of grill content, cocktail content, and dish reveals would put Gulshan in front of tens of thousands of people in the region who eat out every week.

  • Create TikTok account matching the Instagram handle
  • Post 3-4 short videos per week (grill, dish, cocktail, founder)
  • Use Tynemouth and Newcastle location tags on every post
  • Cross-post the best TikToks straight to Reels to double the reach

Lean Harder Into the Cocktail Bar Side

The cocktail bar element is the most under-used asset in the brand. Pour content, garnish work, the 2 for 10 deal, smoking glasses, espresso martinis. This kind of content is gold on Reels and TikTok and opens up the pre-dinner and date-night audience who currently do not see Gulshan as a cocktail destination.

The opportunity: A weekly "Cocktail of the Week" reel format, plus repurposing every cocktail moment from regular service, would build a second content lane and a second revenue stream of bar-only guests who become dinner customers later.
