What Is Already Working Well
Credit Where It Is Due

✓ The product speaks for itself

A 4.9 Google rating is exceptional. Customers are calling out the sourdough base, the bold flavours and the quality of the ingredients. That kind of word-of-mouth loyalty is the hardest thing to build, and you already have it.

✓ You have a genuine craft story

Slow fermented BIGA dough, San Marzano tomatoes, truffle cream, hot honey. This is not another generic takeaway menu. You are making restaurant-quality food from a takeaway kitchen, and that story is exactly the kind of thing that travels on social media.

✓ You own the ordering channel

Having your own website and app for direct orders, rather than relying entirely on Uber Eats or Just Eat, is a massive advantage. Every order through your own platform is higher margin and builds your customer list. Most takeaways never get that far.

✓ Food hygiene is spotless

A 5-star food hygiene rating reinforces everything else. It tells a new customer they can trust the kitchen before they have even tried the food. It is a small detail that matters a lot when someone is deciding whether to order from a place they have just discovered online.

✓ The location has built-in character

Operating from The Bike Shed in Baker's Yard, Gosforth, gives you a story that a unit on an industrial estate does not. It is the kind of setting that looks great on camera and makes people curious, which is exactly what you want from a piece of content.

Where The Opportunity Is
Six Things We Would Focus On

You already have the product, the reviews and the craft. The job is to turn that into reach and orders: get in front of the people in Gosforth, Jesmond, the Tyne Valley and wider Newcastle who would love Farina if they knew it existed. These are the six areas we would focus on.

1

Show the Craft, Not Just the Finished Plate

Your biggest advantage is that you are making proper food from scratch, slow fermented dough, hand-stretched bases, San Marzano sauce. That process is mesmerising on video. The dough stretch, the sauce spread, the pizza going into the oven, the pull apart. These are the moments that stop a thumb mid-scroll and make someone save the video or send it to a friend.

A photo of a finished pizza is nice. A 15-second reel of the dough being stretched and the cheese bubbling is what gets shared.

2

Get on TikTok Before Your Competitors Do

Food is one of the biggest categories on TikTok, and local food discovery is huge. People actively search "best pizza Newcastle" and "takeaway near me" on TikTok now, not just Google. You do not have a TikTok presence yet, which means there is an open lane to be the first Italian takeaway in Gosforth and Newcastle to own that space.

The businesses that get there first build the audience. The ones that arrive later have to fight for it.

3

Turn Your Reviews Into Content

A 4.9 rating and customers raving about the sourdough base and bold flavours is your most powerful marketing asset, and right now it is sitting quietly on Google. Screenshot the best reviews, film the dish they are talking about, overlay the quote. "This customer gave us 5 stars for the N'Duja pizza, here is how we make it." That turns a review into a reason to order.

Social proof is the fastest way to convert someone who has never heard of you into someone placing their first order.

4

Build a Local Following, Not Just a Feed

57 Instagram posts is a solid start, but the content needs to be built to reach new people, not just to be seen by existing followers. That means strong hooks in the first second, trending audio, local hashtags and location tags, and formats that the algorithm rewards (Reels over static posts, every time).

The goal is not just more posts. It is more of the right people in Gosforth, Jesmond, and wider Newcastle seeing Farina for the first time and thinking, I need to try that.

5

Use Short-Form Video to Drive Direct Orders

You already have the ordering infrastructure with your own website and app. The missing piece is the top of the funnel: getting enough new eyes on Farina that a steady stream of first-time customers are landing on the site and ordering. Short-form video is the most cost-effective way to do that for a local food business right now.

Every reel or TikTok that travels is a free advert that sends people straight to your ordering page.

6

Targeted Local Ads to Fill Quiet Nights

Pair the organic content with a small, tightly targeted ad budget aimed at people within your delivery radius. Run your best-performing reels as ads on a Tuesday or Wednesday evening, when takeaway demand is there but most places are not marketing. You already have tiered delivery pricing, so the geography is built in.

For a takeaway, even a handful of extra orders on a quiet night covers the ad spend many times over, which makes this one of the highest-return moves you can make.

Ready-Made Content
4 Video Ideas You Could Film in the Kitchen

These are formats working right now on TikTok and Instagram Reels, built around what you already do: the dough, the toppings, the oven, the finished plate. Each one could be filmed on a phone during a normal service.

Idea 01
"72 hours of fermentation for one pizza"
ProcessCraftASMR

A satisfying process reel: the dough ball, the stretch, the sauce from San Marzano tomatoes, the toppings laid on, into the oven, the bubbling cheese, the slice and pull. Trending audio, no talking needed. Under 20 seconds. Let the craft do the selling.

Why it works: Pizza-making process videos are consistently among the most-watched food content on TikTok and Reels. The slow fermentation angle gives you a hook that sets you apart from every other pizza place.
Idea 02
"Gosforth, this is your sign to try the N'Duja"
LocalMenu highlightHook

A quick, punchy hero product reel. Pick the most photogenic pizza on the menu (the N'Duja with hot honey, or the Parma Ham Blanco with truffle cream) and film it being made and boxed. Strong local hook on frame one. Tag the location. End with "link in bio to order."

Why it works: Local tags and city mentions get pushed to nearby users by the algorithm. A specific product recommendation feels like a tip from a friend, not an advert.
Idea 03
"We read our Google reviews while making the pizza"
Social proofPersonalityTrust

Overlay your best Google reviews on screen while you film the kitchen in action. Read them out or let the text do the talking. "Best pizza I have ever had as a takeaway" over footage of the dough stretch and the oven. Authentic, warm, and it lets your customers do the selling for you.

Why it works: Social proof is the most persuasive content a food business can post. A 4.9 rating hidden on Google does nothing, but shown on a reel it becomes the reason someone places their first order.
Idea 04
"POV: you ordered from Farina for the first time"
UnboxingExperienceRelatable

Film the full customer experience from the other side: the order notification, the delivery arriving, the box opening, the first bite. The "unboxing" format is huge on TikTok and it lets someone imagine exactly what they would get. Keep it real, not polished.

Why it works: It removes the last barrier for someone who has never ordered from you. They can see exactly what arrives at the door, and when it looks this good, the order follows.
Digital Enhancements
Beyond the Main Feed

A few extra wins that would compound the content work and turn more of your local visibility into orders and regulars.

Get the Google Profile Working Harder

A 4.9 rating is gold, but the profile itself can do more. When someone searches "pizza Gosforth" or "takeaway near me," Google My Business is often the first thing they see. Make sure it is fully optimised, regularly updated with photos, and actively generating fresh reviews.

  • Keep the profile optimised for "pizza Gosforth," "Italian takeaway Newcastle" and nearby areas
  • Add fresh food photos regularly (the same content you are creating for social)
  • Encourage every happy customer to leave a Google review at the point of order
  • Turn your best reviews into shareable social content

UGC and Customer Content

People are already talking about Farina on TikTok. Encourage more of it. A simple "tag us and we will repost" goes a long way, and user-generated content is more trusted than anything you post yourself.

  • Repost customer stories and tags to build community
  • Run a simple monthly feature of the best customer photo or video
  • Include a small card in delivery orders encouraging a tag or review

Targeted Local Ads to Your Delivery Radius

Because you already have tiered delivery pricing by distance, the ad targeting writes itself. A small budget aimed at people within 5 miles of Gosforth, running your best-performing reels as the ads, puts Farina in front of people who can actually order.

  • Target within your delivery radius (0 to 5 miles from Gosforth)
  • Use your strongest food reels as the ads
  • Run them on quieter evenings to fill the gaps in the week
  • Track cost per order so the spend is always accountable

Why it fits Farina: A few extra orders on a quiet Tuesday covers the ad spend many times over, making local ads one of the highest-return moves a takeaway can make.
