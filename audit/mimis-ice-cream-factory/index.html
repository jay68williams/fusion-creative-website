Where The Opportunity Is
Six Growth Opportunities

Mimi's has incredible foundations. The brand, the experience, the location, and the product are all strong. The gap is purely in how many people know you exist. Here is where the biggest opportunities are.

1

19 Posts in 10 Months Is Not Enough

This is the single biggest issue. You have posted 19 times total, with gaps of two to three months between posts. In January you posted about a temporary closure. Your next post was not until April. That is a quarter of the year with zero content. Instagram and TikTok reward consistency. When you disappear for months, the algorithm forgets you exist and your followers stop seeing you even when you do post.

The businesses that win on social media are the ones that show up every single day, or at a minimum several times a week. With the amount of content Mimi's could create, there is absolutely no reason to be posting less than 4-5 times per week. Every ice cream order, every topping combination, every happy kid, every behind-the-scenes moment in the factory is a piece of content waiting to happen.

2

Almost No Video Content Is a Massive Missed Opportunity

Out of 19 posts, only 2 are videos. That means 89% of your content is static images. In 2026, video is everything. Instagram Reels and TikTok are where the reach is. Static posts get shown to a fraction of your followers. A well-made Reel gets shown to thousands of people who have never heard of you. Your best reel hit 1,214 views. Your average static post gets 10-26 likes. The maths speaks for itself.

Ice cream is one of the most visual, satisfying, and shareable food categories on social media. Scooping, drizzling sauces, adding toppings, the first bite, kids' faces lighting up. This content practically films itself. Every single day you are open, you could be creating 3-4 short videos that would each outperform any static image you have ever posted.

3

Your Website Is Not Live

The domain mimisicecreamfactory.co.uk exists but it is not connected to anything. It shows a Wix "domain not connected" error. That means anyone who searches for your business online and clicks on the website link hits a dead end. It also means you are missing out on Google search traffic, local SEO, and the credibility that comes with having a professional online presence.

Your Linktree is doing some of the heavy lifting, but it is not a substitute for a proper website. A website with your menu, party packages, opening hours, location, photos, and a booking button would convert significantly more visitors into customers. It also gives you a place to send people from social media, ads, and Google.

4

TikTok Is Where Ice Cream Goes Viral

You have TikTok linked in your Linktree but the account appears inactive. This is the single biggest growth lever available to Mimi's right now. Ice cream content is one of the most popular and viral categories on TikTok. Satisfying scoop videos, topping reveals, ASMR sundae builds, kids party chaos, mascot appearances. This content gets millions of views consistently.

Every ice cream shop that has gone viral in the last two years has done it through short-form video. The algorithm rewards visually satisfying, watch-to-the-end content, and ice cream ticks every box. One well-shot 15-second video of a sundae being built from scoop to sauce to sprinkles could reach more people in Gosforth, Jesmond, and the wider Newcastle area than six months of static Instagram posts combined.

5

The Mimi Mascot Is Being Massively Underused

Your mascot reveal reel was your best performing post by a mile: 1,214 views and 80 likes versus an average of 15 likes on everything else. That tells you exactly what the audience wants. Mimi the character is your secret weapon. A mascot that interacts with kids, dances to trending audio, surprises customers, and shows up in unexpected places is a proven viral formula.

You already have the mascot. You already have the costume. You just need to put Mimi in front of the camera regularly and let the character do the work.

The potential here is enormous. Ice cream, desserts, waffles, brownies, seasonal specials, flavour experiments, Deliveroo orders being packed, customer reactions, staff favourites, the list of content ideas is literally endless. Ready-Made Content
5 Video Ideas You Could Film Tomorrow

These are formats that are working right now for food and dessert businesses on TikTok and Instagram. All of them play to Mimi's strengths: incredible ice cream, a range of desserts, a fun factory experience, and a brand that people want to be part of. And these are just five. With the amount of products, flavours, and experiences you offer, you could come up with fifty.

Idea 01
"Build your dream sundae at Mimi's Ice Cream Factory"
Satisfying ASMR Viral Format

Close-up, overhead camera. Film the full build from empty cup to finished masterpiece. Two scoops going in, sauce drizzling over the top, sprinkles falling, a wafer being placed. No talking, just the sounds and a trending audio underneath. End with a beauty shot of the finished sundae.

Why it works: Satisfying food build videos are one of the most reliably viral formats on TikTok and Reels. Ice cream is especially effective because of the colours, textures, and the way sauces flow. These videos get watched on repeat, saved, and shared with friends. "We need to go here" in the comments is the goal, and this format delivers it consistently.
Idea 02
"Mimi surprised the whole shop today"
Mascot Reaction Shareable

Film Mimi the mascot walking out from behind the counter or through the front door. Capture the kids' faces as they see her for the first time. The excitement, the hugs, the dancing. Set it to a trending audio. Keep it under 15 seconds for maximum replay.

Why it works: Your mascot reveal reel already proved this works. It was your best performing post by a huge margin. Mascot content consistently outperforms on TikTok because it is fun, unexpected, and makes people tag friends and family. "We need to take the kids here" is exactly the reaction you want, and Mimi makes that happen naturally.
Idea 03
"POV: It is your birthday and your mum booked Mimi's"
POV Party Content Emotional

First-person style footage of a birthday party at Mimi's. Walking in to see the decorated booth, the ice cream being served, Mimi appearing for the sing-along, the birthday child's reaction. Mix of clips from a real party, set to a nostalgic or feel-good audio.

Why it works: This sells your party packages without ever feeling like an advert. Parents watching this will immediately picture their own child having the same experience. It drives direct bookings because the viewer can see exactly what they are paying for. Every party you host is a potential version of this video.
Idea 04
"NEW FLAVOUR DROP: you are not ready for this one"
Hype Product Launch Urgency

Quick cuts: the new flavour being scooped, a close-up of the colour and texture, someone tasting it for the first time and reacting, the flavour name revealed at the end. Keep it fast, punchy, and under 10 seconds. Post it the morning the flavour launches with a "limited time" hook.

Why it works: Scarcity and exclusivity drive urgency. "Limited edition" and "new drop" language triggers FOMO. People share it with friends and make plans to visit before it runs out. It also gives you a reason to post regularly because you always have new flavours to announce. This format works every single time.
Idea 05
"Rating every ice cream flavour at Mimi's (honest review)"
Review Taste Test Series

Someone (ideally Daniel or a team member with personality) tries each flavour and gives an honest, quick rating out of 10. Fast cuts between flavours. Genuine reactions. The controversial low rating on one flavour makes people comment to disagree. End with "but which one do YOU think is the best?" to drive comments.

Why it works: Rating and review content drives massive engagement because people love to agree, disagree, and share their own opinion. Asking a question at the end triggers comments, which tells the algorithm to push the video to more people. This can become a recurring series every time new flavours arrive, giving you consistent content that always performs.
Bonus Ideas
Even More Viral Potential

These are the kind of ideas that could genuinely blow up for Mimi's. Each one has massive viral potential and plays perfectly into what makes your brand unique.

Bonus 01
"Blind taste test: can they guess the flavour?"
Challenge Interactive Series

Blindfold a team member, a customer, or a friend. Hand them a spoon with a mystery flavour. Film their genuine reaction as they try to guess what it is. The faces, the wrong guesses, the moment they finally get it right. Quick cuts, trending audio, keep it under 30 seconds.

Why it works: Blind taste test content is one of the most reliably viral formats on TikTok. It is interactive, entertaining, and makes the viewer want to play along. People comment their own guesses, tag friends, and say "we need to try this." It can become a recurring series because you always have new flavours to test. Every single video sells your product without ever feeling like an advert.
Bonus 02
"Mimi hits the streets of Gosforth with free ice cream"
Street Content Reactions Viral

Put someone in the Mimi costume and walk through Gosforth High Street, Great Park, or the Quayside handing out ice cream samples. Film people's reactions as a giant ice cream mascot appears out of nowhere offering them free scoops. Get them to taste it on camera and rate it out of 10. The surprise, the laughter, the genuine reviews.

Why it works: Street reaction content with a mascot is a guaranteed attention-grabber. People stopping to interact with Mimi, tasting your ice cream, and giving real-time reactions is pure entertainment. It puts your product in front of new audiences, builds brand awareness across Newcastle, and creates shareable moments that reach far beyond your existing followers. This is the kind of video that gets 100K+ views because it is funny, unexpected, and makes people want to find out where the ice cream is from.
Bonus 03
"We asked strangers to rate our ice cream out of 10"
Public Rating Social Proof Engaging

Set up outside the shop or at a local event. Hand people different flavours and ask them to rate each one on camera. Film the genuine reactions: the big smiles, the surprised faces, the debates between friends about which flavour is best. Compile the best reactions into a fast-paced montage.

Why it works: Real people giving real ratings of your product is the most powerful form of social proof on social media. Viewers trust strangers' opinions more than any advert. The format drives massive comment engagement because everyone wants to weigh in with their own opinion. "That one is definitely a 10" or "they are wrong, the chocolate is better" fills the comments and tells the algorithm to push the video further. It also shows that Mimi's is for everyone, not just one audience.
