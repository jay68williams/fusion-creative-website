A few ideas I would love to try

Make the science visual, not a caption.The gut-skin axis is fascinating, but it is buried in paragraphs. Turn it into a fifteen-second hook-first reel: "Why your skincare is not working", then the science in motion. Same depth, built to be watched.
Lead with "wait, you drink it?"A probiotic for clearer skin is a genuine pattern-interrupt. That curiosity gap is your single best hook, and right now it is barely used. People will stop just to find out how it works.
Real customer journeys and before-and-afters.You have VOGUE, Superdrug reviews and real results. The honest eight-to-twelve-week story, filmed as a customer diary, is the most trusted content in skincare and it sells.
Put the dermatologist on camera.An expert face like Dr Em Wedgeworth myth-busting bad skincare advice, green-screen reacting to viral skin tips, is pure authority content, and authority travels in this category.
Trend-led and myth-busting.Skincare TikTok is enormous. "Things people get wrong about acne", reacting to bad advice, fast and current. The fastest, cheapest way to land in front of people who have never heard of you.
Turn the reach into subscriptions.This is the bit most agencies miss. We would feed the best content into ads, retargeting and email flows, so the views become subscribers, not just likes. Reach is only worth it if it converts.
