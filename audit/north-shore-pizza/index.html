Where The Opportunity Is
Building the Right Foundations

Being early-stage is not a disadvantage. It is an opportunity. You get to build everything the right way from the start, rather than having to fix mistakes later. Here are the foundations that will make the biggest difference.

1

A Website Gives You a Home Base

Right now, there is no website for North Shore Pizza Co. A website is your home base. It is where people go to find your menu, your location, your hours, and how to order. It is also what appears when someone Googles your name. Without a website, you are invisible to search engines and relying entirely on social media for discoverability.

A simple, clean, mobile-first website with your menu, location, contact details, and ordering information is all you need to start. It does not need to be complex. It needs to be professional, fast, and easy to find.

2

TikTok Is the Most Powerful Free Marketing Tool for Pizza

Pizza content is some of the most viral food content on the internet. The dough being stretched, the sauce being spread, toppings being placed, the oven, the cheese pull. These moments generate millions of views daily across TikTok. And the best part is that TikTok does not care how many followers you have. A brand-new account can have a video reach 100,000 people if the content is good.

Setting up a TikTok account and posting consistently from day one would give North Shore Pizza Co a significant head start over competitors who are not on the platform. The earlier you start building the audience, the stronger your position becomes.

3

Review Profiles Build Trust Before People Visit

Google Business, TripAdvisor, and Facebook are where people go to check reviews before visiting or ordering from a food business. Without these profiles, potential customers who hear about you have no way to verify that you are legitimate and that other people have had a good experience.

Setting up these profiles early means that as customers start visiting, their reviews start building a public track record. Even five positive reviews on Google makes a meaningful difference in building trust with new customers. The sooner the profiles are live, the sooner the reviews start accumulating.

Ready-Made Content
5 Video Ideas You Could Film Today

These are formats that are performing right now for pizza businesses on TikTok and Instagram. Pizza content is universally engaging, and all of these ideas can be filmed with a phone and minimal equipment.

Idea 01
"Watch us build a pizza from scratch"
Process Behind the Scenes Satisfying

Start with the dough ball. Stretch it. Spread the sauce in a spiral. Place the toppings one by one. Slide it into the oven. Wait. Pull it out. Cut it. Show the cheese pull. The whole journey in 30 seconds. Fast cuts, overhead angle, trending audio.

Why it works: Pizza assembly content is one of the most watched food formats on TikTok. The process is inherently satisfying, and people watch it on repeat. It positions you as a craft pizza maker, not a generic takeaway. This is the kind of content that builds a following because people come back to watch it again and again.
Idea 02
"The cheese pull that made me start a pizza business"
Cheese Pull Viral Format Story

Close-up of a perfect cheese pull. Slow motion. The stretch, the strands, the snap. Use a hook that tells a micro-story about why you started North Shore Pizza Co. Keep it authentic and personal. End with the pizza on the plate, ready to eat.

Why it works: The cheese pull is the single most engaging pizza moment on social media. It stops scrolling every time. Pairing it with a personal story about why you started the business creates an emotional connection that turns viewers into fans. People do not just follow for the food. They follow for the person behind it.
Idea 03
"First look at North Shore Pizza Co"
Introduction Brand Launch Hype

A brand introduction video. Who you are, what you make, where people can find you. Quick cuts of the pizza being made, the setup, the finished product. Keep it short, punchy, and confident. "North Shore Pizza Co. We are here."

Why it works: Introduction videos are essential for new brands. They give people a reason to follow, set the tone for the brand, and establish what you are about from day one. Pin it to the top of your Instagram and TikTok profiles so every new visitor immediately understands who you are and what you do.
Idea 04
"POV: Making 50 pizzas before service"
Behind the Scenes Hustle Prep

Time-lapse of prep. Dough being portioned, sauce being made, toppings being prepped, the workspace being set up. Show the work that goes into being ready for service. Fast-paced, energetic, with music that matches the hustle. End with "Ready. Let us go."

Why it works: Prep content humanises the brand and shows the effort behind the product. People respect the hustle, and it makes them appreciate the food more when they see how much work goes into it. This format also performs well because the time-lapse is inherently satisfying to watch.
Idea 05
"Our most popular pizza, step by step"
Signature Dish Menu Hero Recurring

Pick your best-selling or signature pizza. Show every step of making it. Name each ingredient as it goes on. Show it going into the oven and coming out. Cut it. Close-up of the first bite. Make this a recurring series: "Our most popular pizza" one week, "Our newest pizza" the next. It becomes a content calendar built around your menu.

Why it works: Signature dish content gives people a specific reason to visit. It creates a "I need to try that" reaction. Making it a recurring series means you always have content ideas, and followers start anticipating the next one. It also naturally showcases the variety of your menu over time.
Digital Enhancements
Building the Digital Foundations

These are the essential digital foundations that every food business needs. Getting them right from the start saves time and money later.

Build a Simple Website

A clean, one-page website with your menu, location, hours, contact details, and ordering information. This is your digital home base. It makes you findable on Google, gives customers a place to check your menu before visiting, and looks professional.

  • Mobile-first design, as most searches happen on phones
  • Menu with photos of your best pizzas
  • Clear location and contact information
  • Link to order online if applicable

Quick win: Even a simple Linktree or single-page site with your menu and contact details is better than nothing. Get something live while you build the full site.

Set Up Google Business

Google Business is free and is the single most important local discoverability tool. When someone searches "pizza near me," Google Business is what determines whether you show up. Set it up with accurate hours, location, photos, and menu information. Encourage every happy customer to leave a Google review.

Launch on TikTok and Facebook

Instagram is a great start. Adding TikTok and Facebook expands your reach significantly. TikTok for viral discovery, Facebook for local community groups and events. Being present on all three platforms means more people find you, wherever they prefer to spend their time online.
