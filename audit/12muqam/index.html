Where The Opportunity Is
Five Things We Would Focus On

You have the food, the cuisine and the location. The job is to capture the reach yourself: film the food the way the creators do, post it consistently on your own page, and make 12Muqam the Newcastle spot people already know about before they walk down Stowell Street. These are the five areas we would focus on.

1

Start Posting Your Own Reels

This is the single biggest one. You have zero reels of your own, yet a reel about your food pulled 42,000 views. Reels are how Instagram pushes content to people who do not already follow you, which is exactly the reach you are missing. Static photos almost never leave your existing followers. Video does. Switching the focus from photos to reels is the change that unlocks everything else.

The good news is you do not need a studio. The best food reels are shot on a phone, close up, with the sizzle and the steam doing the selling.

2

Film the Food the Way It Deserves

Your dishes are made for video. The big plate chicken reveal, hand-pulled noodles being stretched, lamb skewers over flame, the noodle pull, the salt tea poured from height. These are exactly the close-up, satisfying shots that rack up views and rewatches. Sound up, tight on the food, a strong first frame, no script needed.

Right now this content is being captured by visiting creators rather than by you. Filming it yourself means you own the footage, the reach and the audience it brings.

3

Lean Hard Into "You Have Never Tried This"

Uyghur cuisine is your superpower. Most people in Newcastle have never heard of it, let alone tasted it, and that curiosity is your best hook. Content built around discovery, what is this, where is it from, why is it different, performs far better than a generic "great food here" post. Your captions currently read a bit like an encyclopedia. We would turn that knowledge into curiosity-driven hooks that make people want to come and try it.

Novelty is rare and valuable. You have it built in. Most restaurants have to manufacture a hook, you already have one on the plate.

4

Turn Creator Visits Into a Repeatable Engine

The influencer posts are working, so let's make them deliberate rather than occasional. A steady rhythm of the right Newcastle food creators, paired with your own content capturing the same dishes, compounds fast. Their reach plus your consistent posting is how a new restaurant becomes the talked-about spot in the city.

We know the Newcastle food creator scene and can line up the right collaborations alongside the content we film for you, so it all points back to your page and your bookings.

5

Local Ads and Google to Catch Ready Diners

Once the content is working, a small, tightly targeted ad budget puts your best food reel in front of hungry people in Newcastle deciding where to eat tonight. On top of that, a tidy Google Business Profile catches everyone searching "halal restaurant Newcastle" or "Chinatown food" at the exact moment they want a table.

Content builds the audience and the buzz, ads and search put you in front of people ready to book a table right now. Together they keep the dining room full.

Ready-Made Content
4 Video Ideas You Could Film This Week

These are formats working right now on Instagram Reels and TikTok, built around dishes you already serve. Each one can be filmed on a phone in the restaurant, no extra kit, lead with a strong first frame and let the food do the talking.

Idea 01
"POV: you have never tried Uyghur food"
DiscoveryNoveltyHook-first

Open on the big plate chicken or a steaming bowl of noodles, with a bold text hook on the first frame. Quick cuts through two or three signature dishes, sound up, under 20 seconds. The whole point is "I bet you have never seen this".

Why it works: curiosity is the strongest scroll-stopper there is, and Uyghur cuisine is genuinely new to most people. This is your number one format and your best shot at a breakout reel.
Idea 02
The hand-pulled noodle, up close
ASMRSatisfyingProcess

Tight, slow shots of the noodles being pulled and stretched, the dough working, the steam, the final plated bowl. No talking, sound up, a "wait for it" hook on screen. End on the finished dish.

Why it works: watching skilled food prep is some of the most rewatched content on the internet. The noodle pull is hypnotic, and almost nobody in Newcastle is showing this.
Idea 03
The spicy noodle challenge
ChallengeReactionProven

Your red chilli lehman is already a talking point, and a creator's spicy noodle reel about you did well. Make it your own running format: real people taking on the heat, the reactions, the "can you finish it" hook. Invite locals to come and try.

Why it works: challenge and reaction content is endlessly shareable, drives comments, and turns one dish into a reason to visit. You already have proof this angle lands.
Idea 04
"The tea that confuses everyone"
CuriosityTalking pointLocal

Your salted milk tea surprises everyone who tries it, which makes it perfect content. Film a first-timer's honest reaction, or a quick "salty tea, would you try it" to camera, with the pour shot front and centre. Tag Newcastle and Chinatown.

Why it works: an unexpected, slightly polarising item gets people commenting and tagging friends, which pushes the reel further. It is memorable and unmistakably yours.
