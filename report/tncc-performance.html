Reading the numbers

Our figures vs Facebook

You'll notice our numbers are higher than what Facebook Ads Manager shows. That's expected — here's why, and why we're confident in the real picture.

What Facebook reports
£1,734
Tracked sales59
Reported ROAS4.5×
What actually happened (Shopify)
£3,154
New customers100
True new-customer return8.2×

Facebook only counts a sale when it can directly tie it back to an ad click inside its own attribution window. Since Apple's iOS privacy changes, cookie restrictions and people browsing on one device but buying on another, a large slice of genuinely ad-driven sales never gets matched back to the ad. So Facebook under-counts — it's showing 59 sales / £1,734, while Shopify recorded 100 brand-new customers / £3,154 in the exact same window.

The honest answer sits between the two: Facebook misses a lot, and not every new customer is purely from ads. But by combining Facebook's data with Shopify's actual new-customer orders, we can estimate the real contribution very accurately — and on every measure, conservative or generous, the campaign is comfortably profitable.
