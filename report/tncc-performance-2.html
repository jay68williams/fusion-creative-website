Reading the numbers

Our figures vs Facebook

Same health-check as last time. Facebook's own tracking now shows an 11× return, up from 4.5× in the first brief, and Shopify's real new-customer picture is stronger still.

As we covered in the first brief, Facebook only counts sales it can tie directly back to an ad click, so it always under-counts. What matters is that both measures moved sharply in the same direction: Facebook's own reported return went from 4.5× to 11×, and the true new-customer return went from 8.2× to 12.9×. However you cut it, every pound in is coming back many times over.

Next moves

What we're doing next

01
Keep stock ahead of the ads
The room sprays sold 1,000+ bottles and ran dry mid-window. The restock lands this week and the back-in-stock email goes to the full list Friday morning, then we point spend straight back at them.
02
Scale toward £100/day
At £2.20 to win a customer and 12.9× on new business there's clear headroom. We'll step the winning campaign up gradually so performance holds as spend rises.
03
Fresh creative behind the winners
New ads are already built for the wax melt refill bags, and the new sizzler range just went live on site. Both slot into the proven campaign structure.
04
Rotate out the laggards
LED diffuser and Hinch box creatives are profitable but trailing. We'll replace them rather than let them soak budget the winners could use.
