Where The Opportunity Is
Five Growth Opportunities

NOVOCASTRIAN has world-class product and world-class branding. The gap is between the quality of what you make and the number of people who get to see it being made. These are the five biggest opportunities we identified.

1

The Workshop Is a Content Studio You Are Not Using

Your workshop in Washington is one of the most compelling content environments we have seen. Raw steel being shaped by hand. Metalwork being ground, polished, and finished. Engineers and craftsmen working with a blend of traditional techniques and modern innovation. This is the kind of content that luxury audiences cannot get enough of.

Right now, none of this is being captured on video. Your Instagram is product photography, which is beautiful, but it is only half the story. The other half, the making, the process, the hands, the sparks, the workshop floor, is what builds emotional connection and justifies premium pricing. When a potential client sees a dining table being hand-built from raw material, they understand viscerally why it costs what it does.

Short-form video of the making process is the single biggest unlock for NOVOCASTRIAN's digital presence. It is the difference between "nice table" and "I need to own that."

2

No One Is Telling the Human Story

"Designers, craftsmen, metalworkers, artists, makers, and engineers." "Humble northern souls who are quietly confident in their pursuit of excellence." These are genuinely brilliant lines. But there is no face behind them. No founder story. No team profiles. No one to connect with.

In the luxury market, people buy from people. The most successful luxury makers on social media, whether it is a watchmaker in Switzerland or a ceramicist in Japan, put human faces on their craft. The viewer needs to see the person whose hands shaped the metal. They need to feel the quiet pride. They need to see the concentration, the skill, the moment a piece comes together.

This is not about becoming influencers. It is about giving the brand a heartbeat that photography alone cannot provide.

3

The Blog Exists but Has No Content

The /blogs/journal page is live on your site but completely empty. This is a significant missed opportunity for two reasons. First, it is an SEO gap. People searching for "bespoke dining table UK", "handcrafted lighting North East", or "custom metalwork furniture" are not finding NOVOCASTRIAN through organic search because there is no content for Google to index.

Second, journal content builds authority and trust. A piece about the inspiration behind The Hagstone Collection, or the metallurgical process behind a specific finish, or the story of a commission for a notable client, these are the kinds of articles that architects, interior designers, and high-net-worth buyers read and share. They position NOVOCASTRIAN as thought leaders in the craft, not just product sellers.

4

Pinterest and LinkedIn Are Massively Underutilised

For a luxury interiors brand, Pinterest should be a primary traffic and discovery channel, not an afterthought. Interior designers, architects, and homeowners planning high-end renovations live on Pinterest. Rich pins linked to your product catalogue, mood boards, and installation photography could drive significant qualified traffic to the site.

LinkedIn is where your B2B audience lives. Architects, commercial specifiers, hospitality designers, and interior design firms are all on LinkedIn. Thought leadership content, project case studies, and behind-the-scenes workshop content positioned for a professional audience could open up an entirely new pipeline of trade enquiries and commercial commissions.

5

Paid Ads Are Running but Could Work Much Harder

We can see from the UTM parameters on your current campaigns that you are running Instagram ads. This is a good start. But without video creative, proper audience segmentation, and creative testing, you are likely leaving a lot of performance on the table.

Luxury furniture ads that show the making process, the raw materials, the finished piece in a stunning interior, these consistently outperform static product shots in ad performance. A well-structured ad funnel targeting interior designers, architects, and affluent homeowners, with video creative that tells the NOVOCASTRIAN story, would deliver a significantly stronger return on your current ad spend.

Ready-Made Content
5 Video Concepts for NOVOCASTRIAN

These are formats that perform exceptionally well in the luxury craft and interiors space. Each one is designed to showcase the making process, the materials, and the human story behind the brand.

Concept 01
"From raw steel to dining table. Made by hand in the North East of England."
Process Time-lapse Hero Content

Follow a single piece from raw material to finished product. Open on a sheet of steel or a rough casting. Show the cutting, shaping, welding, grinding, and finishing. The sparks, the precision, the patience. End with the completed dining table in a beautiful interior. 30-60 seconds of pure craftsmanship.

Why it works: "How it is made" content is one of the highest-performing formats on Instagram and TikTok. In the luxury space, it does double duty: it builds emotional connection and it justifies the price point. When someone sees the hours of skilled handwork that go into a single table, the value becomes self-evident.
Concept 02
"The hands behind every piece."
Portrait Storytelling Brand Building

Close-up on the hands of the craftsmen at work. No face initially, just skilled hands shaping metal, polishing surfaces, checking joints. Then a slow reveal of the person. A brief moment of them looking at camera, maybe a quiet smile. Text overlay: "Designers, craftsmen, metalworkers, artists, makers, and engineers." Minimal, powerful, human.

Why it works: Luxury buyers connect with the maker, not just the product. This format humanises the brand without compromising the premium feel. It takes the brilliant copy you already have and brings it to life visually. The restraint of the format matches the restraint of the brand perfectly.
Concept 03
"Sound on. This is how a NOVOCASTRIAN mirror is made."
ASMR Sensory Immersive

Pure workshop audio. The ring of metal being shaped. The hiss of a weld. The quiet scrape of a hand finishing a surface. No music, no voiceover. Just the authentic sounds of the workshop. Close-up visuals of each process. End with silence and the finished Egg Mirror hanging on a wall.

Why it works: ASMR and sensory content consistently outperforms on social media because it creates an immersive experience. For a metalwork brand, the natural sounds of the workshop are incredibly distinctive and satisfying. It positions NOVOCASTRIAN as a maker, not a retailer. Viewers save and rewatch this type of content.
Concept 04
"Same piece. Six different finishes."
Product Educational Carousel/Reel

Showcase the range of finishes available on a single piece. Brushed brass, blackened steel, polished copper, raw iron. Each finish caught in beautiful light. Quick cuts between them or a slow pan across samples. Highlight the bespoke nature of the brand: "Every piece is personalised to your preference."

Why it works: This format educates while it sells. It shows the depth of the offering, positions the brand as truly bespoke, and gives potential clients a reason to reach out to discuss their specific requirements. It also performs well as a carousel post and a Reel, giving you two pieces of content from one concept.
Concept 05
"Delivery day. Washington to [destination]."
Lifestyle Reveal Social Proof

Follow a finished piece being carefully wrapped, crated, and leaving the workshop. Cut to it arriving at a stunning home or commercial space. The unwrapping. The positioning. The moment it sits in its new environment for the first time. The workshop in Washington to a penthouse in London or a hotel in New York. That journey is the story.

Why it works: This format connects the craft to the lifestyle. It shows that NOVOCASTRIAN pieces end up in extraordinary spaces, which elevates the brand. It also reinforces the worldwide shipping capability and gives potential clients a vision of how the piece would look in their own space. The contrast between a northern workshop and a global destination is compelling storytelling.
