Social Media Management Service Agreement

Fusion Creative and The Bake One

Parties

1. About this Agreement

1.1 This Agreement sets out the terms on which Fusion Creative will provide social media content production and management services to The Bake One.

1.2 We have written this in plain English so it is clear and fair to both sides. Defined terms and figures in this Agreement are binding.

1.3 This Agreement starts on the date it is signed by both parties (the "Start Date").

1.4 In this Agreement, a "Working Day" means Monday to Friday, excluding public holidays in England. "Billing Month" means each monthly billing period running from the Start Date (or the date the relevant plan begins) and on the same date each month thereafter.

2. Services and Deliverables

2.1 The Client is signing up to the Starter plan. Each Billing Month, the Provider will deliver the following:

  • 10 short-form videos per month - ten short-form social videos, produced in vertical format, edited with captions, and to a standard reasonably agreed in the brief.
  • 1 content shoot per month - one content shoot, carried out on-site at the restaurant. The ten monthly videos are produced from the footage captured at that shoot.
  • Posting across Instagram and TikTok - the agreed content is published to the Client's Instagram account and the new TikTok account. The Provider decides the posting schedule and split across the agreed channels at its reasonable discretion, in line with what works best for the content.
  • New TikTok account - the Provider will set up a brand new TikTok account for the Client and manage and post to it as part of the service.
  • Google Business Profile management - the Provider keeps the Client's Google Business Profile up to date, including adding photos and posts, so it stays fresh, accurate and active.
  • Live performance dashboard - the Client receives access to a live performance dashboard they can log in to at any time.
  • Weekly performance reporting - the Provider provides weekly performance reporting.

2.2 The shoot date each month will be agreed in advance between the parties at a mutually convenient time.

2.3 Each video includes one round of reasonable amendments. Further rounds, or changes that require re-shooting or substantial re-editing, may be quoted and charged separately.

2.4 The Provider will use reasonable skill and care in producing and managing the content. The Provider does not guarantee specific results such as follower growth, views, reach or sales, as these depend on factors outside our control (including platform algorithms and audience behaviour).

2.5 Anything not expressly listed in this section is outside the scope of the Starter plan and may be quoted separately.

2.6 Ad spend and third-party costs. The fees in this Agreement cover the Provider's services only. Any paid media or advertising spend, boosting, paid platform subscriptions, premium tools, and music or stock licences beyond standard in-platform use are not included. Where the Client asks the Provider to arrange these, they are billed at cost or paid directly by the Client, and are agreed in advance.

3. Approvals Workflow

3.1 The Provider will share content for the Client's review ahead of posting, via the agreed channel (for example the dashboard, a shared folder or messaging).

3.2 The Client will review and provide approval or reasonable amendment requests promptly, and in any event within 2 Working Days of content being shared.

3.3 If the Client does not respond within that time, the Provider may proceed to publish the content as shared, so that the monthly schedule is not delayed. Minor edits requested after this point will be accommodated where reasonably possible, subject to the one round of amendments in clause 2.3.

3.4 The Client is responsible for the accuracy of any factual information it supplies (for example prices, offers, opening hours, allergen and dietary claims).

4. Client Responsibilities

4.1 To allow us to deliver the service, the Client agrees to:

  • Premises access - provide safe and reasonable access to the restaurant premises for the monthly content shoot.
  • Account access - give the Provider the necessary access and permissions to the relevant social media accounts, including the new TikTok account, so the Provider can post and manage content.
  • Onboarding - complete the Provider's onboarding form and supply any reasonable information or materials requested to set the service up.
  • Timely approvals - provide approvals, feedback and any required content or information in good time, in line with section 3.
  • Cooperation - designate a main point of contact (for example the Manager) and respond to reasonable requests without undue delay.

4.2 If the Client's delay or failure to meet these responsibilities prevents the Provider from delivering part of the service in a given Billing Month (for example by not making the premises available for the shoot, or by revoking account access), the Provider is not in breach and the monthly fee remains payable. Videos not produced because of a Client-caused delay are not carried forward to a later month.

4.3 If a scheduled shoot or delivery is delayed because of the Provider's fault, the affected videos will be carried forward and delivered as soon as reasonably practicable at no extra cost.

5. Fees

5.1 The fee for the Starter plan is GBP 600 per month.

5.2 Fees are billed monthly in advance.

5.3 The first month's fee is due on signing this Agreement and before the first content shoot takes place.

5.4 Payment is made by bank transfer or standing order to the account details provided by the Provider on the invoice.

5.5 Each monthly invoice is payable within 7 days of the invoice date, except the first month's fee, which is due as set out in clause 5.3.

5.6 The fees are exclusive of VAT. If the Provider becomes VAT registered, VAT will be added where applicable and shown on the invoice.

6. Late Payment

6.1 If any payment is not received by its due date, the Provider may:

  • send a reminder and allow a short grace period of 5 Working Days; and
  • if payment is still not received, pause the service (including posting, shoots and reporting) until the account is brought up to date.

6.2 The Provider may charge interest and reasonable recovery costs on overdue amounts at the statutory rate under the Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998 (currently 8% per year above the Bank of England base rate, plus the fixed compensation set by that Act).

6.3 Pausing the service for non-payment does not reduce or waive any fees already due, and does not extend the minimum term.

7. Optional Upgrade

7.1 The Client may upgrade at any time during this Agreement. This is an option the Client can choose to take at any stage, and is not automatic.

7.2 The upgrade increases the volume of content to at least 20 and up to 25 videos per month (the upper figure depending on the footage captured at the shoot) at GBP 1,300 per month.

7.3 To upgrade, the Client confirms in writing (email is sufficient). The new monthly fee of GBP 1,300 applies from the start of the next Billing Month after the upgrade is agreed, unless the parties agree otherwise.

7.4 All other terms of this Agreement continue to apply to the upgraded plan. The Client may move back down to the Starter plan by giving written notice, effective from the start of the next Billing Month.

7.5 Upgrading or downgrading does not restart the minimum term in section 8. The original term continues to run, and there is no separate minimum commitment on the upgraded tier.

8. Minimum Term and Rolling Continuation

8.1 This Agreement has a minimum term of 3 months from the Start Date.

8.2 After the minimum term, the Agreement continues on a rolling month-to-month basis until cancelled in line with section 9.

8.3 During the minimum term, the monthly fee remains payable for each of the first three months, even if the Client chooses not to use part of the service in a given month.

9. Cancellation, Notice and Pausing

9.1 The Client may give notice during the minimum term to expire at the end of the minimum term, so long as at least 30 days' written notice is given. Otherwise, after the minimum term, either party may cancel this Agreement at any time by giving 30 days' written notice (email is sufficient).

9.2 Termination for breach or insolvency. Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately by written notice if the other party materially breaches it and does not put the breach right within 14 days of being asked to in writing, or if the other party becomes insolvent, enters into any arrangement with its creditors, or ceases trading.

9.3 On cancellation, provided the Client's account is paid up to date, the Provider will complete any deliverables already paid for in the current Billing Month and will provide a reasonable handover of the social accounts and access back to the Client.

9.4 Pausing: by agreement in writing, the parties may pause the service for a short period (for example over a holiday closure). A pause does not count towards the minimum term, and the service resumes on the agreed date.

9.5 Fees already paid in advance for a month that has begun are non-refundable, save where the Provider has failed to deliver the service due to its own fault.

9.6 Dashboard on termination. Access to the performance dashboard ends on termination. The Client may request a final export of its performance data within 14 days of termination, and the Provider will provide it in a reasonable format.

10. Content and Usage Rights

10.1 The Provider produces the content under this Agreement.

10.2 The Client is granted a full, ongoing licence to use the content produced under this Agreement for its own business and marketing purposes (including on its own social channels, website and advertising), including after this Agreement ends.

10.3 The Provider may also use the content, and name and tag the Client, in the Provider's own portfolio, website, social media and marketing materials as an example of its work, unless the Client objects in writing, in which case the Provider will stop using new material for that purpose going forward and, on the Client's reasonable request, will remove existing portfolio material naming the Client where it is practical to do so.

10.4 The Client confirms that any materials it provides (such as logos, images, menus or branding) may be used by the Provider for the purpose of delivering the service, and that the Client has the right to allow this.

10.5 Where music, stock assets or third-party materials are used, they are licensed for use on the relevant social platforms in the ordinary way; any specialised licensing the Client requires for other uses is the Client's responsibility unless otherwise agreed.

10.6 Client indemnity. The Client is responsible for the materials and factual information it supplies or approves (including logos, images, menus, prices, offers, and allergen and dietary claims). The Client will cover the Provider for any reasonable losses, claims or costs arising from those materials or approved facts being inaccurate, or from them infringing someone else's rights or breaching advertising, consumer or food-information law.

11. Social Media Accounts

11.1 The new TikTok account set up by the Provider is created for and on behalf of the Client, and ownership of that account belongs to the Client. On cancellation, the Provider will hand over the login and administrative access to the Client.

11.2 The Client's existing accounts (including Instagram and the Google Business Profile) remain owned by the Client at all times. Access granted to the Provider is for the purpose of delivering the service and may be revoked by the Client on cancellation or at any time (subject to the Client's payment and other obligations under sections 4 and 8 continuing), recognising that revoking access may limit the Provider's ability to deliver.

12. Platform and Advertising Compliance

12.1 Both parties will comply with the terms of the relevant social media platforms (including Instagram, TikTok and Google) and with applicable advertising and consumer law, including the UK Advertising Codes (CAP Code) and the proper disclosure of paid or advertising content.

13. Data Protection

13.1 Both parties will comply with UK data protection law, including the UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018.

13.2 Where the Provider accesses or handles personal data on the Client's behalf (for example through the social accounts or performance data), the Provider acts as a processor and the Client as controller. The Provider will process that personal data only on the Client's reasonable instructions and for the purpose of delivering the service, and will keep it secure.

14. Confidentiality

14.1 Each party may receive confidential information about the other (for example business plans, performance figures, pricing or account access). Each party agrees to keep such information confidential and to use it only for the purpose of this Agreement.

14.2 This does not apply to information that is already public, becomes public through no fault of the receiving party, or must be disclosed by law.

14.3 This clause continues after the Agreement ends.

15. Limitation of Liability

15.1 Nothing in this Agreement limits liability for death or personal injury caused by negligence, for fraud, or for anything else that cannot be limited by law.

15.2 The Provider is not liable for indirect or consequential losses, or for loss of profit, revenue, goodwill or anticipated savings.

15.3 Subject to clause 15.1, the Provider's total liability arising out of or in connection with this Agreement is limited to the total fees paid by the Client to the Provider in the 3 months immediately before the event giving rise to the claim.

15.4 The Provider is not responsible for the acts, policies, outages or decisions of third-party platforms (such as Instagram, TikTok or Google), including account suspensions or changes to their algorithms or terms.

16. Non-Solicitation of Staff

16.1 During this Agreement and for 6 months after it ends, neither party will directly solicit or hire the other party's staff or contractors who were involved in the services and with whom that party had material contact, without the other party's written consent. This does not prevent either party from hiring someone who responds to a general public job advertisement not directed at them.

17. Force Majeure

17.1 Neither party is liable for delays or failures caused by events beyond its reasonable control (for example serious illness, fire, flood, power or internet outages, platform failures, strikes, or government restrictions).

17.2 If such an event prevents a monthly shoot or delivery, the parties will agree in good faith to reschedule the affected work to a reasonable later date.

18. General

18.1 Independent contractor. The Provider is an independent contractor. Nothing in this Agreement creates a partnership, employment or agency relationship beyond the authority needed to manage the agreed social accounts.

18.2 Whole agreement. This Agreement is the whole agreement between the parties on its subject matter and replaces any earlier discussions or proposals.

18.3 Changes. Any change to this Agreement must be agreed in writing by both parties (email is sufficient).

18.4 Assignment. Neither party may transfer this Agreement to someone else without the other's written consent, which will not be unreasonably withheld.

18.5 No waiver. If a party does not enforce a term, or delays in doing so, that does not waive that term or any other term, and it can still be enforced later.

18.6 Severability. If any part of this Agreement is found to be invalid or unenforceable, the rest of the Agreement continues in full force.

18.7 Notices. Notices under this Agreement may be given by email to the contacts stated in this Agreement.

18.8 Counterparts and electronic signature. This Agreement may be signed in any number of counterparts, and may be signed and exchanged electronically. Each signed copy is an original, and together they form one agreement.

18.9 Governing law. This Agreement is governed by the laws of England and Wales, and the courts of England and Wales have exclusive jurisdiction.
